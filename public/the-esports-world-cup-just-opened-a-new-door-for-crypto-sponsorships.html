The Esports World Cup Just Opened a New Door for Crypto Sponsorships

France's new regulations mean licensed blockchain companies can now officially back esports teams at the biggest event of the year. What does this mean for competitive gaming's future?

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 03, 2026 · 18:45 UTC 3 MIN

The Esports World Cup 2026 has officially kicked off in Paris, transforming the French capital into the epicenter of competitive gaming for seven weeks. From July 6 to August 23, the event is hosting 25 tournaments spanning 24 different titles, all vying for a massive combined prize pool of $75 million, with an additional $30 million dedicated to the Club Championship. The opening ceremony at La Seine Musicale on July 8 set the stage for this esports marathon.

But beyond the colossal prize money and expansive game roster, a significant shift is taking place that could redefine the financial landscape of esports. France has enacted new regulations that now permit licensed crypto and blockchain companies to sponsor esports teams competing at the EWC 2026. This isn't just a minor tweak; it's being hailed as a "landmark moment" for both the competitive gaming scene and the broader adoption of digital assets.

This regulatory green light opens a new avenue for funding, potentially injecting fresh capital into teams and players within the esports ecosystem. For years, the integration of cryptocurrency into mainstream sports and entertainment has been a topic of debate, but this move by France offers a clear path forward for regulated entities to engage with competitive gaming on a grand scale.

The implications of these new sponsorships are poised to be a major storyline throughout the tournament.
