EWC 2026 Moves to Paris, Gives Players a New Voice

The Esports World Cup is headed to France in 2026 with a record prize pool and a new award decided exclusively by pros.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 19, 2026 · 16:45 UTC 3 MIN

The Esports World Cup (EWC) is charting a new course for 2026, officially relocating its main event to Paris, France. From July 6 to August 23, 2026, the French capital will host the EWC, marking a significant shift as it moves outside Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, for the first time. Organizers have attributed this change to the "regional situation in the Middle East."

Beyond its new European home, the EWC is also introducing a fresh honor designed to put professional players squarely in the spotlight: the EWC Players' Choice Award. This new accolade will be decided solely by professional players, with peer voting spanning various titles and regions.

Players competing at the EWC 2026 in Paris will cast their votes in person. For other eligible professionals and clubs around the globe, a secure digital platform, managed by the Esports Awards, will facilitate their participation. To qualify, players must have participated in at least one S, A, or B-Tier competition during the competitive calendar. There's a clear rule: no voting for oneself or teammates, ensuring a truly objective peer recognition.

The 2026 EWC is poised to be a massive international affair, gathering more than 2,000 players and 200 clubs from over 100 nations. These competitors will battle across 25 tournaments spanning 24 different games, all vying for a record-setting prize pool of $75 million. This represents a substantial increase from the $71.5 million offered in 2025, further cementing the event's commitment to competitive excellence.

In related news, the Esports Awards and the Esports Foundation have extended their partnership through 2028. The Esports Awards ceremony for 2026 will take place in North America, kicking off a new rotating host-city model for the awards themselves. This dual announcement highlights an evolving landscape for the Esports World Cup, embracing global accessibility and deepening its connection with the player community.
