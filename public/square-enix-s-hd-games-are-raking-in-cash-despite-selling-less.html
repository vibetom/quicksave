Square Enix's HD Games Are Raking In Cash (Despite Selling Less)

A closer look at Square Enix's Q1 FY2026 earnings shows a massive jump in HD Games operating profit, even as overall sales dipped. Here's how strategic cost reductions and a focus on existing titles helped.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 28, 2026 · 10:39 UTC 3 MIN

Square Enix recently shared its financial outcomes for the first quarter of fiscal year 2026, which spans April to June 2025, revealing a complex picture. While the company's overall net sales saw a 15.2% year-over-year decline to 59.2 billion, and operating income fell 16.8% to 9.0 billion, a closer examination of the HD Games segment unveils a more surprising trend.

Even as the broader Digital Entertainment division, encompassing both HD Games and MMOs, experienced reduced sales, the HD Games sub-segment showcased an impressive surge in profitability. Net sales for HD Games themselves dipped to 8.9 billion, down from 12.3 billion compared to the prior year's period. This sales reduction is primarily linked to a lack of major new game releases in early 2025, leading to a quieter period for fresh titles.

However, what the HD Games segment might have lacked in raw revenue, it more than compensated for in operating profit. Profits for this sub-segment dramatically escalated by an astonishing 1900%, soaring from 0.05 billion to 1 billion. This significant increase in profitability is directly attributed to a deliberate reduction in both development and advertising expenditures when compared to the first quarter of the previous fiscal year.

While the roster of brand-new, big-budget releases was notably quieter during Q1, existing catalog titles and upcoming remasters proved to be crucial in maintaining revenue streams. Specific mentions include Final Fantasy Tactics The Ivalice Chronicles and the Dragon Quest 1&2 HD-2D Remake, both noted for helping to support profits for the current fiscal year. This strategic reliance on a robust back catalog and eagerly awaited remakes illustrates a calculated approach to market engagement.

The MMO segment also registered a year-over-year sales decrease of 14.5 billion. This particular dip was largely anticipated, often observed during the 'expansion gap year' that follows major releases like Final Fantasy XIV: Dawntrail in the previous year, underscoring the typical cyclical nature of major MMO content launches.

These Q1 results offer an early indication of the effects stemming from the 'dramatic restructuring' announced by Square Enix CEO Takashi Kiryu in May 2024. This initiative includes a comprehensive reassessment of the game development pipeline and a renewed emphasis on crafting titles for a wider array of platforms. The improved profitability in HD Games, fueled by more efficient spending and a focus on enduring titles, appears to be an initial outcome of this strategic reorientation, signaling a company prioritizing efficiency and broader market reach in its gaming endeavors.
