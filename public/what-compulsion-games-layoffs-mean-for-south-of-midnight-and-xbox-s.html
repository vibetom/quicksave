What Compulsion Games Layoffs Mean For 'South of Midnight' and Xbox's Future

Microsoft-owned Compulsion Games has begun layoffs, highlighting ongoing industry turbulence and Xbox's reported efforts to 'right the ship.'

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 25, 2026 · 19:22 UTC 2 MIN

Layoffs have begun at Compulsion Games, the Microsoft-owned studio known for its unique titles such as We Happy Few and Contrast, and currently developing the upcoming South of Midnight. The workforce reduction, which began on June 25, 2026, signals continued instability within the wider gaming industry.

While the exact number of employees affected by these recent changes has not been publicly disclosed, the impact on individuals within the studio is a significant concern. Compulsion Games is a key part of Microsoft's Xbox Game Studios family, making these developments particularly noteworthy for the future of their projects.

According to a report from Windows Central, these layoffs are reportedly part of a broader strategic shift within Microsoft's gaming division. Xbox CEO Asha Sharma is said to be leading an effort to "right the ship" of the Xbox brand, which has reportedly been facing a decline. This suggests that the changes at Compulsion Games might be one piece of a larger restructuring across Microsoft's studios.

The gaming industry has seen a wave of workforce reductions across various companies recently, and these layoffs at Compulsion Games add to that challenging trend. Even studios under the umbrella of major publishers like Microsoft are navigating a landscape where job security can be unpredictable.

The implications of these changes for South of Midnight and other potential projects from Compulsion Games remain to be seen. Development cycles are complex and often deeply affected by shifts in team size and structure. We at QUICKSAVE will continue to monitor how these reported restructuring efforts within Xbox might shape the future of its game development and the talented individuals who create these experiences.
