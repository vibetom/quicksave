Black Flag Resynced: Does Modernizing a Fan Favorite Make It Even Better?

Ubisoft's remake of Assassin's Creed Black Flag has sailed onto consoles and PC with glowing reviews, but how does it stack up to the original's legacy?

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 09, 2026 · 17:00 UTC 3 MIN

Ubisoft dropped anchor on a new adventure on July 9, 2026, with the launch of Assassin's Creed Black Flag Resynced. Available now on PS5, Xbox, and PC, this release revives one of the franchise's most cherished entries, bringing a classic back to modern screens.

This isn't just a simple port; Black Flag Resynced arrives with a treasure trove of enhancements. Players can expect overhauled graphics that breathe new life into the Caribbean setting, alongside modernized gameplay mechanics, thoughtful quality-of-life adjustments, and entirely new content. The goal, it seems, was to polish an already brilliant gem.

Early reviews suggest Ubisoft has hit the mark. The game has garnered widespread praise, with outlets like Loot Level Chill awarding it a perfect 10/10. They hailed it as a "superb remake of an already excellent adventure game," specifically commending its refined combat, authentic navigation, and unmatched sailing and ship combat. GameSpot also noted that the title "delivers the series' best reviews since 2013," a clear indicator of its success.

While the reception has been largely stellar, not every plank in the ship is perfectly smooth. Some reviewers have pointed out minor "jank in the combat animations." However, the consensus appears to be that these small imperfections are "not enough to hold this one back." Interestingly, despite overall praise, GameSpot did publish a review titled "Black Flag Resynced Is A Bad Remake Of Peak Assassin's Creed," offering a contrasting perspective on the remake's success in updating the original's feel.

For those looking to take their swashbuckling on the go, there's good news. Black Flag Resynced has earned a 'Verified' status on Steam Deck, implying a concerted effort towards handheld optimization. While stable performance above 30 frames per second might vary on the portable device, this means more players than ever can set sail from anywhere. It’s a fresh opportunity to revisit a beloved tale or experience it anew, now with modern sensibilities and the flexibility of handheld play.
