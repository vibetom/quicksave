Game Over For Discs: PlayStation Goes All-In On Digital By 2028

Sony's latest announcement confirms a full shift to digital-only new releases by early 2028, giving physical media collectors a deadline and hinting strongly at an all-digital PlayStation 6.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 02, 2026 · 11:05 UTC 3 MIN

Sony Interactive Entertainment has officially announced a pivotal shift for its console ecosystem: starting January 2028, physical game disc production for all new PlayStation titles will cease. This move signals a significant acceleration toward an all-digital future for the brand, mirroring broader trends across the entertainment landscape.

From January 2028 onward, players will find all new PlayStation games exclusively on the PlayStation Store and through digital storefronts at various retailers. It

is a clean break from the past, though not one that will erase existing collections; games already released on disc or slated for a physical launch before the January 2028 cutoff will remain unaffected. Your current disc library isn

isn't going anywhere.

SIE attributes this strategic pivot to the changing tides of consumer preference. The company notes a clear movement away from physical media and towards digital consumption across the entire entertainment sector. For PlayStation, this means aligning with how the majority of its community already chooses to engage with their games. The aim is to channel resources toward enhancing innovation in game access and purchasing options, whether directly through the PlayStation Store or via other digital retailers.

This announcement carries significant weight beyond just how we buy games today. Industry observers are already calling it a "gigantic hint" that the next iteration of PlayStation hardware, commonly referred to as the PlayStation 6, will likely be an all-digital console. If the market is shifting this decisively, it makes sense for future hardware to reflect that reality, shedding the disc drive as an optional, rather than essential, component.

As the industry continues its inexorable march toward digital-first experiences, Sony's move is less a surprising twist and more a confirmed trajectory. The console maker is planting its flag firmly in the digital landscape, setting the stage for what comes next in how we play and own our games.
