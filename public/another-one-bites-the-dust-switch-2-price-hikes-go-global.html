Another One Bites the Dust: Switch 2 Price Hikes Go Global

South Korea is the latest region to see a significant price increase for the Nintendo Switch 2, following similar adjustments across North America, Europe, and Japan. What's driving the change, and what does it mean for your wallet?

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 30, 2026 · 16:36 UTC 3 MIN

Nintendo has announced another price increase for the Switch 2, this time impacting South Korea. Starting September 1, 2026, the console will jump from "648,000" to "758,000", representing a substantial "110,000" difference, which is roughly $71 USD. This translates to a 16.9% increase for players in the region.

This move by Nintendo is not an isolated incident. The company has been systematically raising the price of the Switch 2 across various global markets. Previously, price adjustments were announced for the United States, where the console saw a $50 USD increase, and Canada, with a $50 CAD bump. European markets also experienced a "30" hike, while in Japan, the price rose by "10,000" to "59,980", effective May 25, 2026. Nintendo attributes these changes to "changes in various market environments" and a review of "future global business feasibility."

The driving force behind these widespread price adjustments appears to be the rising cost of components. Nintendo President Shuntaro Furukawa had previously indicated that an ongoing memory crisis, fueled by substantial investment in AI data centers, alongside increasing oil prices, could "put pressure on profitability" and affect hardware pricing. Indeed, reports suggest that RAM procurement costs for the Switch 2 have climbed by approximately 41% since the console's initial development phase, making manufacturing more expensive.

This trend of rising console prices isn't exclusive to Nintendo. Competitors like Sony and Microsoft have also implemented price increases for their hardware in recent times. Furthermore, the cost of Switch 2 software is following a similar upward trajectory; new exclusives such as Fire Emblem: Fortune's Weave are set to retail at $80 for physical copies and $70 for digital versions, mirroring the price point of Mario Kart World.

With hardware and software prices on the rise, players may wonder what else in the Nintendo ecosystem could be affected. While Nintendo Switch Online (NSO) subscriptions have not yet seen price increases in regions like the U.S., ScreenRant has reported that adjustments to NSO pricing are considered "likely just a matter of time", given Nintendo's pattern of changes, though this remains to be seen. Players worldwide should prepare for the possibility of further price shifts across Nintendo's offerings.
