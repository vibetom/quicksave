PlayStation's Next-Gen Could Cost You Big: What Leaks Say About PS6 and Project Canis

With reported component price hikes and high memory costs through 2027, the PS6 'Orion' might be PlayStation's most expensive console yet, alongside a powerful new native handheld.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 15, 2026 · 10:20 UTC 5 MIN

The curtain is beginning to part on Sony's next console generation, and early indications suggest players might need to brace their wallets. Recent leaks in June and July 2026 have painted a picture of a potent, yet potentially pricey, future for PlayStation, with details emerging for the console codenamed 'Orion' and a new handheld device, 'Project Canis.'

According to reports from Shattered, the component cost for 'Orion' has reportedly climbed by around $200 in just three months. This substantial increase in the bill of materials arrives at a time when Sony's CEO has publicly acknowledged that memory costs are projected to remain very high through their fiscal year 2027, as reported by Wccftech. This confirmed market reality, paired with the rumored jump in production costs, has led independent analysts, according to GameRant, to consider a four-figure retail price for the PS6 as a realistic, rather than remote, possibility. This aligns with recent industry trends, such as Valve's Steam Machine launching its top configuration at $1,049 on June 29, 2026, as noted by Kotaku.

Beyond the console, rumors from Shattered point to 'Project Canis,' a companion handheld device. This device is described as Sony's first native gaming handheld capable of running full PlayStation software locally, a significant departure from the remote-play dependent PS Vita and PlayStation Portal, according to Wccftech. Leaked specifications for Project Canis, reported by GameRant, include a smaller Zen 6 configuration, an RDNA 5 GPU, a memory setup using LPDDR5X technology, with options for 16GB or 24GB connected via a 192-bit pathway, and a 1080p display offering 60Hz or 120Hz refresh rates. This suggests Sony might be ready to re-enter the dedicated portable gaming arena with a powerful new contender.

Further details concerning the console itself include a reported PlayStation 6 patent that, according to Kotaku, scraps liquid metal cooling. This move potentially addresses issues encountered with the PS5, where leaks reportedly 'fried APUs and motherboards for years.' This evolution in cooling technology, alongside the other rumored specifications, points to a console designed for cutting-edge performance, albeit at what could be a premium price point for consumers.
