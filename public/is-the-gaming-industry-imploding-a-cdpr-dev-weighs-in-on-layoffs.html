Is the Gaming Industry 'Imploding'? A CDPR Dev Weighs In on Layoffs

Cyberpunk 2077's Pawe\u0142 Sasko shares his deep concerns as job cuts continue to rock major studios, raising questions about the future for developers.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 28, 2026 · 18:41 UTC 3 MIN

The video game industry, a landscape often seen as resilient, is currently facing a period of intense instability, prompting even those within seemingly secure positions to voice their apprehension. Pawe\u0142 Sasko, a designer at CD Projekt RED, recently took to social media to express his profound worries about the future, stating that the constant job losses sometimes make him feel the industry is "imploding."

Sasko's comments arrive as the gaming sector grapples with widespread turbulence, a period marked by thousands of job eliminations and numerous studio closures. This current wave of restructuring has left many developers facing an uncertain path forward, even as major titles continue to ship.

The impact is visible across the industry's biggest players. Electronic Arts, for instance, saw a significant reduction in its development staff. Bungie, the studio behind popular franchises like Destiny and the upcoming Marathon, has also undergone staff cuts. Looking ahead, Xbox Game Studios is reportedly undergoing a major internal restructuring, with some internal reports indicating further job cuts are likely across various teams. Certain first-party Xbox studios, including Ninja Theory, Compulsion Games, and Double Fine, are reportedly at a critical juncture, with some said to be exploring options to regain their independence.

Even a studio like CD Projekt RED, known for its critically acclaimed titles, is not immune to the wider anxieties. The Polish developer is currently deep into multiple high-profile projects, including the highly anticipated The Witcher 4, a sequel to Cyberpunk 2077, and a brand-new intellectual property codenamed Hadar. The studio is also actively working to achieve a consistent 60 frames per second on consoles for The Witcher 4, noting the Xbox Series S as a particular challenge for optimization. Yet, despite these ambitious endeavors, the collective concern voiced by developers like Sasko underscores the human impact of these widespread layoffs, reflecting a pervasive sense of precarity that transcends individual company successes.
