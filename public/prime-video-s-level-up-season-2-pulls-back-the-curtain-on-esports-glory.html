Prime Video's 'Level Up' Season 2 Pulls Back the Curtain on Esports Glory (and Grind)

The docuseries takes viewers behind the scenes of the 2025 Esports World Cup, showcasing the players, families, and clubs chasing a $70 million prize pool in Riyadh.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 18, 2026 · 22:29 UTC 2 MIN

The curtain is set to rise once more on the high-stakes world of professional esports, as the Esports Foundation has announced the return of 'Esports World Cup: Level Up' for its second season. Fans eager for a deeper look into competitive gaming can mark their calendars: the entire five-episode second season will debut on Prime Video on June 26, 2026.

This docuseries isn't just about the clicks and quick reflexes; it's a deep dive into the human stories that power the competitive scene. Under the direction of Emmy-winning filmmaker R.J. Cutler, 'Level Up' offers an intimate look at the 2025 Esports World Cup, capturing the personal journeys of players, their clubs, and even their families. It

Among the featured competitors are notable figures from the esports landscape, offering a window into their individual quests. Viewers will follow Jake 'Boaster' Howlett, a VALORANT star from Fnatic, and Vivi 'Vivian' Indrawaty of Team Vitality, who competes in MLBB. Their stories, woven into the fabric of the championship, promise an intimate glimpse into the dedication and pressure required to reach the pinnacle of esports.

The second season transports viewers to Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, where the seven-week-long 2025 Esports World Cup unfolded. Here, the series tracks the relentless pursuit of the formidable $70 million prize pool and the coveted EWC Club Championship. It's a snapshot of ambition and skill colliding on a global stage, with fortunes on the line.
