National Pride, Powerhouse Rosters: What The ENC 2026 Reveals Mean For Esports

The Esports Nations Cup 2026 unveils its lineups for Rocket League, Dota 2, Rainbow Six Siege, and Mobile Legends: Bang Bang, showcasing a blend of returning champions and national dream teams ready for Riyadh.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 12, 2026 · 22:05 UTC 4 MIN

The Esports Foundation has pulled back the curtain on the initial rosters for the inaugural Esports Nations Cup (ENC) 2026, setting the stage for a global showdown fueled by national pride. On June 11, 2026, the lineups for Rocket League, Dota 2, Ubisoft's Rainbow Six Siege, and Mobile Legends: Bang Bang were officially revealed, marking a significant moment for the country-based competition. The ENC 2026 is slated to debut in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, running from November 2 to 29, 2026.

In Rocket League, the reveal offered a glimpse of some truly formidable national teams. Fans will see a highly anticipated reunion for Team Saudi Arabia, as the former Falcons trio of Rw9, trk511, and Kiileerrz will play alongside Nwpo. Not to be outdone, Team France fields a strong lineup with Vatira, juicy, and zen, while Team United States brings together talents like Atomic, Daniel, Firstkiller, and Chronic.

Moving to Dota 2, the ENC will feature a staggering 88 national rosters. Team China arrives with a powerful blend of talent drawn from Xtreme Gaming and Vici Gaming, signaling a force to be reckoned with. Across the pond, Team United States showcases experienced players such as Sneyking, Quinn, and SumaiL. It's also notable that players from Saudi-based Team Falcons, like Sneyking and Cr1t-, are represented across various national rosters, highlighting the widespread impact of prominent esports organizations.

Ubisoft's Rainbow Six Siege promises intense competition, with elite national teams entering the fray. Team Brazil stands out as the top-ranked roster, ready to defend its position. Meanwhile, Team France has reunited a core of international champions, signaling their intent to challenge for the title. Mobile Legends: Bang Bang, a titan in its own right, emerges as one of the largest competitions within the ENC program, boasting an impressive 107 confirmed national rosters, underlining the title's massive global appeal.

The reveal of the ENC rosters arrives amidst a bustling esports calendar, a testament to the vibrant and ever-expanding global competitive scene. Qualification events for the Esports World Cup 2026, which has relocated to Paris, are currently underway this month across various titles. Beyond the ENC, major tournaments like the IEM Cologne Major 2026 for Counter-Strike 2 are generating significant buzz, with wagering markets on Kalshi indicating $23.7 million in total activity for CS2 this week. VALORANT Masters London is also in full swing, running through June 21. We've also just seen the conclusion of the War Thunder Championship Series Pro League Major II, where Team Invictus secured first place and Leozander earned the MVP title. The competitive year is packed with high-stakes events, including the Honor of Kings World Cup 2026 ($3,000,000), MLBB Mid Season Cup 2026 ($3,000,000), PUBG Mobile Global Championship 2026 ($3,000,000), PUBG Mobile World Cup 2026 ($3,000,000), and the Esports World Cup 2026's Rainbow Six Siege tournament ($2,000,000), all featuring substantial prize pools of $1 million or more.
