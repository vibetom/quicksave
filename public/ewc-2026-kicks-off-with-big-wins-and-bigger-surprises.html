EWC 2026 Kicks Off With Big Wins and Bigger Surprises

Paris is buzzing as the Esports World Cup begins, with VALORANT, Apex Legends, and Fatal Fury already seeing championships conclude and the Club Championship race heating up.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 10, 2026 · 11:23 UTC 5 MIN

The Esports World Cup 2026 has opened its doors in Paris, transforming the Paris Expo Porte de Versailles into a global battleground for competitive gaming. Running from July 8 through August 23, the event is a true titan in the esports world, boasting a colossal $75 million prize pool spread across 25 tournaments and 24 different games. Over 2,000 players and 200 clubs, representing more than 100 countries, have converged to stake their claim in this massive spectacle.

The opening week has already delivered a flurry of high-stakes action, with championships for VALORANT, Apex Legends, and Fatal Fury: City of the Wolves set to conclude. The VALORANT championship, in particular, is reaching its climax on Sunday, July 12. Defending EWC champions Team Heretics have shown their prowess by topping Group D without a single series loss. American powerhouse 100 Thieves and French fan favorites Team Vitality and Gentle Mates are also holding strong, each boasting unblemished 2-0 records. The pressure is on, as teams navigate a single-elimination, best-of-three playoff bracket, with 2025 VALORANT Champions winners NRG also in the fray.

Beyond the tactical gunplay of VALORANT, Week 1 is also seeing the conclusion of the ALGS Year 6 Split 1 Playoffs for Apex Legends, a $2 million prize pool tournament where Elite Esports EU currently commands the standings with 138 points, holding an 18-point lead over their closest competitors. Other top teams like Team Liquid, AG.AL, and Gaimin Gladiators are locked in the chase. Meanwhile, the fighting game arena is witnessing 32 top competitors clash in Fatal Fury: City of the Wolves, vying for a $1 million prize pool that will crown its champions by week's end.

The Dota 2 group stage has also kicked off with its share of early drama and upsets. The 24 teams are battling for a $2 million prize pool, leading up to the Dota 2 Championship Weekend in Week 2. Favorites such as Team Falcons and Team Liquid have been held to unexpected draws by BB Team and PlayTime, respectively. In a significant early surprise, underdogs Rune Eaters managed to secure a draw against Xtreme Gaming, shaking up the initial expectations. Teams like Nigma Galaxy, Aurora Gaming, PVISION, 1W, and GamerLegion have also made strong starts, indicating a wide-open tournament ahead.

Underpinning all this individual game competition is the overarching $30 million Club Championship race. Clubs earn points based on their performance across various tournaments, making every match a crucial step towards overall supremacy. Saudi's Team Falcons are reportedly aiming for their third Club Championship title, adding another layer of intrigue to the proceedings. While the EA SPORTS FC 26 main event is yet to begin, its Last Chance Qualifier started on July 9, ensuring the football simulation fans aren't left out of the early excitement. The “Road to EWC” qualification program, which saw over 1.5 million players participate across 330 tournaments, has clearly set the stage for this truly global showcase, with coverage available in over 40 languages and a robust Creator Program supporting up to 5,000 official co-streamers.
