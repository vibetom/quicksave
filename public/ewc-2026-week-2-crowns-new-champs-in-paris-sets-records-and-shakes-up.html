EWC 2026: Week 2 Crowns New Champs in Paris, Sets Records, and Shakes Up the Leaderboard

From Free Fire's elimination record to a dominant Dota 2 victory, the Esports World Cup's second week brought plenty of action and new faces to the winner's circle.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 20, 2026 · 16:08 UTC 4 MIN

The Esports World Cup 2026 continued its high-stakes action this past week, with Paris hosting the crowning of four new champions. As the tournament unfolds, its global appeal and historic prize pool are being matched by the fresh narratives emerging from the competitive arena.

Individual glory shone brightly across several titles. LYON blazed a trail in Free Fire, not only taking the title but also setting a new EWC elimination record with 33 kills, culminating in a decisive 'Booyah' in the fifth game of the finals. Team Vitality raised the Mobile Legends: Bang Bang Women's International trophy, while Dplus KIA secured a significant victory in League of Legends. PVISION demonstrated commanding form in Dota 2, overcoming Team BB with a 3-1 score in the Grand Final, a performance that earned Evgeniy “Noticed” Ignatenko the MVP honors for the event.

This year marks a historic moment for the EWC, as it's being held in Paris, France, for the first time outside Saudi Arabia. The event features an unprecedented US$75 million prize pool, drawing over two thousand competitors and two hundred organizations representing more than one hundred nations. These participants are battling across 25 tournaments spanning 24 diverse game titles.

The massive prize money is thoughtfully distributed across multiple layers, designed to reward excellence at various levels. A substantial US$30 million is allocated to the Club Championship, with approximately US$7 million designated for the ultimate champion club. Additionally, funds are set aside for individual Game Championships, MVP awards, player honors, and a range of qualifying events, ensuring both team cohesion and individual brilliance are recognized.

Following a dramatic Week 1 that saw unexpected upsets in VALORANT and an emotional victory for Kenta 'mi2ha4' Ichihara in FATAL FURY, Week 2 has maintained the EWC's reputation for unpredictable and intense competition. The previous week also witnessed local French VALORANT teams Gentle Mates and Team Vitality advance undefeated through their group stages, with Gentle Mates famously upsetting Masters Santiago winners Nongshim RedForce, though both were ultimately eliminated in the playoffs.

The Esports World Cup continues its run through August 23, promising many more weeks of high-octane competition and the emergence of new storylines from its diverse array of games and global participants.
