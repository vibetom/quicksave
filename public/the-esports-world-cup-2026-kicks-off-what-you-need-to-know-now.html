The Esports World Cup 2026 Kicks Off: What You Need To Know Now

Paris is hosting the biggest names in Dota 2, Valorant, and League of Legends, with millions on the line and real-time viewership data for this year's massive club-focused event.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 04, 2026 · 12:41 UTC 3 MIN

The Esports World Cup (EWC) 2026 has officially begun, transforming Paris, France, into the epicenter of competitive gaming. The sprawling event kicked off on July 6 and will run through August 23, with the initial wave of major competitions scheduled from July 6 to July 19. While the official start date for the overall EWC is July 6, the action for some titles has already begun.

Indeed, the Valorant EWC tournament is already deep into its group stages, which ran from July 2-7, leading into playoffs from July 9-12. According to EGamersWorld, Team Heretics has already secured a victory, edging out All Gamers 2-1 in a Group D opener on July 3. Hot on its heels, the Dota 2 EWC is set to run from July 6-18, followed by the League of Legends EWC, which will take center stage from July 13-19.

This year's EWC is a monumental undertaking, featuring 25 tournaments spanning 24 different games and drawing more than 2,000 players. The prize pools are equally staggering: a combined $75,000,000 is on the line across all games, with an additional $30,000,000 dedicated to the Club Championship. Each of the individual Dota 2, Valorant, and League of Legends EWC tournaments boasts its own $2,000,000 prize pool. This structure underscores the EWC's emphasis on club organizations competing for supremacy, rather than a focus on national teams.

Adding a layer of spectacle to the intense competition, the official opening ceremony is slated for July 8 at La Seine Musicale in Paris. The event promises a star-studded affair, featuring performances by DJ Snake, Aya Nakamura, and Theodora.

For those eager to track the pulse of the tournament, Esports Charts has launched a dedicated EWC Viewership Hub. Starting July 6, this hub will deliver real-time audience data, daily updates, and breaking news. This initiative builds on the impressive performance of the 2025 EWC, which saw a significant 51.3% growth in total viewing hours compared to the prior year, accumulating nearly 184 million hours viewed and attracting over 3 million concurrent viewers during the MLBB Mid Season Cup 2025. With French creators Inoxtag and Squeezie also set to face off in an exclusive Valorant Showmatch, the 2026 EWC is poised to capture an even larger audience.
