Esports World Cup 2026 Crowns First Champs, But What's Next?

Paris is buzzing as the EWC 2026 hits its stride, with major victories for Fatal Fury and Apex Legends, and a massive VALORANT showdown on the horizon. Here's a look at the early winners and what to watch for.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 12, 2026 · 10:06 UTC 2 MIN

The Esports World Cup 2026 is officially in full swing, turning Paris into a vibrant hub for competitive gaming. Running from July 6th to August 23rd, this sprawling event features an immense $75 million prize pool distributed across 25 tournaments and 24 different game titles, as reported by the Daily Tribune.

The initial week wrapped up with a flourish, celebrating the first champions in events like Fatal Fury: City of the Wolves and Apex Legends. These early finishes set a high bar for the competition and showcased the diverse lineup of games vying for glory.

Natus Vincere's Darkangel claimed the very first Fatal Fury: City of the Wolves championship on July 11th. This impressive win netted them $250,000 from a $1 million prize pool, along with 1,000 Club Championship points. This victory, highlighted by Bitcoin News, marked a strong start for the fighting game scene at the EWC 2026.

Meanwhile, the Apex Legends Global Series saw its Split 1 Playoffs seamlessly integrated into the Paris festival. Team UNLIMIT emerged victorious in the 2026 ALGS event, further solidifying the EWC's ambition to unite existing competitive circuits with its grand stage, according to PR Newswire.

Looking ahead, all eyes are on the VALORANT EWC 2026 Grand Final. An exciting all-North American showdown is confirmed between 100 Thieves and NRG. Both teams secured their spots by winning their semifinals on July 11th, and will now face off for a substantial $600,000 and 1000 EWC Club points, as reported by VALORANT Esports News. This promises another high-stakes clash as the tournament continues to unfold.
