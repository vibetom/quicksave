Drill Baby Drill: How MOLE Tunneled Into Our Collective Horror Hearts

An indie psychological horror game about operating a deep-bore drilling machine has surprised everyone by becoming one of the year's most talked-about and highly-rated titles, thanks to tight design and major streamer attention.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 07, 2026 · 13:31 UTC 3 MIN

Sometimes, a game just hits different. The indie horror title `MOLE` recently launched on Steam, and within its first two weeks, it didn't just hit — it burrowed deep into the collective consciousness, earning a stunning "97% Overwhelmingly Positive" rating from over 1,500 user reviews. This isn't just critical acclaim; it's a testament to a unique vision carving out a space in a crowded genre.

`MOLE`, a psychological horror experience brought to life by Off Black Creations and published by Oro Interactive, plunges players into the claustrophobic confines of a massive deep-bore drilling machine. The objective? Descend into the Earth, chasing a mysterious signal from below. But this isn't a mere simulation; it's a descent into psychological horror, where hands-on machinery operation intertwines with profound isolation and a creeping sense of breakdown. Players are left to uncover complex systems through trial and error, piecing together fragmented memories and environmental clues as the machine groans and something unknown calls from the abyss.

Part of `MOLE`'s explosive success can be attributed to a powerful groundswell of support from major content creators. Streamers like Jacksepticeye, Insym, and Hollow have all highlighted the game to their massive audiences, with many nominating `MOLE` as one of the best horror experiences of the year. Their enthusiasm acted like a sonic boom, amplifying the game's reach far beyond what a typical indie title might expect.

The unique terror of `MOLE` springs from a clear creative well. Off Black Creations was founded by Sean Falk, a former AAA developer who previously worked as a level designer on `GTFO`. Falk left the world of large studios specifically to prioritize creative direction in an independent setting. This singular focus shines through in `MOLE`'s design: a tightly woven horror experience that clocks in at a concise three to four hours. It's proof that sometimes, less truly is more, especially when every moment is crafted with such deliberate intent.

Oro Interactive, `MOLE`'s publisher, also plays a crucial role in bringing such distinct visions to light. Their stated mission is to support "creative, and wonderfully bizarre games... that instantly hook players." This ethos is a perfect match for `MOLE`'s unconventional premise. Adding another layer of industry support, Oro Interactive benefits from a minority investment from Kepler Interactive, a publisher recognized for anticipated titles like `Clair Obscur: Expedition 33`. This backing creates a fertile ground for unique games like `MOLE` to not only see the light of day but also to thrive and surprise us all.
