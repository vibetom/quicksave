Esports World Cup Kicks Off In Paris With Record Prize Pool and Big Questions About Crypto

The Esports World Cup 2026 is officially underway in Paris, boasting a $75 million prize pool and new French regulations that could change the game for crypto sponsorships.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 05, 2026 · 10:35 UTC 2 MIN

The Esports World Cup 2026 has officially opened its doors in Paris, France, setting the stage for a summer of high-stakes competition. Running from July 6 through August 23, the event is taking over the Paris Expo Porte de Versailles, drawing eyes from across the globe.

This year's EWC is not just a major tournament; it's a financial behemoth, featuring an unprecedented total prize pool of $75 million. This sum cements its position as one of the most lucrative events in esports history, offering a staggering opportunity for competing teams and individual players.

Beyond the prize money, the French capital is also a fitting backdrop for a new chapter in esports economics. New regulations in France now permit licensed crypto and blockchain companies to sponsor esports teams. This policy shift could dramatically reshape sponsorship landscapes, potentially ushering in a wave of new financial partnerships for organizations within the competitive gaming sphere.

Reports indicate that the EWC's relocation from Riyadh to Paris in May 2026 was prompted by escalating conflict in the Middle East, which had begun to disrupt air travel to the region. This reported shift underscores the broader, real-world factors that can influence even the most insulated corners of the gaming world.

As the tournament unfolds, all eyes will be on not just the thrilling matches, but also on how these new financial currents—from the record prize pool to the evolving crypto sponsorship rules—will impact the future of esports.
