Resident Evil's 'Forgotten' Chapter Is Finally Getting Its Due — and Its Producer Has Thoughts

Code Veronica is being remade for 2027 across PS5, Xbox Series, Switch 2, and PC. Capcom's Yoshiaki Hirabayashi says it deserves to stand with the numbered games. The fans have been saying that for 25 years.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 10, 2026 · 16:30 UTC 2 MIN

For a quarter century, Resident Evil Code: Veronica has been the series' great asterisk — beloved by longtime fans, essential to the saga of Chris and Claire Redfield, and perpetually skipped in the remake renaissance that has already rebuilt RE2, RE3, and RE4. That asterisk is finally being erased.

Resident Evil Code Veronica is being remade, with a 2027 launch planned for PS5, Xbox Series, Switch 2, and PC. The project got a striking trailer at Summer Game Fest, and in a post-show Q&A, producer Yoshiaki Hirabayashi made the case fans have been making since the Dreamcast era: that Code Veronica deserves a remake and is just as important as the numbered entries.

That statement matters more than it might look. Code Veronica's odd position — a mainline story without a mainline number — is widely seen as the reason it kept losing the remake queue to flashier siblings. Hearing Capcom's producer frame it as a peer of the numbered games reads like a deliberate re-canonization.

The rumor worth flagging

One more wrinkle, clearly filed under rumor: leaker Dusk Golem claims there are murmurs the Code Veronica remake could actually arrive before the Requiem DLC. Dusk Golem has a notable track record on Resident Evil specifically, but Capcom has confirmed nothing about relative timing, so treat that as informed speculation and nothing more.

For now, the confirmed picture is enough: the most-requested remake in the series' history is real, it's multiplatform, and it has a producer publicly invested in giving it the respect fans always felt it was owed. 2027 suddenly has a very crowded survival-horror calendar.
