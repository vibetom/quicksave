Wait, Dragon Age: Dreadwolf is Now The Veilguard? Here's What That Means.

BioWare is dropping 15+ minutes of gameplay for the newly renamed Dragon Age: The Veilguard. The change signals a renewed focus on companions and player choice.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 24, 2026 · 13:35 UTC 4 MIN

After years of anticipation, BioWare has officially rebranded its next Dragon Age installment, moving past the working title Dragon Age: Dreadwolf to introduce Dragon Age: The Veilguard. This shift comes alongside the exciting news of a comprehensive gameplay reveal, scheduled for Tuesday, June 11, at 8:00 AM Pacific Time, 11:00 AM Eastern Time, or 4:00 PM UK time. Fans can tune into the official Dragon Age YouTube channel to witness over 15 minutes of footage from the game's opening moments.

IGN first reported the game's name change. According to BioWare executive producer Gary McKay, the new title better reflects the core emphasis of the game: the player and their companions. While the 'Dread Wolf,' Solas, still holds a significant place in the narrative, the spotlight is now firmly on the player's custom-created hero and the seven distinct companions who will join their journey. Each companion is promised to have compelling storylines, with player decisions directly shaping relationships and their fates.

This re-focus signals a deliberate return to the series' roots. BioWare has confirmed The Veilguard will be a single-player RPG experience, a decision made after exploring various concepts, including potential multiplayer elements. This commitment to the single-player formula, driven by player choice and deep character interactions, aims to reassure long-time fans that the heart of Dragon Age remains intact.

So, who exactly will be accompanying us on this grand adventure? While we've seen a brief teaser for Dragon Age: The Veilguard at the Xbox showcase, more details are emerging about the party members. Reported characters include returning favorites like Varric and Scout Harding, alongside a fresh cast. These new allies are reported to be Neve, a mage detective; Lucanis, a 'mage killer;' Bellara, a 'veil jumper' who utilizes Eluvians; Emmrich, a necromancer; and Davrin, a Grey Warden. Together, they'll embark on a standalone narrative set within the vibrant world of Thedas, exploring new and epic locales as they face fresh threats.

The upcoming gameplay reveal isn't just a glimpse; it's our first substantial look at how this renewed vision for Dragon Age translates into actual play. With a reported launch window of Fall for PC, PlayStation 5, and Xbox Series X/S, this reveal serves as a crucial moment for BioWare to showcase the evolution of one of gaming's most beloved RPG franchises. We're eager to see how the 'Veilguard' comes together.
