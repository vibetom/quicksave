Take-Two Is Rolling In Cash—And They're Ready To Spend It

Strong performances from *NBA 2K*, *GTA Online*, and Zynga titles have exceeded expectations, with *GTA VI* on the horizon, Take-Two is openly eyeing new acquisitions.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 21, 2026 · 12:52 UTC 3 MIN

Take-Two Interactive recently pulled back the curtain on an "exceptional" fiscal year 2026, blowing past initial expectations and signaling a readiness to flex its financial muscle with potential new acquisitions.

The company announced that GAAP net revenue for the fiscal year, which wrapped up on March 31, 2026, soared 18% to a hefty $6.66 billion, a significant jump from the $5.63 billion reported in the previous year. Total Net Bookings for FY2026 hit $6.72 billion, eclipsing initial guidance by approximately $750 million. Even the GAAP net loss saw a dramatic improvement, narrowing to $298.2 million, or $1.62 per share, a vast recovery from the $4.48 billion loss in the prior year.

This impressive performance wasn't a fluke; it was powered by a roster of consistent heavy hitters. The NBA 2K series achieved unprecedented Net Bookings and robust spending from its players, while Grand Theft Auto Online and Grand Theft Auto V continued to be major drivers. Zynga, too, had its best year since its 2022 acquisition, reaching its highest level of Net Bookings. Other key contributors to the revenue stream included several prominent titles like Borderlands 4, Red Dead Redemption 2, Red Dead Online, and a selection of mobile hits such as Toon Blast, Match Factory!, Empires & Puzzles, Color Block Jam, and Words With Friends.

A significant portion of this financial health comes from long-term player engagement: recurrent consumer spending climbed 16% and accounted for a dominant 78% of the total GAAP net revenue. This underscores the power of sustained interest in Take-Two's diverse portfolio, from AAA blockbusters to mobile staples.

Looking ahead, the company is poised for an even bigger splash. Strauss Zelnick, who serves as Chairman and CEO of Take-Two Interactive, conveyed that Fiscal 2027 is expected to "establish new record levels of operating performance." The catalyst for this ambitious forecast? The highly anticipated launch of Grand Theft Auto VI on November 19th. With its release, Take-Two anticipates generating over $1 billion in operating cash flow in the next 12 months, setting a new benchmark for the company.

This robust financial outlook isn't just about bigger numbers on a balance sheet; it's about future strategy. Take-Two has explicitly stated its readiness "to pursue value-adding merger and acquisition prospects and to build out our in-house project pipeline." In an industry currently undergoing significant shifts, this declaration from a company flush with cash and a monumental release on the horizon signals potential major moves, possibly reshaping the landscape with new acquisitions. Full financial details are expected to be unveiled in a shareholder call on September 17, two months prior to Grand Theft Auto VI's arrival.
