Esports World Cup 2026: The First Championship Weekend Is Here

Trophies are on the line for VALORANT, Apex Legends, and Fatal Fury as the Esports World Cup heats up in Paris, offering a glimpse into the future of competitive gaming.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 10, 2026 · 16:37 UTC 4 MIN

The Esports World Cup 2026 has officially kicked off its run in Paris, transforming the city into a global battleground for competitive gaming. Running from July 6th through August 23rd, this year's event is a landmark occasion, boasting a record-shattering $75 million prize pool spread across 25 tournaments and 24 different games. Over 2,000 players and 200 clubs, hailing from more than 100 countries, are currently engaged in a fierce contest for supremacy.

This weekend marks the first major Championship Weekend for the Esports World Cup, with critical trophies up for grabs. Fans are witnessing the culmination of intense competition in `Riot Games' tactical shooter`, `Respawn Entertainment's battle royale`, and `SNK's fighting game, Fatal Fury: City of the Wolves`. Alongside these individual game championships, the high-stakes $30 million Club Championship race is escalating, as teams like Saudi Arabia's Team Falcons aim to secure what would be their third title.

In VALORANT, the defending Esports World Cup champions, Team Heretics, have already made a strong impression, dominating Group D without a single series loss. Their quest for another title will conclude on Sunday, July 12th, with a series of single-elimination, best-of-three matches. Meanwhile, the Apex Legends (ALGS Year 6 Split 1 Playoffs) competition has seen its group stage wrap up, with 14 teams directly advancing to the Finals. The Survival Stage is underway today, July 10th, and the Finals are set to run from July 11th-12th. Elite Esports EU currently holds a commanding lead with 138 points, 18 points ahead of the second-place team, all battling for a share of the $2 million prize pool. Not to be outdone, Fatal Fury: City of the Wolves also brings a substantial $1 million prize pool to the Esports World Cup.

Beyond the intense competition, the Esports World Cup 2026 is actively broadening its appeal and reach. New game additions for this year's contests include Trackmania, Fortnite in a Reload format, `Activision's Call of Duty: Black Ops 7`, and `EA's football sim, EA Sports FC 26`, reflecting the evolving landscape of esports. The event, hosted at Paris Expo Porte de Versailles, has also embraced an entertainment component, with an opening ceremony featuring performances from artists like DJ Snake, Aya Nakamura, and Theodora. This push for broader engagement aligns with the rapid expansion of the global esports audience, which reached 640.8 million people in 2025. This figure underscores the growing interest and serves as a benchmark for the impressive 8.1% annual growth rate for enthusiasts observed since 2020, demonstrating the massive potential of events like the Esports World Cup. With broadcasts accessible in more than 40 languages and support from up to 5,000 official co-streamers helping spread the word, the Esports World Cup 2026 is not just a tournament; it's a global spectacle underscoring the ascendance of competitive gaming.
