Xbox's Studio Shake-Up: What the Layoffs Mean for Devs and Your Favorite Games

Recent job cuts hit ZeniMax Online, Obsidian, Bethesda, and Id Software, sparking concerns about the future of iconic franchises and the people who make them.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 09, 2026 · 21:55 UTC 3 MIN

Xbox has recently announced a wave of layoffs impacting several of its prominent game studios, including ZeniMax Online Studios, Obsidian Entertainment, Bethesda Game Studios, and Id Software. These significant job reductions have ripple effects for developers and raise questions about the stability of ongoing projects and the future of beloved franchises.

The ZeniMax umbrella has seen a total of 379 layoffs across its various entities. A significant portion of these cuts, 213 jobs, occurred specifically at ZeniMax Online Studios in Maryland, according to a WARN notice. These particular reductions mean ZeniMax Online Studios has lost at least 60% of its staff within the last year. Additionally, 166 layoffs occurred at ZeniMax Media, a group that includes Bethesda Game Studios.

Obsidian Entertainment also faced job reductions, with 52 developers laid off in California. These cuts come alongside reports that unannounced projects at the studio have been canceled. Despite this, a small team is reportedly continuing work on a planned Avowed sequel, and it's also reported that a new Fallout game is in development at Obsidian, with New Vegas director Josh Sawyer at the helm.

The impact on Bethesda Game Studios has raised specific concerns. Staff members have reportedly expressed fears that the layoffs could lead to project delays, increased crunch time, and a shift away from experienced, long-term talent in favor of contract workers. These anxieties have reportedly led staff to suggest potential repercussions for the development of highly anticipated titles such as The Elder Scrolls 6.

These recent workforce reductions across multiple key studios underscore a broader period of change and uncertainty within the gaming industry. The confirmed job losses affect hundreds of individuals, casting a long shadow over the future stability and creative direction for some of gaming's most iconic titles under the Xbox banner.
