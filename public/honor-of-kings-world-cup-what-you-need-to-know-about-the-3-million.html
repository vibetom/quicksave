Honor of Kings World Cup: What You Need to Know About the $3 Million Mobile MOBA Showdown

Full details for the Esports World Cup 2026's Honor of Kings tournament are here, bringing together players from two communities on a custom patch.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 02, 2026 · 16:18 UTC 4 MIN

The Esports World Cup 2026 is steadily approaching, and the full schedule for the highly anticipated Honor of Kings World Cup has now been revealed. Mobile MOBA enthusiasts can mark their calendars: the tournament is set to kick off on July 30th, 2026, culminating in the Grand Final on August 8th. The entire Esports World Cup 2026, including this major mobile title showdown, will unfold in Paris, France.

Competitors will vie for a substantial $3 million prize pool, positioning this event as a significant milestone in the mobile esports landscape. This substantial sum reflects the growing stature of mobile gaming on the global competitive stage.

One of the most compelling aspects of the Honor of Kings World Cup is its ambitious roster. The tournament will uniquely bring together top players from both the traditional 'Honor of Kings' circuit and the 'Arena of Valor' community. This merger promises a fresh competitive dynamic, as strategies and playstyles from two distinct, yet related, mobile MOBA scenes collide.

Adding another layer of strategic depth, the competition will be played on a dedicated patch of 'Honor of Kings'. This custom build, based on Season 15 of the game, will feature 111 playable heroes. The developers state this extensive hero pool is designed to offer "maximum strategic depth and ban-pick flexibility," ensuring that teams will need to be well-versed in a wide array of compositions and counter-picks to secure victory.

The tournament itself is structured to rigorously test its participants. It begins with a Play-In event, which will narrow an initial field of eight teams down to four. These successful teams will then advance to the Group Stage, where 16 teams, split into four groups, will battle it out, with only the top two from each group moving forward. Finally, the eight remaining teams will enter the Playoffs, a single-elimination bracket where they will compete for the championship title.

While the Honor of Kings World Cup focuses on a single title, it's worth noting the broader competitive landscape of the EWC 2026. Team Falcons, for instance, is making its presence felt across 25 different events within the World Cup, aiming to maximize their accumulation of Club Championship points. A total of $30 million is allocated for these Club Championship points, with a massive $7 million reserved for the winning club, highlighting the extensive investment in this year's esports spectacle.
