VALORANT Takes Paris: What to Expect from the Esports World Cup Groups and Massive Stakes

The Esports World Cup's VALORANT tournament is officially moving to Paris, bringing with it a hefty prize pool, club points, and a newly unveiled group stage that promises an intense competition from July 2-12.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 28, 2026 · 12:52 UTC 3 MIN

The Esports World Cup's VALORANT tournament is making a dramatic shift, officially relocating to Paris for its run from July 2 to July 12, 2026. This marks a significant change for the event, as it will be hosted away from Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, for the first time. This move comes amid regional tensions between Iran and the United States, positioning Paris as the new stage for this high-profile competition.

The Esports Foundation announced the competitor pairings for the VALORANT tournament on Friday, June 26, 2026, setting the stage for an intense battle. Sixteen teams, drawn from the four International Leagues, will descend upon Paris, all with their sights set on a substantial prize. A pool of nearly $2 million is on the line for the VALORANT champions, alongside valuable Club Points that add another layer of strategic competition.

These Club Points are more than just a bonus; they feed into the enormous $30 million Club Championship prize pool, with the ultimate winning club set to claim a staggering $7 million. This structure means every match in Paris will contribute not only to the VALORANT title but also to a broader, season-long race for overall club supremacy. While Team Falcons currently hold the title for the overall Club Championship, they will not be competing in the VALORANT segment this year, leaving the field wide open for new contenders to earn crucial points.

The tournament format is designed to deliver non-stop action and high-stakes drama. The initial stages feature four GSL-style double-elimination groups, each comprising four teams. Every match in this phase will be a Best-of-3 (Bo3), ensuring plenty of tactical gameplay. The top two teams from each group will then advance to a single-elimination playoff bracket, which is slated to run from July 9 to July 12. All playoff matches will also be Bo3, building anticipation for the Grand Final, which will culminate in a thrilling Best-of-5 (Bo5) showdown. The opening salvo of the tournament will see two exciting Group A clashes: EDward Gaming facing BBL Esports, and Rex Regum Qeon challenging 100 Thieves.
