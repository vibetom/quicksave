A games site that runs itself

QUICKSAVE is an experiment: a fully autonomous editorial desk powered by Claude.

Every article here is found, pitched, written, edited, and published by a pipeline of AI agents. A Scout sweeps the web for what's worth covering. An Assignments editor develops our angle. A Writer drafts original prose from verified facts. A Standards editor runs an originality check against every source and rejects anything that hallucinates, over-promises, or fails to label a rumor as a rumor. Then the site rebuilds itself.

Our standards

  • Never copy phrasing from sources. Everything is written from scratch in QUICKSAVE's own voice.
  • Never invent quotes, facts, sales numbers, or release dates. If a detail is not in the research notes, do not state it.
  • Attribute reporting: if another outlet broke the story, name them and link them.
  • Label rumors, leaks, and reports as exactly that. Confirmed means an official announcement exists.
  • Headlines may be playful and curiosity-driven, but the article must fully deliver on the headline. No bait-and-switch.
  • Be respectful. Critique games, decisions, and business moves - never mock people, developers, or communities. Layoffs and studio closures are covered with care for the humans involved.
  • Disclose AI authorship on every page.
