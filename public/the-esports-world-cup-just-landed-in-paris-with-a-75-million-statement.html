The Esports World Cup Just Landed in Paris with a $75 Million Statement

Beyond the record-breaking prize pool, the EWC 2026 is expanding its global reach and game roster, aiming to redefine what a world championship can be.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 06, 2026 · 21:53 UTC 4 MIN

The Esports World Cup 2026 has officially opened its doors in Paris, France, marking a significant shift for the global competition. Running from July 6 to August 23, the event is taking over the Paris Expo Porte de Versailles, relocating from its previous home in Saudi Arabia.

This year's EWC is not just a change of scenery; it's a statement of ambition, anchored by a record-shattering $75 million total prize pool. A hefty $30 million of that sum is earmarked specifically for the Club Championship, with the top organization potentially taking home $7 million. This substantial investment has drawn in more than two thousand players from two hundred clubs, representing a hundred different countries, all vying for a piece of the unprecedented winnings.

The tournament's competitive landscape is equally expansive, featuring 25 tournaments across 24 different games. Fans can expect to see major titles, including League of Legends, Counter-Strike 2, VALORANT, PUBG, Apex Legends, and Dota 2. The roster also welcomes new disciplines, including Trackmania and Fortnite in Reload mode, a result of a three-year agreement with Epic Games. Notably, mobile esports are gaining serious traction, with titles such as Honor of Kings, PUBG Mobile, and Mobile Legends: Bang Bang offering significant prize pools, and Mobile Legends: Bang Bang even hosting a Women's Invitational and a Mid-Season Cup. Meanwhile, StarCraft 2 and Rennsport have been removed from the lineup.

Beyond the intense competition, the EWC is also cultivating a broader appeal. The opening ceremony, scheduled for July 8 at La Seine Musicale, will feature performances by artists like DJ Snake, Aya Nakamura, and Theodora. Throughout the event, fan fest activations and creator content are planned, alongside other on-site experiences designed to engage audiences beyond the core competitive viewership.

The Esports World Cup is organized by the Esports World Cup Foundation (EWCF), an organization structured as a non-profit and backed by Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund. However, the day-to-day operations for running the various competitions are handled by ESL FACEIT Group. Several headline games are already underway this July. The Dota 2 EWC is scheduled from July 6-18, featuring 24 clubs competing for a $2 million prize pool. The VALORANT EWC follows from July 9-12, bringing together 16 clubs also vying for a $2 million prize. Finally, the League of Legends EWC will run from July 13-19, with another 16 clubs competing for a $2 million prize pool.
