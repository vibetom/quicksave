Riot's New Fighting Game Finally Has a Name, and You Can Try it Sooner Than You Think

After years as 'Project L,' Riot's tag-team brawler 2XKO gets a title, fresh gameplay footage, and an early 2026 release window, with playtests coming up.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 14, 2026 · 21:29 UTC 2 MIN

After living in the shadows as "Project L" for what feels like an eternity, Riot Games has officially pulled back the curtain on their highly anticipated fighting game, giving it a proper name: 2XKO. This reveal isn't just a fresh title; it's a declaration of a distinct vision for the competitive genre, confirming a focused 2v2 tag-team experience that’s already drawing buzz. According to Polygon, this announcement marks a significant step for the project.

Alongside the official title, Riot also dropped a new gameplay trailer, offering a deeper look into the brawler's core mechanics. The footage puts the 2v2 tag-team format front and center, showcasing a dynamic interplay between champions from the expansive League of Legends universe. We caught glimpses of familiar faces like Ahri, Darius, Ekko, and Yasuo, all engaging in fluid, fast-paced combat. IGN reports that the trailer highlights both the visual polish and the strategic depth promised by the tag-team system.

The studio has confirmed that 2XKO is set to launch on PC, PlayStation 5, and Xbox Series X/S, with a release window pegged for 2026, as PCGamesN detailed. This multi-platform approach signals Riot’s intent to cast a wide net, inviting a broad audience into the fray, from seasoned fighting game enthusiasts to curious newcomers.

While 2026 might feel a little distant, Riot isn't leaving players in the lurch. The developer announced plans to offer early access opportunities, allowing fans to get their hands on the game well before its official release. Players can look forward to trying out 2XKO at EVO 2025, a major fighting game event, and through various playtests scheduled to roll out throughout the year. This hands-on strategy, as reported by Polygon, suggests a commitment to gathering player feedback and refining the experience before its full debut, making the wait a bit more bearable.
