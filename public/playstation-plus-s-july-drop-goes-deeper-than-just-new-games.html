PlayStation Plus's July Drop Goes Deeper Than Just New Games

Beyond *Avatar* and *Rise of the Ronin*, PS Plus is subtly changing how and when it delivers games, including a welcome increase to the long-neglected Classics tier.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 15, 2026 · 18:36 UTC 3 MIN

PlayStation Plus subscribers are in for a significant refresh this July, with nine new titles joining the Game Catalog before the month concludes, according to ComicBook.com. While headliners like Avatar: Frontiers of Pandora for PS5 and Rise of the Ronin also for PS5 are certainly attention-grabbing additions, as reported by the PlayStation Blog, the real story goes beyond just the games themselves.

This month marks a notable shift in how new content arrives for Extra and Premium tier members. Instead of a single, large drop, Sony is moving towards a more consistent, staggered release of games throughout the month, a change highlighted by Push Square. This new cadence could mean a steadier flow of fresh experiences, keeping the catalog feeling active and dynamic for subscribers.

Perhaps even more impactful for long-time PlayStation fans is the boost to the Classics tier. July sees the addition of two titles: Psi-Ops: The Mindgate Conspiracy and Indigo Prophecy, both available on PS5 and PS4, as confirmed by IGN. This is a significant departure from the pattern of the past year, where Sony typically added only one Classic title per month.

This limited Classic selection had previously drawn "some pushback" from PS Plus Premium subscribers, according to GameSpot. The decision to double down on retro offerings suggests Sony is listening to that feedback, acknowledging the value players place on access to older gems. It's a move that could significantly enhance the appeal of the higher subscription tiers.

Taken together, July's PlayStation Plus update feels like more than just a new list of games. It suggests a strategic evolution in how Sony curates and delivers content to its subscribers. The increased frequency of releases and the overdue expansion of the Classics catalog hint at a more responsive and continuous value proposition, aiming to provide more for players to dive into throughout the month.
