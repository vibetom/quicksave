VALORANT's New Map Just Dropped. Literally.

A new map with a game-changing gimmick, a focused new mode, and fresh cosmetics are coming to VALORANT, promising to reshape how you play and compete.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 22, 2026 · 22:21 UTC 3 MIN

Riot Games is preparing to inject a significant dose of fresh strategy into VALORANT with a new map, a new game mode, and a striking cosmetic collection, all set to arrive on June 24th with Season 2026 Act 4. The grand reveal took place during the Masters London Grand Finals at the Copper Box Arena, where Leviatán claimed victory over Paper Rex.

Leading the charge is "Summit," the 13th map to join the VALORANT rotation. The map, inspired by agent Sage and set within a Radiant training academy nestled in the mountains of China, brings a literal game-changer to its standard three-lane, two-site layout. Summit introduces three unique droppable walls—one positioned at Site A, one at Site B, and a third in the Mid area. These aren't just decorative; shooting a dedicated node will collapse them, permanently altering sightlines and rotation paths for the remainder of the round. This dynamic element promises to introduce a whole new layer of tactical depth and on-the-fly decision-making for players.

Recognizing the steep learning curve a new map can present, Riot Games is offering a helping hand. For the first two weeks post-launch, players will experience a 50% reduction in RR loss for defeats on Summit, while wins will grant a 100% RR bonus. Additionally, a dedicated "Summit Only" Swiftplay queue will be available for seven days, giving players a casual environment to explore and master the map's intricacies before diving headfirst into competitive play.

Beyond Summit, a permanent new game mode called "Retake" will also launch. This 3v3 mode hones in on clutch scenarios, starting each round with the Spike already planted. Teams will alternate between defending the planted Spike and attempting to defuse it, with the first team to secure five round wins taking the match. It's a focused arena designed to sharpen those critical post-plant skills.

Finally, for those who love to customize their arsenal, the new "Blackspyre Collection" is on its way. This collection blends sci-fi aesthetics with mystical influences, offering skins for the Phantom, Sheriff, Spectre, Ares, and a brand-new melee weapon called the Divide. Players can also look forward to a new Battle Pass, priced at 1,000 VP, which includes rewards such as the spectral Sky Reaper Ghost cosmetic, a player card featuring the Heal Up Squad, and the fun Blep spray, among other offerings. All this new content is poised to keep VALORANT's competitive landscape vibrant and visually fresh.
