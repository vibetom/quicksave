EWC 2026 Takes Flight: Saudi Arabian Airlines Named Official Carrier Amidst Record Viewership

A new partnership with Saudia and the unveiling of an EWC-branded aircraft mark a significant expansion for the Esports World Cup, which is already smashing viewership records in its second week.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 21, 2026 · 18:48 UTC 4 MIN

The Esports World Cup (EWC) 2026 is currently underway, and its global presence just got a major boost. Today, July 21, 2026, the Esports Foundation and Saudia, the national airline of Saudi Arabia, announced an expanded collaboration, officially designating Saudia as the Official Airline for the EWC 2026.

This collaboration arrives as the EWC hosts its inaugural international edition in Paris, France. To celebrate this milestone, Saudia unveiled the world's first commercial aircraft entirely dedicated to an esports event. This plane, emblazoned with EWC branding, was revealed at an official ceremony held at Saudia Technic in Jeddah, Saudi Arabia. It's set to shuttle travelers between Riyadh and Paris for the duration of the EWC tournament, and will continue to traverse Saudia's international network for the next two years.

The EWC 2026, which kicked off on July 6 and runs until August 23 in Paris, is an ambitious spectacle. It brings together more than 2,000 players from over 100 countries, representing 200 clubs, all vying for a cut of a staggering $75 million+ prize pool. Competitions span 24 different games across 25 tournaments, creating a vibrant hub of competitive gaming.

The tournament's second week, from July 13-19, saw viewership soar, generating over 47.8 million Hours Watched. This figure represents double the engagement from the opening week and a notable 34.4% increase compared to the second week of EWC 2025. Leading this viewership surge was League of Legends, which hit a peak of over 1.48 million viewers – the highest for any EWC event this year.

The League of Legends EWC 2026 title ultimately went to Dplus KIA, who delivered a dominant 3-0 victory over Karmine Corp in the Grand Finals. Dplus KIA's bot laner, Sin 'Smash' Guem-jae, was named MVP, securing an additional $25,000 prize.

This remarkable growth in viewership, paired with a high-profile international partnership, underscores the Esports World Cup's expanding influence. It serves as a clear indicator of esports' burgeoning mainstream appeal and its continued commercial legitimization on a global stage.
