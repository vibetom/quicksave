Sony's Next Move: What PS Portal 2 and PS6 Leaks Mean For Your Gaming Future

Rumors are swirling about an OLED-equipped PlayStation Portal Pro and a beastly PlayStation 6. Here's a look at what players can expect from Sony's potential hardware roadmap.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 20, 2026 · 11:21 UTC 4 MIN

Sony appears to be laying the groundwork for substantial hardware enhancements across its gaming ecosystem, with persistent whispers of an upgraded PlayStation Portal and an ambitious PlayStation 6. These reported plans suggest a future where both remote play and high-fidelity console gaming receive significant attention.

First up, the PlayStation Portal, which defied early skepticism to become a commercial hit, repeatedly selling out. Now, new leaks, as reported by NoobFeed, hint at a refreshed model, possibly dubbed the PlayStation Portal Pro or PlayStation Portal 2. This rumored device is said to feature an OLED display, a substantial upgrade from the current 1080p LCD screen. An OLED panel would bring deeper blacks, superior contrast, and more accurate colors, potentially resolving the original's lack of HDR support. We hear an improved refresh rate, possibly 120Hz, is also on the cards, according to NoobFeed.

Beyond the visual upgrade, sources familiar with Sony's internal plans suggest several other significant improvements. The new Portal might support direct streaming via the PlayStation Cloud service, potentially removing the need for a PlayStation 5 console to initiate streams. Further rumored enhancements include better Wi-Fi connectivity, extended battery life, and even Bluetooth music support. The prototype is reportedly sleeker, with thinner bezels and improved ergonomics, akin to the matte finish of the DualSense Edge. An upgraded ARM processor for higher-quality video decoding (1080p 60FPS) and reduced input lag is also rumored. NoobFeed indicates a potential release for this OLED Portal model as late as 2026.

Looking further ahead, the PlayStation 6 is also generating considerable buzz, even though Sony has yet to make any official announcements as of July 2026, according to Centraljersey.com. Leakers point to a 2027 target release date for the PS6, though some reports suggest it could extend into 2028 or even 2029. The rumored specifications are ambitious: a Zen 6 CPU and RDNA 5 GPU. Centraljersey.com suggests these specs could deliver roughly triple the rasterization performance and a staggering 6 to 12 times the ray-tracing capabilities compared to the current PS5. The PS6 is also expected to incorporate AI upscaling through PSSR 2.0, aiming for consistent 4K resolution at 120 frames per second with full ray tracing.

This all paints a picture of a console designed for a "genuine generational leap," moving far beyond a mid-cycle refresh. The reported bill of materials for the PS6 is close to $1,000, hinting that a premium launch price could be on the horizon. Adding another layer to Sony's portable strategy, leaks also suggest a dedicated native-gaming handheld might launch around the same time as the PS6, though this remains unconfirmed, Centraljersey.com reports.

From a souped-up streaming handheld to a console aiming for unprecedented graphical fidelity, these rumors signal Sony's intent to push the boundaries across its hardware offerings, potentially delivering a significantly enhanced gaming experience to players in the coming years.
