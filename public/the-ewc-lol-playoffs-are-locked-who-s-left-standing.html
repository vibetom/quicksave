The EWC LoL Playoffs Are Locked: Who's Left Standing?

After a lightning-fast Group Stage, eight teams are ready to fight for the $2M prize pool in a brutal single-elimination bracket.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 16, 2026 · 21:30 UTC 2 MIN

The dust is settling in the Esports World Cup 2026 League of Legends tournament as the Group Stage wraps up today, July 16. This swift conclusion means the field has been sharply cut, with eight teams now poised to enter a high-stakes, single-elimination Playoffs bracket.

The entire League of Legends event at the EWC is built for speed, unfolding over a mere five days from July 16 to July 20. This compressed timeline leaves little room for error, transforming every match into a critical moment for the 16 qualified teams representing six distinct regions.

The unforgiving nature of the Group Stage has already claimed its first victims. Four teams have been sent packing after losing their initial two matches: Furia from Group A, Team Secret and Movistar KOI from Group B, and Lyon from Group D. Their early exits underscore the intense pressure and quick turnaround that defines this tournament.

Today's remaining Group Stage games are the final crucible, determining which top two teams from each group will secure their spot in the Playoffs. Once the bracket is set, the path to victory narrows considerably with a single-elimination format, turning every subsequent game into a do-or-die scenario.

These surviving eight teams aren't just battling for bragging rights; they're competing for a significant share of a $2 million prize pool. Beyond the immediate financial reward, valuable Club Championship points are also on the line, adding another layer of strategic importance to every play. As the tournament sprints toward its climax, the stakes couldn't be higher for the teams vying to be crowned champions of the Esports World Cup.
