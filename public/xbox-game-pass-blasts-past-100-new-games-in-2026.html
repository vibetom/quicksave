Xbox Game Pass Blasts Past 100 New Games in 2026

June additions, including several day-one launches, push the total number of new titles beyond triple digits, solidifying the service's value proposition.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 16, 2026 · 13:11 UTC 3 MIN

Xbox Game Pass continues its aggressive expansion in 2026, officially surpassing 100 new titles added to the service this year. The latest surge includes a trio of day-one launches on June 11, injecting immediate fresh content for subscribers.

The new games arriving on day one were the vibrant Beastro, the quirky Frog Sqwad, and the expansive Starseeker: Astroneer Expeditions. These additions proved to be a significant milestone, with Starseeker and Frog Sqwad specifically marking the 100th and 101st games, respectively, to join the Game Pass library in 2026, according to GameRant.

June's influx isn't just about day-one drops; the month has already seen a diverse collection of titles enhance the catalog. Subscribers gained access to Final Fantasy VI and Jurassic World Evolution 3 on June 2. Herdling, and Total Chaos, also became available for Game Pass Premium members on June 4. The additions continued with Solarpunk, Undisputed, and Where Winds Meet on June 8, and the critically acclaimed Persona 5 Royal on June 9, as reported by Eurogamer.net.

The momentum isn't slowing down. Looking ahead, Denshattack! is slated to arrive on June 17, and Abyssus will join the service on June 26, according to the Xbox Game Pass Reddit community. Additionally, the 3D action platformer Junkster, which includes building mechanics, is also confirmed to be coming to Game Pass, as announced by Xbox Wire.

While new adventures beckon, the rotating nature of the Game Pass library means some titles are making their exit. As of June 15, subscribers will wave goodbye to a selection of games, including the dinosaur park management sequel Jurassic World Evolution 2, the unique action-adventure Lost in Random: The Eternal Die, the beat 'em up Scott Pilgrim vs. The World, the sci-fi action title Warhammer 40K Space Marine: Master Crafted Edition, and the retro brawler Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles: Shredder's Revenge, Eurogamer.net noted. It's a reminder that while the volume of new games is impressive, the window for some experiences is finite, encouraging players to dive in before they're gone.
