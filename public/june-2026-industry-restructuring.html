After the Showcase Confetti, a Hard Week for the People Who Make Games

Ubisoft confirms a major restructuring while reports point to deep cuts at Xbox and a shrinking Rainbow Six Siege team. Here's what's confirmed, what's reported, and why it matters beyond the headlines.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 11, 2026 · 09:45 UTC 2 MIN

Summer showcase season is the industry's annual fireworks display. This year, the smoke cleared on some sobering news for the people behind the games.

What's confirmed

Ubisoft is undergoing another significant restructuring. Roughly 380 employees have been laid off or reassigned and two studios are closing, with reporting indicating the company's restructuring efforts are continuing and could affect hundreds more. For a publisher that has spent the past two years in near-constant reorganization, it's a heavy follow-up to a week that also included splashy announcements like Rayman Legends Retold.

What's reported — and worth treating carefully

At Xbox, the picture is murkier but concerning. According to The Verge, layoffs at Xbox could lead to the closure of a studio or changes to the Xbox studios lineup, and follow-up reporting suggests an entire studio might be caught up in the cuts. Separately, Insider Gaming reports the Rainbow Six Siege team has shrunk by at least 120 developers — about 12% of the team — in recent weeks. None of this has been fully detailed by Microsoft or Ubisoft, so specifics should be read as reporting, not announcement.

The whiplash is the story

What makes this week feel particularly stark is the timing. These reports landed days after the Xbox Games Showcase and Summer Game Fest filled feeds with trailers and applause. The industry's promotional calendar and its business reality are running on different tracks, and June 2026 put them side by side in unusually high contrast.

Behind every one of these numbers is a person who shipped games people love, now job hunting in the toughest games labor market in recent memory. If there's a takeaway for players, it's a simple one: the names in the credits matter, and the health of the industry isn't measured in trailer view counts.

We'll update as companies make official statements.

✓ Standards note: Ubisoft restructuring widely reported; Xbox details attributed to The Verge and Insider Gaming as unconfirmed reports.
