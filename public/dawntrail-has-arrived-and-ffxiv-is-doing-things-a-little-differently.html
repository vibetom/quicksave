Dawntrail Has Arrived, And FFXIV Is Doing Things A Little Differently This Time

Early access for Final Fantasy XIV's new expansion is here, bringing with it a fresh direction and some surprisingly player-friendly moves.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 27, 2026 · 15:48 UTC 4 MIN

The gates to a new adventure in Final Fantasy XIV have officially swung open, with early access for pre-ordering players of the highly anticipated Dawntrail expansion commencing on Friday, June 28, 2024, at 2:00 AM PDT. The full release is slated for Tuesday, July 2, across PC, Apple computers, and all PlayStation and Xbox current-generation consoles, as well as the prior-generation PlayStation 4. This fifth expansion arrives not just with fresh content, but with a confident, player-centric approach that's a refreshing change of pace.

Dawntrail brings us to a new continent, Tural, promising a vibrant change of scenery. At the helm are familiar and trusted figures: Naoki Yoshida serves as both director and producer, maintaining the steady hand that has guided the game's resurgence. The soundtrack, a cornerstone of the FFXIV experience, is once again composed by Masayoshi Soken, ensuring the series' signature musical quality continues.

Square Enix has rolled out the red carpet for Dawntrail with a marketing blitz that's as creative as it is ambitious. Fans around the globe have seen the world of Hydaelyn bleed into our own, with physical installations like a replica "Aetheryte" appearing at King's Cross in London. Murals have popped up in cities including Los Angeles, Melbourne, and Sydney, acting as vibrant portals to the new expansion. The team also launched TourTural.com, a parody tourism website featuring actor Rhys Darby as the "Interim Deputy Director of 'Turalism'" in a series of amusing commercials. It's a playful, confident campaign that speaks to the game's established personality.

Perhaps the most telling sign of Square Enix's player-first mentality comes directly from Director Naoki Yoshida himself. He made it clear that the July 2 full launch date was chosen specifically to give players a full week to enjoy the Elden Ring DLC before diving into Dawntrail. This level of consideration for player time, especially when a major competitor is releasing content, is a rare and welcome gesture.

Pre-ordering Dawntrail came with its own set of compelling incentives. Beyond early access, players received a Wind-up Zidane minion and Azeyma's Earrings, the latter designed to help adventurers reach level 90 before embarking on their new journey. For the truly dedicated, the Collector's Edition offers even more nostalgic treats: a Wind-up Garnet minion and the Ark mount, both drawing inspiration from Final Fantasy IX, alongside a chocobo brush to outfit the new Pictomancer job. It's a blend of utility and fan service that sweetens the deal for veteran and new players alike.

First announced with launch and pre-order details at PAX East 2024, Dawntrail feels like a new chapter, not just for the story it tells, but for how it's being presented to its community. It's a testament to a development team that understands its audience, delivering not just an expansion, but an experience tailored with care.
