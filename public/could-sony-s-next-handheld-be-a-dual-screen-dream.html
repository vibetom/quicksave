Could Sony's Next Handheld Be a Dual-Screen Dream?

A new patent filing for a portable gaming device with two screens hints at a potential successor to the PlayStation Portal or an entirely new PlayStation handheld experience.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 29, 2026 · 12:45 UTC 4 MIN

Sony Interactive Entertainment might be gearing up to expand its portable gaming ambitions, as a recent patent filing suggests the company is exploring a device with dual screens. This could mark an intriguing evolution for the PlayStation Portal, or perhaps signal a completely new direction for PlayStation handhelds.

According to a report by VGC, a patent submitted by Sony Interactive Entertainment details a "portable gaming device" equipped with "at least two distinct screens." These screens could operate independently, displaying different information, or seamlessly combine to create one larger, more expansive display. Imagine a digital book, but for games, or a mobile command center with a map on one screen and gameplay on the other.

The patent illustrations, as described by VGC, paint a picture of a device that shares some DNA with the original PlayStation Portal, which launched in November 2023 primarily as a remote play companion for the PS5. However, this new concept introduces a crucial difference: a hinge. This suggests a clamshell design, allowing the device to fold shut, similar to how many popular dual-screen devices operate. Furthermore, the patent even hints at the possibility of touchscreens embedded directly into the controller grips themselves, potentially opening up a host of new input methods and interactive possibilities.

While the original PlayStation Portal carved out its niche as a dedicated remote play streamer, some players yearned for a device with more standalone functionality. This new patent could be Sony's answer, potentially hinting at a "PlayStation Portal 2" that offers a more versatile experience, perhaps even extending beyond just streaming from a PS5. The very idea of two screens suggests a departure from the single-purpose design of its predecessor, pushing into territory that could offer more robust standalone play or significantly enhance the remote play experience.

It's important to remember that patent filings are often conceptual blueprints, not guaranteed products. Companies frequently patent ideas that never see the light of day. However, this particular filing indicates that Sony is actively thinking about and investing in the future of portable gaming hardware. The exploration of a dual-screen, clamshell design points to a desire to innovate and perhaps even take cues from successful hybrid gaming systems that allow for both handheld and docked play. The possibilities for how a device with two adaptable screens could change how we interact with PlayStation games on the go are certainly exciting to consider.
