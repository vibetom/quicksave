New Metal Slug Ditches Side-Scrolling For Roguelike Runs, Bleach Mobile Beta Now Open

Metal Slug Rush is out globally, taking the classic series in a new direction for its 30th anniversary, while Bleach fans can sign up for the Mirrors High closed beta.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 11, 2026 · 15:29 UTC 2 MIN

Two major mobile gaming announcements have just landed, showcasing how beloved franchises are adapting their formulas for new platforms. SNK and Ring Games have released Metal Slug Rush to players across the globe, now available on both Android and iOS devices. This launch coincides with the franchise's 30th anniversary celebrations, presenting a fresh perspective on its renowned action.

Metal Slug Rush transforms the venerable series into a roguelike survival experience. Moving away from the classic side-scrolling mechanics, players are instead immersed in relentless waves of adversaries, clashing with the rebel army, pirates, and even the Mars people. Each session grants experience, which can then be utilized to unlock new upgrades and randomized combat perks, allowing for dynamic progression with every attempt.

Despite this shift in core gameplay, Metal Slug Rush preserves many of the series' hallmarks. Fans will immediately recognize the distinct pixel art, the memorable announcer voice lines, and familiar elements such as POW rescues and the character Hayakutaro Ichimonji. The game is available as a free-to-play download, supported by optional in-game purchases and equipment gacha systems.

Meanwhile, adherents of the Bleach universe have a fresh opportunity to engage with an upcoming mobile title. Bandai Namco has initiated recruitment for the Bleach Mirrors High closed beta. Prospective players can submit their applications for a chance to participate in the beta, with the sign-up window closing on July 13, offering an early look before its broader release.
