Nintendo's European Switch Plans Signal A Bigger Shift

Say goodbye to the original Switch in Europe by 2027, but get ready for user-replaceable batteries on the Switch 2, a move that could change everything.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 07, 2026 · 21:51 UTC 3 MIN

Nintendo of Europe is charting a clear course for its console lineup, signaling the end of an era for the current Switch family while simultaneously introducing a significant, player-focused upgrade to its successor. By mid-February 2027, the company will cease selling the original Nintendo Switch, Switch Lite, and Switch OLED Model to European retailers and through its own Nintendo Store.

This impending farewell to the current generation is coupled with a welcome announcement for the future: Switch 2 consoles arriving in Autumn 2026 will feature user-replaceable batteries. New Joy-Con 2 controllers, also equipped with replaceable power sources, are slated for release even sooner, beginning in Summer 2026.

While the revised Switch 2 console with a replaceable battery will see a minor adjustment in specifications – a slightly smaller 5172mAh battery compared to the current 5220mAh, and a marginal increase in weight from 401g to 411g (or 534g to 548g with Joy-Con 2s attached) – the practical benefits for players are substantial. The ability to easily swap out a fading battery can significantly extend the usable life of a console, addressing a common pain point for device longevity.

For those still enjoying their current hardware, Nintendo of Europe has confirmed that online services like Nintendo Switch Online will continue for the foreseeable future. However, the discontinuation of current-generation hardware clearly nudges future buyers towards the Switch 2.

Industry observers like Dr. Serkan Toto, CEO of Kantan Games, suggest this European strategy offers a glimpse into Nintendo's global plans. Toto believes the company aims to encourage upgrades to the Switch 2, adapt to evolving EU regulations concerning replaceable batteries, and streamline its product catalog and supply chain. This approach could see the original Switch models discontinued in other regions beginning in late 2027 or early 2028, making Europe a testing ground for a wider strategic pivot.
