The Esports World Cup Hits High Notes in Paris: What's Driving Its Record Growth?

Mid-tournament in Paris, the EWC 2026 is seeing massive viewership spikes and unprecedented global reach, signaling a new era for international esports.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 23, 2026 · 18:43 UTC 4 MIN

The Esports World Cup 2026 is currently unfolding in Paris, a vibrant new stage for an event that has previously called Riyadh home. Since its grand opening on July 8, the tournament has been a beacon of global competition, set to run until August 23 at the expansive Paris Expo Porte de Versailles. This international debut marks a significant milestone, drawing a truly worldwide contingent to the French capital.

The scale of this year's World Cup is staggering. More than 2,000 elite players, representing 200 leading esports clubs, have converged from over 100 countries. They're all vying for supremacy across 25 distinct tournaments, featuring 24 of the most popular games on the planet. From tactical team battles to one-on-one showdowns, the breadth of competition is designed to capture every corner of the esports landscape.

Driving this intense competition is an equally impressive reward: a record-shattering $75 million prize pool. This monumental sum underscores the EWC's ambition and its growing stature within the global esports ecosystem, ensuring that the stakes are higher than ever for every club and player involved.

Viewership figures reflect this soaring interest. During its second week, from July 13 through July 19, the EWC 2026 captivated audiences for over 47.8 million collective hours, according to Esports Charts. This isn't just a healthy number; it's double the viewership recorded during the event's opening week and represents a substantial 34.4% increase compared to the second week of EWC 2025. Such a jump signals a rapidly expanding and deeply engaged audience, tuning in from around the globe to witness the action.

As the tournament moves through its third week, from July 20 to July 26, the intensity shows no signs of waning. This crucial period features decisive matches across a lineup of major titles, including the popular arena brawler Mobile Legends: Bang Bang, the battle royale phenomenon PUBG, the fighting game Street Fighter 6, the strategic auto-battler Teamfight Tactics, and the football simulation EA FC26. With so many pivotal contests on the schedule, the competitive energy remains high, promising even more thrilling moments for the millions of viewers following along.
