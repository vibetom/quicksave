Overwatch and Warzone Hit the Big Stage: What You Need to Know About EWC Week 4

The Esports World Cup 2026 is heating up as two massive titles, Overwatch and Call of Duty: Warzone, kick off their high-stakes tournaments in Paris with million-dollar prize pools.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 24, 2026 · 12:53 UTC 3 MIN

The Esports World Cup 2026 has officially entered its fourth week, bringing with it a fresh wave of competitive action. This week, the global tournament welcomes two powerhouse titles, Overwatch and Call of Duty: Warzone, to the main stage. All the intense action is unfolding at the Paris Expo Porte de Versailles, drawing top-tier talent from around the globe.

For Overwatch, Week 4 marks a significant moment with the Overwatch Champions Series 2026 Midseason Championship. Running from July 29 to August 2, this tournament is set to crown a new champion and distribute a substantial $1,000,000 prize pool, with the top team securing a cool $400,000. According to Insider Gaming, this event arrives as Overwatch esports experiences a 'resurgent year,' shaking off what was described as a period of 'existential dread.'

The field for the Overwatch Champions Series is stacked with talent. Winners from various regional splits, including organizations like Team Secret, Virtus.pro, Dallas Fuel, and Weibo Gaming, alongside Crazy Racoon, Zeta Division, and VARREL, are all battling for the title. South Korea has historically dominated the Overwatch competitive scene, a trend underscored by Team Falcons' victory in the 2025 Midseason Championship, and this year's competition looks poised to continue that legacy.

Meanwhile, Call of Duty: Warzone enters the fray with its Resurgence Series 2026 Championship. This high-octane tournament will take place from July 29 to August 1, featuring its own $1,000,000 prize pool. The ultimate victors in Warzone will claim $250,000, a massive reward for navigating the chaotic battlegrounds. This championship is the culmination of what's been dubbed the 'new-look Call of Duty circuit,' now officially known as the Resurgence Series.

Teams earned their spots in the Warzone championship through various paths. Nine teams distinguished themselves with strong performances at LAN events, specifically DreamHack Birmingham and DreamHack Atlanta. Another 22 teams secured their berths through a rigorous series of online qualifiers. The returning champions from the 2025 Call of Duty: Warzone event, Twisted Minds, will undoubtedly be looking to defend their title against a strong field of contenders.
