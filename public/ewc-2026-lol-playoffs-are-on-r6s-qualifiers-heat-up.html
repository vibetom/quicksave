EWC 2026: LoL Playoffs Are ON, R6S Qualifiers Heat Up

As the Esports World Cup 2026 rages in Paris, League of Legends enters its single-elimination playoffs and All Gamers makes history in Rainbow Six Siege.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 17, 2026 · 21:13 UTC 3 MIN

The Esports World Cup 2026, unfolding in Paris, France, from July 6 to August 23, is currently a crucible of high-stakes competition. Featuring 25 tournaments across 24 distinct games, this global event sees over two thousand competitors representing more than two hundred clubs and a hundred-plus nations. They are all contending for a monumental combined prize pool of $75 million, with an additional $30 million dedicated to the Club Championship.

Today, July 17, marks the intense kickoff of the League of Legends EWC tournament playoffs. After a challenging Group Stage that saw eight teams exit the competition, the remaining eight contenders are now plunged into a single-elimination knockout bracket. The Quarterfinals are slated for today, setting the stage for the Semifinals tomorrow, July 18. The tournament will culminate in both the Third Place Match and the Grand Final on Sunday, July 19.

Amidst the initial playoff clashes, Gen.G delivered a remarkable performance, orchestrating a comeback victory against JD Gaming. This decisive win has propelled Gen.G directly into the semifinals, establishing an early benchmark for the competitive fire shaping the rest of the bracket.

Meanwhile, in the world of Rainbow Six Siege, All Gamers has secured their spot in the Esports World Cup 2026. Their qualification came after a commanding performance against One Coin, where they clinched the series with map scores of 7-2 on Nighthaven Labs and 8-6 on Fortress. This marks All Gamers' second foray into an international event, following their prior participation in the BLAST R6 Salt Lake City Major.

All Gamers holds a unique distinction as the only Chinese team to achieve a regulation victory over a non-Asian-Pacific roster during an international Rainbow Six Siege competition, specifically against FURIA on Fortress. This record stands despite recent reports indicating the team has faced challenges domestically, including consecutive losses against EDward Gaming in Stage 1, and additional defeats at the hands of KINGZONE eSports and TYLOO.
