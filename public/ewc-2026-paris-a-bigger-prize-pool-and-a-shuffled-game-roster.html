EWC 2026: Paris, A Bigger Prize Pool, And A Shuffled Game Roster

The Esports World Cup has moved to Paris, increased its prize money, and brought back fan-favorites, but not every genre made the cut for this year's competition.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 19, 2026 · 15:30 UTC 3 MIN

The Esports World Cup (EWC) 2026 is currently unfolding in Paris, France, from July 6 to August 23. This year marks a significant relocation for the event, as it is being held outside of Saudi Arabia for the first time. This shift to Paris was a response to security concerns stemming from the 2026 Iran war, which reportedly involved attacks on Saudi infrastructure.

Setting a new financial benchmark, the EWC 2026 features a prize pool of US$75 million, which establishes a new high for the tournament. This figure represents an increase from the US$71.5 million offered in 2025. This substantial sum has drawn over 2,000 players, representing more than 200 clubs and over 100 countries, competing across 25 tournaments featuring 24 distinct game titles.

Familiar headliners are back in the fold, including titans like Counter-Strike 2, Dota 2, League of Legends, and VALORANT. Mobile titles such as Mobile Legends: Bang Bang and PUBG Mobile also make their return, alongside fighting game staples Tekken 8 and Street Fighter 6. Fortnite is present once more, though it features a specific 'Fortnite Reload Elite Series Championship' using the game's Reload mode, part of a multi-year agreement with Epic Games. Newcomers this year include Trackmania (2020), making its EWC debut. Additionally, yearly updated sports and combat titles, such as Call of Duty: Black Ops 7 and EA Sports FC 26, have taken the place of their prior editions.

However, the 2026 lineup sees some notable absences. StarCraft II and Rennsport are not featured, which means the real-time strategy (RTS) genre is entirely missing from this year's competition. This omission has sparked criticism from RTS communities, highlighting a strategic shift in the EWC's game selection.
