Don't Miss These Steam Next Fest Demos Before They're Gone

Thousands of free game previews are available right now, but a few stand out from the crowd with serious player buzz and potential.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 20, 2026 · 10:45 UTC 3 MIN

Steam Next Fest is currently underway, unrolling a digital red carpet for thousands of upcoming indie and AA games, all available to try for free. It’s a truly golden opportunity for players to dip their toes into a vast ocean of potential future hits, from the next big action RPG to a charming crafting adventure.

The festival is a true smorgasbord, with demos spanning an impressive range of genres. We've seen significant attention on action RPGs, intricate Metroidvanias, high-stakes extraction games, challenging roguelikes, pulse-pounding horror titles, and the always-popular Soulslikes. It's like a rapid-fire sampler platter for every gaming palate.

Among the crowd, a few demos are already making serious waves. _Mortal Shell II_, for instance, has already drawn an impressive 250,000 beta downloads, hinting at a strong appetite for its particular brand of challenge. Other titles drawing buzz include _Mistfall Hunter_, _Duskfade_, _Silver Pines_, _Valor Mortis_, _Chained Beasts_, and the unsettlingly named _The Mound: Omen of Cthulhu_. Each offers a distinct flavor of gameplay, from dark fantasy to cosmic horror, and they're all ripe for discovery.

To put the potential impact of these demos into perspective, consider _Solarpunk_. This relaxing crafting game saw over 500,000 players download its preview during an earlier Steam Next Fest, leading to an impressive milestone of over a million wishlists. It's a clear example of how a strong showing during this event can propel a game into the spotlight, building a robust community before launch.

Beyond the action and suspense, there are also unique narrative experiences to be found. Take _Crushed in Time_, a point-and-click adventure that serves as a spin-off from the acclaimed _There Is No Game: Wrong Dimension_. It offers a different kind of engagement, proving that Next Fest truly has something for everyone, no matter your preferred playstyle. Don't let these limited-time previews slip away!
