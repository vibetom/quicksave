Esports World Cup 2026 Heats Up in Paris: What to Watch in Week 3

Beyond the record-breaking $75 million prize pool, the EWC's first outing in Paris is delivering big moments and even bigger viewership numbers.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 22, 2026 · 10:37 UTC 3 MIN

The Esports World Cup (EWC) 2026 has landed in Paris, marking its first venture outside of Saudi Arabia and signaling a significant global expansion for competitive gaming. This monumental event is currently in its third week, with the action unfolding at the Paris Expo Porte de Versailles until August 23.

Week 3 features a quintet of major competitions for esports enthusiasts. Fans can dive into the FC Pro World Championship, the PUBG: BATTLEGROUNDS tournament, the MLBB Mid Season Cup, and Teamfight Tactics, among others. The FC Pro World Championship, which kicked off its League Stage on July 22 with 36 players, will ultimately crown its champion with the newly minted Diogo Jota FC Pro World Championship Trophy, honoring the Liverpool and Portugal forward's passion for EA SPORTS FC and esports. The final weekend of FC Pro will even include a special showmatch with football legend Karim Benzema. Meanwhile, PUBG: BATTLEGROUNDS began its Group Stage on July 21 with 24 teams vying for a spot in the Grand Finals, and the MLBB Mid Season Cup's Group Stage, running from July 22-26, will see 16 teams battle for a spot in Week 4's Knockout Stage.

Driving all this high-stakes competition is a truly unprecedented $75 million prize pool. This record-breaking sum is divided across the Club Championship, individual game championships, and various awards and qualifiers, drawing more than two thousand elite players and two hundred top esports organizations from over 100 countries. These athletes are competing across 25 tournaments spanning 24 diverse game titles.

The tournament’s momentum is building at an impressive rate. According to reports, the second week of the EWC accumulated over 47.8 million hours of viewing time. This figure not only doubled the viewership from the opening week but also represented a 34.4% increase compared to the second week of EWC 2025. League of Legends led the charge in Week 2, achieving a peak of more than 1.48 million concurrent viewers, which was the highest for any EWC event this year. Other titles also saw significant engagement, with the Free Fire tournament reaching a peak of over 804,000 viewers, making it a highly successful event for the game in four years. The MLBB Women's Invitational 2026 also saw strong numbers, peaking at over 565,400 viewers, an increase of 13.8% from last year.

The sheer scale of the event, coupled with its diverse game offerings—ranging from traditional esports titans like League of Legends to mobile powerhouses such as MLBB—underscores a clear strategy. The EWC is aiming to cast a wide net, capturing a broad audience and firmly cementing its status as a premier global spectacle in the world of esports.
