Xbox's CEO Says Gaming Is Unaffordable. Here's How She Plans To Fix It.

Asha Sharma outlined her vision for a more open Xbox platform, aiming to lower barriers for both players and developers in a changing industry landscape.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 23, 2026 · 21:55 UTC 3 MIN

Xbox CEO Asha Sharma recently offered a candid assessment of the current state of gaming, articulating a sentiment many players have felt: the hobby is becoming increasingly "unaffordable." Speaking in an interview with Entertainment Weekly, Sharma pointed to a confluence of rising hardware costs, expanding subscription services, and the ever-growing demand for player attention as key factors contributing to this financial squeeze.

This isn't just an observation; it's a driving force behind Xbox's evolving strategy. Sharma detailed a vision for the Xbox ecosystem that reaches far beyond the traditional console experience. Her goal is to cultivate a more open platform, one designed to welcome a broader spectrum of developers, support a greater diversity of games, and ultimately attract a wider audience of players.

To make good on this promise, Xbox is already rolling out the welcome mat for creators. The company has introduced updates specifically geared to assist developers working with Unreal Engine 5.8. Furthermore, Xbox has released detailed documentation aimed at helping Godot developers bring their games to Xbox on PC, effectively lowering the barrier to entry for independent and smaller studios. These moves illustrate a clear intent to democratize game creation, fostering an environment where innovation can flourish without prohibitive costs.

Sharma's insights were part of a wider conversation in the Entertainment Weekly interview, which also included perspectives from Todd Green, the head of Candy Crush and King. While the discussion touched on various aspects of the gaming industry, the emphasis on making gaming more accessible and affordable stands out as a significant strategic pivot for Xbox, indicating a commitment to adapting to the changing economic realities faced by players worldwide.
