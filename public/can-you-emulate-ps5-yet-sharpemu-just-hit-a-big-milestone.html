Can You Emulate PS5 Yet? SharpEmu Just Hit a Big Milestone

A new PlayStation 5 emulator is showing early signs of life, booting Demon's Souls to character creation and executing native CPU instructions, but don't sell your console just yet.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 24, 2026 · 10:32 UTC 3 MIN

The world of game emulation just took a fascinating leap forward. The PlayStation 5 emulator, SharpEmu, has achieved a notable development milestone: it can now boot the Demon's Souls remake all the way to its character creation screen. This progress, reported by the YouTube channel "Gaming News - July 23," marks a significant step for a console platform that many consider to be in its infancy in the emulation scene.

While players won't be able to sculpt their ideal slayer and delve into Boletaria just yet – the emulator cannot progress beyond this character creation menu – its developers view this as a powerful signal that the foundational systems are starting to click into place. It's like seeing the engine of a highly complex machine sputter to life for the first time; it's not ready to race, but it's clearly on its way.

This early success points to core functionalities within SharpEmu beginning to operate as intended. The emulator is now capable of loading original game executables and directly carrying out native CPU instructions, alongside partially managing PlayStation 5 kernel functions. These are critical building blocks for any ambitious emulation project, confirming that the digital architecture is starting to mimic the real thing.

Beyond Demon's Souls, the team behind SharpEmu isn't resting on its laurels. We're seeing tests with other PlayStation 5 titles, including Dreaming Sara and Void Terrarium. This broader compatibility testing is a good sign, indicating that the developers are looking to build a robust system rather than one tailored to a single game.

However, for those dreaming of playing their full PS5 library on other hardware, patience is still key. The journey from booting a game to full, stable playability is often a long and winding road. According to reports, several major components still need to be designed and implemented before modern PS5 titles can be fully emulated and become truly playable.

So, while a PlayStation 5 emulator capable of running demanding titles is still a distant ambition, this recent SharpEmu milestone is a powerful reminder that the wheels are indeed turning. The path to full PS5 emulation is being paved, one crucial step at a time.
