What's Next for PlayStation? Your June State of Play Recap

From a new Astro Bot adventure to fresh looks at Monster Hunter Wilds and a revamped Until Dawn, here's everything Sony just showed off for PS5 and PSVR2.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 25, 2026 · 11:13 UTC 3 MIN

Sony rolled out a new State of Play presentation on June 24, 2026, offering a look at what's on the horizon for PlayStation 5 and PlayStation VR2 players. The broadcast delivered a steady stream of reveals, blending fresh faces with anticipated updates for existing titles.

Leading the charge was the official unveiling of Astro Bot, a brand new 3D platformer from Team Asobi. Fans can look forward to diving into this new adventure in early 2027, as reported by The Verge.

Capcom also kept the energy high with a fresh trailer for Monster Hunter Wilds, offering an expanded view of its sprawling open world and showcasing new combat mechanics. The presentation then shifted to Firewalk Studios' 5v5 multiplayer shooter, Concord, which received an extended gameplay trailer and a confirmed launch in August 2026, according to The Verge.

Horror enthusiasts have something to anticipate with the announcement of an Until Dawn remake. This refreshed take on the classic is slated for release on PS5 and PC in late 2026. The Verge detailed that the remake will feature enhanced graphics, introduce new camera angles, and include expanded story elements, aiming to breathe new life into the chilling narrative.

The State of Play also provided updates on several previously announced heavy hitters. The Silent Hill 2 Remake now has an early 2027 release window, while new gameplay footage for Marvel's Wolverine was shown, though a release window for the latter remains unannounced. Both updates were highlighted on the PlayStation Blog.

The presentation wasn't just about the major tentpoles; several indie titles also got their moment in the spotlight. Infinity Nikki is set for an early 2027 arrival, and Ballad of Antara is expected in late 2026. These smaller-scale projects round out a robust lineup, ensuring a consistent flow of content for PlayStation gamers well into next year, as noted by the PlayStation Blog.
