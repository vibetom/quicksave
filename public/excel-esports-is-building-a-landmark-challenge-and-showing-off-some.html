Excel Esports is Building a Landmark Challenge (and Showing Off Some Serious Hardware)

The upcoming 'Landmark Battle' brings top spreadsheet pros to a new kind of competition, highlighting how ASUS gear tackles high-pressure workflows.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 24, 2026 · 21:53 UTC 3 MIN

Brace yourselves, number crunchers: a new kind of competition is on the horizon. ASUS and the Microsoft Excel World Championship (MEWC) have joined forces to announce 'Landmark Battle,' a special Excel Esports event that promises to be anything but ordinary.

This isn't just about formulas and functions; it's about pushing the boundaries of competitive spreadsheet work. The event marries high-stakes Excel challenges with themes inspired by famous global landmarks, creating a unique backdrop for top-tier data manipulation.

The 'Landmark Battle' is designed with a clear goal in mind: to showcase the power and portability of ASUS hardware. The event will put ASUS ExpertBook Ultra laptops and ASUS ZenScreen OLED portable monitors through their paces, demonstrating how these tools empower flexible, high-performance workflows, even in the most demanding, real-world-inspired scenarios.

At the heart of the competition are some of the biggest names in Excel Esports. Fans can look forward to seeing the likes of Andrew Ngai, an Australian three-time world champion and a legend in the scene, go head-to-head with the United Kingdom's own Jaq Kennedy. Their expertise will be tested as they navigate the intricate, landmark-themed challenges.

For those eager to witness this unique blend of brainpower and hardware prowess, the livestream is scheduled for July 10th at 5 PM London Time. You can catch all the action live on the official Excel Esports YouTube channel.

This event is more than just another entry in the competitive gaming calendar; it's a testament to the evolving world of esports and the serious processing power required to excel—pun intended—at the highest levels. It’s a compelling argument that powerful, portable tech isn't just for traditional gaming; it's a game-changer for pros across all competitive domains, including the intricate world of spreadsheet showdowns.
