FaZe Clan Locks In: R6S Powerhouse Earns Shot at Esports World Cup Glory

With League of Legends playoffs underway, FaZe Clan secured their spot in the Rainbow Six Siege tournament, highlighting the multi-game excitement brewing at the record-breaking Esports World Cup 2026.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 18, 2026 · 12:10 UTC 3 MIN

The Esports World Cup (EWC) 2026 is currently underway in Paris, France, cementing its status as a landmark event in competitive gaming. Running from July 6 to August 23, 2026, the tournament boasts an unprecedented $75 million prize pool, with an additional $30 million dedicated to the Club Championship. This immense financial incentive underscores the high stakes for every team and player involved across various titles.

While the EWC unfolds, the League of Legends (LoL) segment is already deep in its competitive rhythm. From July 16-20, 2026, 16 qualified teams representing six distinct regions are clashing for their share of a $2 million prize pool. Beyond the immediate winnings, these battles are crucial for accumulating valuable Club Championship points, which feed into the larger, overall EWC standings.

Adding to the multi-title spectacle, FaZe Clan has officially secured its place in the Rainbow Six Siege Esports World Cup 2026. The R6S powerhouse clinched qualification with a decisive 2-0 victory against LOUD, demonstrating a strong rebound after an earlier 0-2 defeat to Team Liquid Alienware on Friday. This win firmly plants FaZe Clan among the elite competitors vying for the R6S title.

Central to FaZe Clan's qualifying performance was the exceptional play of Thiago "Handyy" Ferreira. "Handyy" delivered a standout showing, earning a SiegeGG rating of 1.40. His impact was further highlighted by a KOST of 76, a crucial plant, and two clutch plays that turned the tide for his team in critical moments.

FaZe Clan now prepares for its next challenge against LOS. The outcome of this upcoming match holds significant weight: the victor will bypass the R6S EWC 2026 play-ins entirely, securing a direct entry into Phase 2 of the tournament. This would not only guarantee them a top spot but also cement their position among the top three teams of their region's Stage 1, setting the stage for a deeper run at the EWC 2026 title.
