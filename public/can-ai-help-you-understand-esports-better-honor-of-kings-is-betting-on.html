Can AI Help You Understand Esports Better? Honor of Kings Is Betting On It

A new broadcast system is bringing real-time tactical breakdowns and localized commentary to global audiences, promising to bridge the language gap in esports.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 13, 2026 · 15:56 UTC 3 MIN

The world of esports is as dynamic and global as ever, and nowhere is that more evident than in the current Honor of Kings 2026 Asian Games Esports Qualifiers. Twenty teams are currently battling it out in Malaysia's capital city, Kuala Lumpur, during a competition running from June 13 to June 21, all vying for a coveted spot at the 20th Asian Games in Nagoya, Japan, this September. With China, Malaysia, and Thailand already qualified directly, the stakes are incredibly high.

But beyond the fierce competition, these qualifiers are also showcasing a quiet revolution in esports broadcasting. TiMi Studio Group and Level Infinite have unveiled a new broadcast pipeline that's designed to dismantle a significant language barrier in global esports. This innovative system translates intricate, real-time tactical breakdowns into various languages, making the nuanced plays and strategies accessible to a worldwide audience.

The system first made its debut at the 2025 Anniversary event last October, where its accurate subtitles on platforms like YouTube and TikTok garnered considerable praise. Since then, it's been integrated into significant esports tournaments, including the KPL Championship matches, the Esports World Cup (EWC), and the Challenger Cup, proving its robust capability to deliver complex information clearly and promptly.

Yet, subtitles, while crucial, can't fully capture the electric atmosphere of a live esports match. The IgniteX team is now pushing the boundaries even further, actively developing Text-to-Speech (TTS) AI commentators. The vision is to convert these real-time translations into localized audio, infused with the emotional resonance typically found in human commentary. The ultimate goal is to ensure international fans experience the same heart-pounding adrenaline rush as the home crowd, transforming a viewing experience from passive understanding to active engagement through dynamically voiced, culturally attuned insights.
