Switch 2 Price Hike Incoming, But What About That OLED Model?

Your current Nintendo Switch 2 is getting more expensive next month, while a rumored OLED version is still years away from even starting development.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 14, 2026 · 18:36 UTC 3 MIN

Players eyeing a Nintendo Switch 2 might want to act fast: the console is confirmed to see a price increase starting in September. This immediate change means anyone planning a purchase in the near future will find themselves paying more.

While the current model adjusts its price, the rumor mill is spinning with talk of a potential future OLED variant for the Switch 2. According to a report from South Korean site ZDNet Korea, Nintendo is allegedly considering producing an OLED version of the console at some point down the line. ZDNet reporter Lee Ki-jong further claims that Samsung could be tapped to supply the new OLED panels for this rumored device.

Should this rumored Switch 2 OLED ever materialize, it would reportedly offer a significant visual upgrade. The display is said to boost resolution from the original Switch OLED's HD (1280x720) to a sharper FHD (1920x1080). This would bring a crisper image to the handheld experience, should Nintendo decide to pursue the concept.

However, it's important for players to temper expectations. The report indicates that product development for such an OLED model might not even begin until the close of 2026, and only "if the release is decided." This timeline places any potential Switch 2 OLED release many years into the future, making it a distant prospect rather than an imminent arrival.

This landscape of rising prices and distant hardware rumors isn't unique to Nintendo. The industry at large is grappling with component shortages, particularly for crucial parts like RAM and SSDs, driven in part by high demand from AI data centers. These market conditions are contributing to increasing console prices across the board and could influence the timing and feasibility of new hardware releases.

For now, the key takeaway for players is clear: the current Nintendo Switch 2 is getting a price bump next month. While an upgraded OLED model for the Switch 2 might sound appealing, it remains a rumor that's years away from even the earliest stages of development, if it happens at all. It's a reminder that not all whispers translate into immediate reality, especially when market forces are at play.
