Xbox Series X/S Prices Are Climbing Again What It Means For Your Wallet

Another price hike for Xbox Series X/S consoles starts August 1, with a $750 entry point and more increases expected. Here's why the cost of next-gen is going up (again).

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 25, 2026 · 22:00 UTC 2 MIN

Players looking to jump into the current generation of Xbox gaming will soon face a steeper entry fee, as Xbox Series X/S consoles are seeing another price increase. Microsoft is set to raise the cost of its Xbox consoles, with these changes taking effect starting August 1. For many, this means a notable shift in what to expect when budgeting for a new system.

Beginning August 1, the most affordable Xbox Series X console will retail for $750. This establishes a new baseline for what consumers can expect to pay, marking a significant increase for anyone considering a purchase in the coming months. This adjustment raises the barrier to entry, making the consoles less accessible for some potential buyers.

The driving force behind this latest price hike, according to reports, is a substantial 2.5x increase in the cost of storage and memory components. These essential parts are becoming more expensive to produce, and those costs are now being passed on to the consumer. It's a clear signal of broader market pressures influencing the gaming hardware landscape.

Looking further down the road, Xbox anticipates that these component costs will continue their upward trajectory. The company expects storage and memory prices to double once more by Fall 2027. This projection suggests that the current price increase may not be the last, potentially setting the stage for even higher console costs in the future and further impacting purchasing decisions for gamers.

In related news, amidst these pricing adjustments, Xbox has also discontinued its 2TB configuration for the Series X/S consoles. This change in available configurations, coupled with the rising prices, narrows the options for consumers, especially those who might have preferred the larger storage capacity.
