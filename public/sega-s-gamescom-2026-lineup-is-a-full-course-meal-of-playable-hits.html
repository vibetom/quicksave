SEGA's Gamescom 2026 Lineup is a Full-Course Meal of Playable Hits

From Alien: Isolation 2's terrifying debut to Persona 4 Revival and a new Crazy Taxi, SEGA is bringing an extensive and diverse playable demo experience to Cologne.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 17, 2026 · 10:16 UTC 3 MIN

SEGA is gearing up for Gamescom 2026 with an impressive and diverse lineup, offering attendees the chance to get hands-on with some of its most anticipated titles. Leading the charge is the European public playable debut of Alien: Isolation 2, set to be a major draw at their Koelnmesse booth in Hall 7.

Fans eager for more intergalactic dread can dive straight into the prologue of Alien: Isolation 2, marking the first time the public can experience the game on European soil. This alone is enough to send a shiver down the spines of survival horror aficionados, but SEGA isn't stopping there.

The booth will be a hub of activity, packed with other playable demos that span genres and appeal to a wide range of players. Attendees can look forward to revving their engines in Crazy Taxi: World Tour, exploring new fantasy realms in STRANGER THAN HEAVEN, revisiting a beloved classic with Persona 4 Revival, and commanding vast armies in Total War: WARHAMMER 40,000.

Beyond the dedicated demo stations, a free play area promises even more gaming goodness. Here, visitors will find Metaphor: ReFantazio playable on the Nintendo Switch 2, offering an early glimpse at the next-generation hardware. Also available for free play will be the Lords of the End Times DLC for Total War: WARHAMMER III, the latest updates for Sonic Racing: CrossWorlds, and the delightful chaos of SONIC PICO PARK.

SEGA is clearly invested in making Gamescom 2026 a memorable experience, not just a showcase of games. The company's booth will feature a full fan experience, including various hands-on opportunities, engaging photo opportunities, on-stand challenges to test skills, and merchandise giveaways to take a piece of the action home.

All this excitement will unfold from August 26 to August 30, 2026, in Cologne, Germany. SEGA's carefully curated selection of highly anticipated sequels and beloved revivals is poised to create a buzz, appealing to both long-time devotees and newcomers looking for their next gaming obsession.
