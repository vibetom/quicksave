EWC 2026 Kicks Off: Bigger Prizes, More Games, and a Whole New Way to Win

The Esports World Cup is already breaking records in Paris, with its $75 million prize pool and a Club Championship reshaping how teams compete.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 11, 2026 · 21:13 UTC 4 MIN

The Esports World Cup 2026 has officially begun, transforming Paris Expo Porte de Versailles into the epicenter of competitive gaming. From its launch on July 6th through August 23rd, the event is setting unprecedented benchmarks, anchored by an astounding $75 million prize pool. This record sum is distributed across 25 tournaments spanning 24 diverse games, attracting more than two thousand competitors and two hundred organizations hailing from over a hundred nations to the French capital.

At the heart of this year's innovation is the $30 million Club Championship, a game-changer designed to elevate organizational prestige. This structure sees a substantial $7 million earmarked for the winning club, fostering sustained team success and dynamic rivalries. Saudi's Team Falcons, the reigning champions, are particularly keen, aiming for an unprecedented third Club Championship title.

Getting to Paris was no small feat for many competitors. Over 1.5 million players worldwide battled through 330 "Road to EWC" qualification tournaments, earning their place among the elite. The opening ceremony at Hôtel de Ville underscored the event's significance, with remarks from figures like Paris Mayor Emmanuel Grégoire and French Sports Minister Marina Ferrari, signaling a strong civic embrace of esports.

The initial wave of competition, dubbed the first Championship Weekend, is wrapping up today, July 11th, showcasing high-stakes finals in major titles. VALORANT is a key highlight, with defending EWC winners Team Heretics topping their group, alongside strong contenders like American team 100 Thieves and French favorites Team Vitality and Gentle Mates. The tournament also features 2025 VALORANT Champions victors NRG, adding another layer of intensity.

Apex Legends is also commanding attention, with its ALGS Year 6 Split 1 Playoffs integrated into the EWC schedule. Forty teams are vying for a substantial $2 million prize pool, with Elite Esports EU currently leading the charge, closely followed by Team Liquid, AG.AL, and Gaimin Gladiators. Meanwhile, the Fatal Fury: City of the Wolves tournament is delivering its own brand of fighting game spectacle, with 32 skilled fighters competing for a $1 million prize.

Beyond the intense competition, the EWC ensures global accessibility. Coverage is available in over 40 languages, bolstered by as many as five thousand independent content creators participating in the Creator Program. This extensive reach, combined with key partnerships like stc group returning as a Founding and Elite Partner, holding naming rights for the stcArena, the stc Player's Lounge, and stc Contenders, solidifies the Esports World Cup's position as a transformative force in the esports landscape.
