Classic Xbox Games Just Got a PC Glow-Up

Backward compatibility officially lands on PC, bringing favorites like Conker: Live and Reloaded with upscaled graphics and new achievements.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 22, 2026 · 18:39 UTC 3 MIN

Xbox has officially thrown open the vault doors, announcing that backward compatibility for classic titles is now available on PC. This move immediately gives PC gamers access to a library previously confined to older consoles, marking a significant step for game preservation and accessibility.

The initial rollout comes with some heavy hitters, including the famously irreverent `Conker: Live and Reloaded` and the aerial combat favorite `Crimson Skies`. But don't mistake this for a small-scale revival; Xbox describes this as "just the start" of a broader effort to bring more of its back catalog to PC, hinting at a future where even more beloved classics find a new home.

These aren't just straight ports, either. The PC versions arrive with a fresh coat of paint, boasting 4x upscaling to make those decades-old visuals shine on modern displays. Beyond the visual enhancements, players can also look forward to new achievements, offering fresh reasons to dive back into these timeless adventures or experience them for the first time.

The horizon for these classics might extend even further. According to reports from Wccftech, this new backward compatibility feature is also expected to reach handheld devices. This potential expansion would mean an even wider range of platforms for players to enjoy their favorite Xbox games, making classics more mobile than ever before.

This initiative underscores Xbox's commitment to its gaming heritage, ensuring that iconic titles not only survive but thrive in the current gaming landscape. For many, it's a chance to revisit cherished memories with a visual polish, while a new generation gets to discover the games that shaped an era, all from the comfort of their PC.
