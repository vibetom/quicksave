Xbox's Big Bet Flops: What the Game Pass Pivot Means for Players and Studios

Massive layoffs, studio changes, and leadership's admission of 'failure' reveal a new, uncertain direction for Microsoft Gaming, moving away from its Game Pass-first strategy.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 09, 2026 · 14:03 UTC 3 MIN

Xbox is in the midst of what Microsoft Gaming CEO Asha Sharma has described as the most significant restructuring in its history. This shake-up has brought a wave of changes, including a reported 1,600 layoffs already, with another 1,600 planned over the coming year. These reductions have touched veteran staff and accessibility teams, alongside several studios under the Xbox umbrella, including Obsidian, Id Software, and ZeniMax.

At the heart of this dramatic shift is the acknowledged underperformance of the Xbox Game Pass model. Leadership has confirmed that the substantial financial investment poured into Game Pass hasn't delivered the anticipated returns, with reports indicating a decline in subscribers since 2024. Sharma explicitly stated that the company's gaming strategy had failed, pinpointing Game Pass as a core component of that failure.

This strategic pivot means Xbox is re-evaluating its broader goals, channeling resources into what it sees as its most prominent franchises and projects with the 'greatest potential.' This refocus is already prompting notable changes across its studios. For instance, Bethesda has informed its teams that it needs to 'change course,' prioritizing projects with strong potential. There's also uncertainty surrounding the future of State of Decay 3 on Game Pass, and Obsidian is reportedly shifting its focus to a new Fallout game, helmed by New Vegas director Josh Sawyer, while an Avowed sequel has reportedly been cancelled. This shift to Fallout for Obsidian is particularly noteworthy given reports that an earlier Fallout game by the studio was stopped by Bethesda before an Xbox mandate.

Further compounding these changes, four development teams are no longer part of Microsoft's internal structure. Double Fine and Compulsion Games have reverted to independent operation, while Undead Labs and Ninja Theory have been sold off. The future of Arkane Studios is currently in negotiations, underscoring the deep re-evaluation happening across Microsoft's gaming division.

Experts weighing in on these developments point to internal mismanagement and aggressive, premature gambles on AI-driven development as potential factors contributing to these strategic missteps. The current landscape paints a picture of Xbox retreating from its broad Game Pass ambitions, instead sharpening its focus on established, high-potential intellectual properties.
