Xbox's Studio Shake-Up: What's Next for Double Fine, Ninja Theory, and Compulsion Games?

Reports indicate several Xbox studios are on the chopping block, sparking fear for their future and the fate of their unique games. We break down what's known about the looming 'carnage' and what it could mean for players.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 16, 2026 · 18:43 UTC 3 MIN

A wave of concern is sweeping through the games industry, as reports suggest a significant restructuring could be underway at Xbox, potentially impacting several well-regarded development studios.

Reputable French journalist Sylvain Trinel recently shared on social media that a "summer of utter carnage" is anticipated across the games industry, with major publishers like Microsoft reportedly eyeing the closure of entire studios. Trinel characterized Xbox's current situation, with multiple active development teams facing imminent closure, as "just the beginning of a massive bloodbath for studios owned by publishers."

Among the studios reportedly facing potential closure are Ninja Theory, known for its work on 'Hellblade'; Double Fine, the creative force behind 'Psychonauts'; and Compulsion Games, currently developing 'South of Midnight.' These teams have earned a reputation for delivering distinctive, critically acclaimed experiences. However, an analyst told IGN that studios described as "brilliant for prestige" might be considered "rotten for the spreadsheet," following a "reset" warning from Xbox's chief strategy officer, Asha Sharma.

Amidst these developments, reports also indicate that Xbox Game Studios boss Craig Duncan has stepped down. The affected studios are reportedly in discussions with Xbox, exploring avenues to avoid closure, potentially by buying their independence. Such a move, while preserving the studios, would likely still involve layoffs for some staff.

The industry and community are clearly feeling the weight of these reports. Double Fine posted a single emoji on social media, a move widely interpreted as a direct response to the swirling rumors and a reflection of the pervasive unease. This shift towards a more fiscally driven approach could have broader implications for the diversity of creative projects within the Xbox ecosystem.

While this news circulates, Xbox Game Pass recently confirmed its June 2026 Wave 2 lineup, featuring additions like 'EA Sports FC 26', 'RV There Yet?', 'Winds of Arcana: Ruination', 'Call of Duty: Vanguard', and 'Junkster.' The contrast between these confirmed Game Pass additions and the reports of potential studio closures highlights a period of significant change and uncertainty for Xbox and its development partners.
