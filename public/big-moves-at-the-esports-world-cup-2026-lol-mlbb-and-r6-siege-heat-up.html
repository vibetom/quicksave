Big Moves at the Esports World Cup 2026: LoL, MLBB, and R6 Siege Heat Up

Playoffs are underway for League of Legends and Mobile Legends: Bang Bang Women's International, while Rainbow Six Siege locks in its first qualified teams for the upcoming August battles at the Esports World Cup 2026.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 17, 2026 · 12:41 UTC 4 MIN

The Esports World Cup 2026 is kicking into high gear, with major tournaments across multiple titles witnessing dramatic advances, intense rivalries, and the securing of crucial qualification spots. From high-stakes League of Legends playoffs in Paris to a highly anticipated rematch in Mobile Legends: Bang Bang, and the first teams securing their berths in Rainbow Six Siege, the competitive landscape of the Esports World Cup 2026 is rapidly evolving.

In Paris, the League of Legends event at the Esports World Cup 2026 has entered its playoff stage today, July 17th. Eight teams are now battling it out in a single-elimination knockout bracket, each vying for supremacy. Among this elite group, Karmine Corp stands as the lone reported Western team to have made it into the top eight, a testament to their skill on a globally dominated stage.

The Mobile Legends: Bang Bang Women's International tournament at the Esports World Cup 2026 has also seen significant movement. In the early hours of Friday, July 17th (Manila time), Natus Vincere Philippines (NAVI PH) delivered a decisive performance, sweeping Team Rey Young from Malaysia with a 2-0 victory. This win propels NAVI PH directly into the semifinals, where they are set for a compelling rematch against Galaxy Phoenix of Cambodia. Galaxy Phoenix holds the distinction of being the only team to have defeated NAVI PH previously, taking a 2-0 win during the group stage.

Meanwhile, the competitive scene for Rainbow Six Siege is firming up its roster for the Esports World Cup 2026. Today, July 17th, ENTERPRISE Esports clinched Oceania's top seed, overcoming the Chiefs in a qualifying match. While ENTERPRISE secures a direct spot, the Chiefs will still have an opportunity to compete in the EWC 2026 Rainbow Six Siege by navigating through the play-ins. The main event for Rainbow Six Siege is slated to run from August 4th to August 15th.

In other League of Legends news, Riot Games has made a notable change based on player feedback, making Classic Skins the default and free option in League of Legends Classic, replacing the previous base skins. It's a small but welcome detail amidst all the high-stakes competitive action.

The Esports World Cup 2026 continues to deliver a vibrant spectacle, showcasing the pinnacle of competitive gaming across diverse titles and bringing together teams from every corner of the globe.
