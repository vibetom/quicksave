Riot Games Teams Up With KICK: What It Means For Esports Fans And Creators

Starting with MSI 2026, Riot's biggest esports titles are heading to KICK, promising wider reach, new creator opportunities, and the same viewer rewards.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 26, 2026 · 19:15 UTC 3 MIN

Riot Games is making a significant play to broaden the reach of its competitive scene, officially announcing a partnership with KICK that will bring global and regional esports events to the platform. Fans of League of Legends (LoL Esports), VALORANT, and Teamfight Tactics (TFT) can look forward to seeing their favorite tournaments broadcast on KICK, with the rollout kicking off with MSI 2026.

This strategic collaboration is designed to expand the distribution of Riot's popular esports content, particularly focusing on communities in Latin America (LATAM), the Middle East and North Africa (MENA), and Europe. For many esports enthusiasts in these regions, KICK has already become a go-to platform, making this move a direct connection to an engaged audience.

For viewers, the transition to KICK won't mean a compromise on the perks they've come to expect. Riot Games confirms that the same drops and incentives available on other broadcasting platforms will also be offered to those watching on KICK, ensuring a consistent and rewarding viewing experience across the board.

Beyond viewership, this partnership also cultivates new ground for creators. KICK streamers will find fresh opportunities to expand their communities and forge connections with Riot Games. Expanded creator and partner programs are on the horizon, encompassing League of Legends, VALORANT, TFT, and even Wild Rift, opening new avenues for engagement and growth within the Riot ecosystem.

Crucially, Riot Games has stated its commitment to maintaining a safe and positive environment on KICK. The company will apply its existing moderation practices and standards to KICK streamers, mirroring the approach it takes with creators on other platforms. This ensures that the expanded reach doesn't come at the expense of community well-being, aiming for a consistent and secure experience for all viewers and participants.
