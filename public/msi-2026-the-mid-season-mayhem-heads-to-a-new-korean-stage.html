MSI 2026: The Mid-Season Mayhem Heads to a New Korean Stage

All 11 teams are locked in, the dates are set, and a fresh city is ready to host the League of Legends Mid-Season Invitational. Here's what you need to know about where the battle for bragging rights will unfold.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 14, 2026 · 18:51 UTC 2 MIN

The Mid-Season Invitational (MSI) for League of Legends in 2026 has confirmed its full roster, with all 11 competing squads now finalized and ready to join the fray. This yearly tournament serves as a key proving ground, spotlighting premier talent from across the globe ahead of the World Championship.

Daejeon, South Korea, will host the MSI 2026, with action running from June 28 to July 12. The Daejeon Convention Center II is set to be the arena where these top-tier teams will contend. This marks a notable inaugural for Daejeon, as the city steps onto the international platform to stage a major Riot Games event. It signals a new phase for League of Legends esports, moving beyond traditional, established centers like Seoul.

Contenders from six distinct competitive territories will battle for dominance. Two teams will represent South Korea's LCK, China's LPL, Europe, the Middle East, and Africa's LEC, North America's LCS, and the Asia-Pacific region's LCP. Additionally, Brazil's CBLOL will send one qualifying squad, ensuring a diverse range of gameplay and national pride are on display.

Entry into MSI 2026 was a fiercely contested process, concluding between June 6 and June 13. Among the early teams to secure their berths are Team Secret Whales from the LCP and G2 Esports, representing Europe. These teams, along with the others, are now poised for a mid-season conflict, strategically placed on the calendar to steer clear of other major gaming events. The stage is primed for an electrifying showcase of League of Legends prowess.
