Why Is Gaming Still Bleeding Jobs?

Despite record-breaking revenue in 2025, over 3,700 game industry professionals have lost their jobs globally in 2026, with the U.S. bearing the brunt of these cuts.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 20, 2026 · 21:45 UTC 2 MIN

The video game industry, a vibrant landscape of innovation and creativity, is currently navigating a period of profound turbulence. So far in 2026, more than 3,700 verified job losses have rippled across the global sector. This figure, according to reporting by Social News XYZ, likely climbs past 4,000 once unconfirmed reductions, undisclosed staff changes, and studio closures are factored in.

The epicenter of this dramatic shift appears to be the United States, which accounts for a significant 2,153 confirmed job cuts. This represents a substantial 58.2% of the total global layoffs, marking it as the region most acutely affected by the industry's ongoing restructuring.

Major players in the gaming world are not immune to these cutbacks. Epic Games, the powerhouse behind Fortnite and the Unreal Engine, implemented the largest single reduction, impacting approximately 1,000 roles. Bungie, known for its work on the Halo and Destiny franchises, has also signaled plans to lay off around 400 team members this summer. Meanwhile, Ubisoft announced roughly 680 layoffs across its various studios as part of a sweeping restructuring effort, with impacts felt in France and beyond.

The trend extends its reach across continents. Canada has recorded at least 257 gaming sector layoffs. A significant portion of these, 124 positions, came from Eidos-Montréal following the cancellation of a major AAA project. The ripple effect of these decisions is also leading to outright studio shutdowns, further illustrating the widespread instability.

This wave of job losses presents a stark paradox when viewed against the industry's recent financial performance. As Wccftech noted, the global games revenue reached an all-time high of $201.6 billion in 2025. Similarly, PC Gamer highlighted the ongoing layoffs even as the market experiences growth. This disconnect between record revenue and widespread job insecurity paints a concerning picture of a global games market in active, and often painful, transformation.
