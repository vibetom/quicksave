Switch 2's Latest Drops: Star Fox, Devil May Cry, and More Make a Big Splash This Week

Nintendo's newest console is getting a robust shot in the arm with highly anticipated remakes, fresh ports, and new titles hitting the eShop, giving players plenty to dive into.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 24, 2026 · 19:09 UTC 3 MIN

The Nintendo Switch 2 is carving out a strong launch with an impressive slate of games arriving this week. Players eager to dive into new experiences or revisit classics with a fresh coat of paint will find a lot to love, signaling that the platform is quickly building a compelling library.

Leading the charge is a highly anticipated return for a beloved franchise: Star Fox. This remake of the Nintendo 64 classic promises revamped graphics and modernized gameplay, giving both long-time fans and newcomers a reason to jump into the cockpit when it lands on June 25, 2026.

Beyond new takes on old favorites, the Switch 2 is also proving itself as a prime destination for meticulously optimized ports. Devil May Cry 5 Devil Hunter Edition arrives on June 23, 2026, showcasing sharp graphics and a silky-smooth 60 FPS in both docked and handheld modes. Meanwhile, the open-world action-adventure game Destroy All Humans! makes its Switch 2 debut on June 22, 2026, bringing its brand of alien mayhem to the hybrid console.

But it's not all big-budget blockbusters. The week's releases also offer a refreshing dash of variety. The point-and-click adventure thriller The Drifter made its way to both Switch and Switch 2 on June 22, 2026, offering a more cerebral experience. And for those seeking something visually distinct, the cinematic platformer Deer & Boy arrived for multiple systems, including PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X|S, Switch, and PC, on June 23, 2026, promising an evocative journey.

These immediate additions underscore Nintendo's commitment to the Switch 2's library. They arrive hot on the heels of the June 2026 Nintendo Direct, a 50-minute showcase that also teased a robust future. Confirmed upcoming titles include a day-one release for Kingdom Hearts 4, Final Fantasy Resonance slated for October, and Xenoblade Genesis arriving in 2027. Fans can also look forward to a remake of The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time on Switch 2 later in 2026. This week's launches are just the beginning, but they certainly set an exciting precedent for the console's future.
