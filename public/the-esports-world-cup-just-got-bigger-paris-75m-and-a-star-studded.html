The Esports World Cup Just Got Bigger: Paris, $75M, and a Star-Studded Kickoff

The record-breaking prize pool and move to the City of Lights aren't just details - they're a statement. Here's what the 2026 EWC means for players, fans, and the future of esports.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 05, 2026 · 12:47 UTC 4 MIN

The Esports World Cup is planting its flag in new territory for 2026, officially moving to Paris, France. This marks the first time the global esports spectacle has ventured outside of Saudi Arabia, setting up shop from July 6 to August 23, with the initial leg concluding on July 19.

This isn't just a change of scenery; it's a statement. The EWC 2026 is rolling out an unprecedented $75 million prize pool, the largest in esports history. A significant chunk of that, approximately $30 million, is earmarked for the Club Championship, where the top organization stands to pocket a cool $7 million. This kind of financial firepower is a clear signal of the event's ambition and commitment to rewarding top-tier talent.

Players and fans have a packed schedule to look forward to, with 25 tournaments spanning 24 game titles. The lineup is both broad and exciting, featuring new additions like the high-speed racing of Trackmania, the frantic fun of Fortnite in Reload mode, the tactical combat of Call of Duty: Black Ops 7, and the virtual pitch battles of EA Sports FC 26. Mobile esports will also take a prominent place, with titans such as Honor of Kings, PUBG Mobile, and Mobile Legends: Bang Bang. Notably, Mobile Legends: Bang Bang will feature both a Women's Invitational and a Mid-Season Cup. Over 2,000 players, representing more than 200 clubs from over 100 countries, are set to compete.

The early days of July are already stacked with major showdowns. The Dota 2 EWC will run from July 6-18, pitting 24 clubs against each other for a $2 million prize pool. VALORANT fans can catch their tournament from July 9-12, where 16 clubs will battle for another $2 million. League of Legends will then take center stage from July 13-19, with 16 clubs also vying for a $2 million prize pool. The main venue for all this action will be the Paris Expo Porte de Versailles.

To kick things off, a star-studded opening event is planned for July 8 at La Seine Musicale in Boulogne-Billancourt. The proceedings promise a fusion of gaming and mainstream entertainment, with confirmed performances by DJ Snake, Aya Nakamura, Theodora, and Mosimann. Adding a local touch, influential French online personalities Inoxtag and Squeezie are slated to appear in a special VALORANT Showmatch. Beyond the main stages, the Esports Foundation Creator Program is also offering rewards for approved co-streamers, widening the net for community engagement. Paris itself is no stranger to major esports events, having previously hosted the final CS-GO Major in 2023, the Rainbow Six Siege Six Invitational in February, and the Rocket League Paris Major, also in February. All signs point to the EWC 2026 in Paris being a landmark event, not just for its scale, but for solidifying esports' place on the global entertainment stage.
