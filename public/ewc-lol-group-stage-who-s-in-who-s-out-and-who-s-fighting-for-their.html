EWC LoL Group Stage: Who's In, Who's Out, and Who's Fighting for Their Lives Today

The League of Legends Group Stage at the Esports World Cup 2026 wraps up today, July 16, with half the field already eliminated and the remaining teams locking in their playoff berths.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 16, 2026 · 15:53 UTC 3 MIN

The five-day initial phase of the League of Legends competition at the Esports World Cup (EWC) 2026 is reaching its climax today, July 16. This crucial day will determine which teams secure their advancement and which will be sent home. Out of the original contenders, eight teams will punch their ticket to the Playoffs bracket, while the other eight will see their run in the EWC come to an end during this Group Stage.

Already, the elimination rounds have claimed their first victims. Four teams are now definitively out of the running, having lost two matches in their respective groups. Furia from Group A faced an early exit after falling to Dplus in the Lower Bracket Semifinal. Team Secret's journey in Group B concluded following a defeat by Karmine Corp. Movistar KOI was eliminated from Group C after a loss to GAM Esports, and LYON’s challenge in Group D ended against MIBR.LOS.

Today's schedule is packed with critical matchups. The Upper Bracket Finals will grant direct entry to the Playoffs for their victors, featuring some high-profile confrontations. G2 Esports will clash with AG.AL in Group A, while Sentinels will go up against Gen.G Esports in Group B. Group C will witness a battle between Bilibili Gaming and T1, and in Group D, Hanwha Life is set to face JD Gaming. Each of these games offers a direct route to the next stage of the tournament.

For those who do not succeed in the Upper Bracket, or for teams that have already battled through the Lower Bracket, the fight continues. Today also hosts the Lower Bracket Finals, where teams will fiercely compete for the second Playoffs slot from each group. These are do-or-die contests, which will finalize the lineup for the impending knockout stage.

This League of Legends event stands as a key highlight of the broader Esports World Cup 2026, currently underway in Paris, France, from July 6 to August 23. This grand esports celebration features 25 competitions across 24 distinct titles, boasting an impressive total prize pool of $75 million. The League of Legends segment alone offers a substantial $2 million prize pool, ensuring high stakes for every match.
