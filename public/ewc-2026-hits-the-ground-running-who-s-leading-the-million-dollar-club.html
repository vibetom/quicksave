EWC 2026 Hits the Ground Running: Who's Leading the Million-Dollar Club Race?

The Esports World Cup in Paris is underway, with a record-breaking $75 million on the line and early upsets already shaking up the Club Championship standings.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 10, 2026 · 13:24 UTC 3 MIN

The Esports World Cup 2026 has roared to life in Paris, launching on July 6th and set to run through August 23rd. This isn't just another tournament; it's a colossal gathering of competitive gaming talent, bringing together participants from over 100 nations, with more than 2,000 players across 200 clubs, according to Arab News. The scale is staggering, with PR Newswire reporting a record-shattering $75 million prize pool distributed across 25 tournaments in 24 different games.

At the heart of this esports titan is the Club Championship, a unique competition boasting an additional $30 million prize pool. As reported by Gamereactor, clubs aren't just vying for individual game titles; they're accumulating points across multiple events, all building towards a grand, cumulative victory for the most dominant organization. This structure adds a compelling layer of strategy and excitement, as every match in every title could contribute to a club's overall standing.

Early results are already painting a dramatic picture. In the VALORANT championship, Week 1 has been a rollercoaster. Defending EWC champions Team Heretics, who initially sailed through Group D undefeated, have surprisingly been knocked out of the playoffs, joined by Gentle Mates. Instead, NRG and BBL Esports have punched their tickets to the semi-finals, overcoming Gentle Mates and Team Heretics respectively, as detailed by PandaScore.

Meanwhile, the competitive season for Apex Legends, specifically the Year 6 Split 1 Playoffs, is also a key part of the EWC 2026. This segment alone features 40 teams battling for a hefty $2 million prize pool. Currently, Elite Esports EU holds a commanding lead in the Apex Legends standings with 138 points, putting them 18 points clear of the second-place contender, according to PandaScore.

As Week 1 draws to a close, we'll see the first champions crowned in VALORANT, Apex Legends, and Fatal Fury: City of the Wolves. The initial drama and the sheer scale of the Esports World Cup 2026 have set a high bar, promising an intense and unpredictable journey towards the ultimate Club Championship.
