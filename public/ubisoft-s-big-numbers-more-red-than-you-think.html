Ubisoft's Big Numbers: More Red Than You Think

A closer look at the company's FY26 financials shows deep losses, significant job cuts, and a 'softer' immediate future, despite promises of a long-term rebound.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 02, 2026 · 18:55 UTC 3 MIN

Ubisoft's latest financial report for the 2025-2026 fiscal year (FY26), released on May 20, 2026, paints a stark picture. The company saw its revenue decline by 21.8% year-over-year, settling at €1.4 billion. Net bookings also took a hit, down 17.4% year-over-year to €1.5 billion, according to GamesIndustry.biz.

The most striking figure from the report is the substantial operating loss of €1.3 billion for FY26. This represents a significant widening from the €196.5 million loss recorded in the previous fiscal year. Ubisoft attributed this downturn to a 'softer new release schedule' and the implementation of a new operating model.

In response to these financial challenges, Ubisoft has undertaken considerable cost-cutting measures. The company confirmed the reduction of approximately 1,200 jobs, alongside an €118 million cut in fixed costs. These decisions reflect a period of intense restructuring for the publisher.

Looking ahead, Ubisoft anticipates that FY27 will mark a 'low point in our free cash flow trajectory,' citing a 'softer release slate and restructuring costs.' Despite this immediate hurdle, management projects a notable recovery with increasing free cash flow in FY27-28, expecting robust financial returns by FY28-29. This recovery is slated to be driven by its live service portfolio, new titles such as Assassin's Creed Black Flag Resynced, and other premium game experiences built around familiar Ubisoft intellectual properties.

While Ubisoft charts a course for a future rebound, the immediate impact of these financial struggles and ongoing restructuring efforts on its development pipeline and the overall player experience remains a central question. As the company navigates this turbulent period, some industry observers, as reported by Simply Wall St News, perceive these outcomes as indicative of profound systemic issues within the organization. The coming years will be crucial in determining whether Ubisoft can successfully pivot towards the recovery it envisions.
