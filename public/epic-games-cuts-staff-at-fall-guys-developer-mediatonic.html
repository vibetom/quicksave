Epic Games Cuts Staff at Fall Guys Developer Mediatonic

Layoffs impact the team behind the popular battle royale, part of a broader restructuring at Epic Games.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 16, 2026 · 12:58 UTC 2 MIN

Epic Games has confirmed a round of layoffs at Mediatonic, the UK-based studio known for developing Fall Guys. These staff reductions are part of a wider restructuring initiative by the publisher.

While Epic Games or Mediatonic have not immediately provided specific figures regarding the number of employees affected, reports from outlets like GamesIndustry.biz and Eurogamer indicate that these cuts have impacted the Fall Guys development team.

Mediatonic joined the Epic Games family in March 2021. Just over a year later, in June 2022, Fall Guys shifted its business model to become a free-to-play title, coinciding with its launch on Xbox and Nintendo Switch platforms.

These adjustments at Mediatonic occur during a challenging period for numerous studios across the gaming sector, even for those behind widely popular games such as Fall Guys.

We will continue to monitor this situation and provide updates as further details become available regarding the scope and implications of these changes.
