What's Going On With Gaming Layoffs? 2026 Sees Thousands More Jobs Lost

From Epic Games to indie studios, a turbulent year for game development continues as major companies restructure and smaller teams face sudden closures. Here's what we know.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 21, 2026 · 13:23 UTC 3 MIN

The video game industry continues to navigate a turbulent period, with 2026 marking another year of significant job losses across the globe. So far this year, approximately 3,700 verified job cuts have occurred, a figure that swells beyond 4,000 when accounting for unverified reductions, undisclosed staff movements, and studio closures, according to reports from TradingPlatforms and ETHRWorld.com.

North America has emerged as a central point for these industry contractions. The United States alone accounts for 2,153 confirmed gaming job cuts, making up over 58% of the global total. Canada has also felt the impact, recording at least 257 layoffs in the gaming sector this year.

Even major players in the industry are undergoing significant restructuring. Epic Games, known for its dominant titles, has cut around 1,000 roles, representing one of the largest single waves of layoffs this year. Bungie, the studio behind popular online games, announced plans to lay off approximately 400 people this summer. Ubisoft has also seen substantial reductions, with around 680 employees affected across various studios as part of its ongoing restructuring efforts.

The ripple effects extend to Canada, where nearly half of the country's layoffs—124 jobs—came from Eidos-Montreal. These cuts followed the cancellation of a major AAA project, illustrating how the halting of ambitious development can directly translate to job losses.

Beyond the major publishers, even smaller development teams are not immune to this instability. The team responsible for the bullet hell FPS Luna Abyss reportedly faced layoffs less than a month after the game's release, as noted by PC Gamer. This highlights the precarious nature of game development across all scales, where even successful launches do not always guarantee job security.

Further potential shifts are on the horizon. Xbox (Microsoft) is reportedly exploring significant changes to its business, including the consideration of shuttering several studios such as Compulsion Games, Double Fine, and Ninja Theory. According to Northeastern Global News, these developers are reportedly engaged in negotiations to transition to independent status, a move that could still involve substantial layoffs.

The ongoing wave of job reductions underscores a challenging environment for developers and a volatile market for the games we anticipate. These widespread changes, from the largest publishers to independent studios, affect the creative landscape and the future of gaming experiences.
