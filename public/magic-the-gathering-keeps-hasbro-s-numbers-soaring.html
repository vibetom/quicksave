Magic: The Gathering Keeps Hasbro's Numbers Soaring

Hasbro boosts its annual revenue and profit forecasts, crediting a surge in digital gaming and the unstoppable momentum of its fantasy card game.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 21, 2026 · 16:00 UTC 3 MIN

Hasbro delivered some good news on Tuesday, July 21, 2026, announcing it has lifted its financial outlook for the year. The company is now projecting higher annual revenue and profit, driven significantly by the robust performance of its digital gaming ventures and the sustained power of its Magic: The Gathering franchise.

The Wizards of the Coast and Digital Gaming unit, a key pillar for Hasbro, reported an impressive 27% jump in revenue during the second quarter. This marks a substantial acceleration from the 16% growth observed in the same period a year prior, showing the segment's increasing momentum.

Hasbro CEO Chris Cocks highlighted the internal view on the card game's success, stating that "the Magic flywheel is firing on all cylinders." He also signaled strong indications for forthcoming releases and anticipated continued growth into 2027, painting a picture of enduring vitality for the fantasy franchise.

Looking ahead, Hasbro now anticipates annual revenue growth to land between 5% and 7%, an upgrade from its previous forecast of 3% to 5%. The adjusted core profit for the year also received a bump, now expected to range from $1.45 billion to $1.50 billion, up from the prior estimate of $1.40 billion to $1.45 billion.

This improved financial performance is attributed to a strategic success: higher-income consumers have helped offset the demand pressures stemming from lower-income households grappling with persistent inflation. This suggests a resilient market for Hasbro's offerings among a key demographic.

However, some analysts are peering into the future with a touch of skepticism, according to reports. Questions have been raised about whether Magic: The Gathering can sustain this impressive pace of growth, especially as it faces tougher comparisons in the latter half of the year. The game has set a high bar, and maintaining that momentum will be the next challenge.
