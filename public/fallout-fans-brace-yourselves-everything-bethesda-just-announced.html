Fallout Fans, Brace Yourselves: Everything Bethesda Just Announced

From confirmed remasters of beloved classics to the first whispers of Fallout 5 and an unexpected collaboration, here's what's coming to the Wasteland.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 18, 2026 · 09:53 UTC 2 MIN

The Wasteland is about to get a lot more crowded, as Bethesda has pulled back the curtain on a truly expansive roadmap for the Fallout franchise. Players have been clamoring for news on several fronts, and the latest announcements deliver a full blast of confirmation, from future mainline entries to long-desired revivals and a surprising new partnership.

First on the docket, and perhaps the most anticipated news for many, is the official confirmation that Fallout 5 has entered pre-production. While details remain sparse, this marks the first concrete step toward the next chapter in the iconic post-nuclear RPG series, according to IGN. It’s a clear signal that the future of the franchise is being actively built, even if it's still taking shape in the early stages.

But the future isn't the only focus; Bethesda is also looking to its past. Fans will be thrilled to learn that remasters of two fan-favorite entries, Fallout 3 and Fallout: New Vegas, are officially confirmed, as reported by PC Gamer. These titles are often considered benchmarks in the series, and their return with modern enhancements is bound to reignite countless journeys through their distinct, irradiated landscapes.

Perhaps the most intriguing announcement, however, is the confirmed collaboration between Bethesda and Obsidian for a new Fallout game, GameSpot reports. Obsidian Entertainment developed Fallout: New Vegas, a title celebrated for its rich narrative and complex player choices. The prospect of these two creative forces reuniting for a fresh take on the Fallout universe is a genuine cause for excitement, hinting at a project that could blend classic design with new ideas.

This wave of announcements suggests a robust, multi-pronged approach to the Fallout franchise, aiming to satisfy both the craving for nostalgia and the hunger for new adventures. It's a comprehensive vision that acknowledges the series' storied past while firmly planting its flag in the future.
