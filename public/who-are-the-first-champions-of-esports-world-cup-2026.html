Who Are the First Champions of Esports World Cup 2026?

A record-breaking prize pool and global competition kick off with intense showdowns in Fatal Fury, Apex Legends, and VALORANT.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 12, 2026 · 18:15 UTC 3 MIN

The Esports World Cup 2026 (EWC 2026) has swiftly launched into action, already crowning its initial champions while setting the stage for more high-stakes finals. Launched in Paris on July 6 and running through August 23, the tournament is making waves with its unprecedented $75 million prize pool. This global event encompasses 25 tournaments across 24 distinct games, drawing in more than 2,000 players and 200 clubs from over 100 countries.

The first week of competition saw two titles decided in thrilling fashion. In `Fatal Fury: City of the Wolves`, Natus Vincere's Darkangel emerged victorious on July 11, securing the inaugural championship title along with $250,000 and 1,000 Club Championship points. Darkangel defeated Virtus.pro player mi2ha4 in the grand final. Meanwhile, UNLIMIT took the top spot in the `Apex Legends` Global Series (ALGS) competition, which was integrated into the Paris festival, marking another significant championship within the EWC 2026.

Looking ahead, the `VALORANT` Grand Finals at EWC 2026 are shaping up to be an electrifying all-North American clash. 100 Thieves will face off against NRG after both teams dispatched their semifinal opponents, Nongshim RedForce and BBL Esports, respectively. This best-of-five series will award the winner $600,000 and 1000 EWC Club points. The battle for third place is also on, with Nongshim RedForce and BBL Esports vying for $220,000 and 500 Club points.

The rapid-fire start to the EWC 2026 underscores the immense scale and competitive intensity now driving the global esports scene. As reported, the worldwide esports audience soared to 640.8 million people in 2025, with an impressive 8.1% annual growth in the enthusiast segment since 2020. The Esports World Cup 2026 appears poised to not only meet this growing demand but further fuel the competitive spirit of an industry that continues to expand its reach and excitement.
