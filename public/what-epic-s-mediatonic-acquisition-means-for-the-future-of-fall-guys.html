What Epic's Mediatonic Acquisition Means for the Future of Fall Guys

Your favorite bean-based battle royale is joining the Epic family, and that could mean a lot for where and how you play.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 03, 2026 · 11:07 UTC 2 MIN

Epic Games, the company behind Fortnite, officially acquired Tonic Games Group on March 2, 2021. This move brings Mediatonic, the studio responsible for the colorful chaos of Fall Guys: Ultimate Knockout, along with Fortitude Games, under the Epic umbrella.

Fans of the game might be wondering what this acquisition means for their beloved jelly bean antics. Epic Games has confirmed that Fall Guys will continue to be available on its existing platforms, PlayStation and PC. The company emphasized its commitment to the game's ongoing development, stating they would "continue to invest in the game's continued evolution," according to GamesIndustry.biz.

One of the most significant announcements to emerge from this deal is Epic's clear intention to expand Fall Guys' reach. Epic has specifically named Nintendo Switch and Xbox as target platforms, promising a much wider audience will soon be able to stumble their way through the Blunderdome.

While current players won't see immediate changes to their gameplay experience, the acquisition has ignited a wave of discussion regarding future possibilities. As PC Gamer reported, the move has fueled speculation about features like cross-play functionality or deeper integration with the broader Epic Games ecosystem, potentially through services like Epic Online Services or even the Epic Games Store.

This acquisition fits neatly into Epic's ongoing strategy of expanding its collection of popular live-service titles. By bringing Fall Guys into the fold, Epic continues to bolster its position in the ever-evolving landscape of competitive online gaming, promising a broader and potentially more connected experience for players.
