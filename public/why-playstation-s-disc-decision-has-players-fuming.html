Why PlayStation's Disc Decision Has Players Fuming

Sony's plan to end physical game releases by 2028 has ignited a massive petition and widespread criticism from fans and preservation advocates.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 08, 2026 · 10:33 UTC 2 MIN

Sony's recent declaration on July 1st, stating that new PlayStation releases will transition to digital-only distribution starting January 2028, has deeply unsettled the gaming community. This pivotal change is set to position the upcoming PlayStation 6 as the company's inaugural console without a disc drive, fundamentally reshaping how players will acquire and retain their game libraries.

The immediate fallout from the announcement was a torrent of community dissent. Players swiftly voiced their strong negative feelings, with the widespread disapproval culminating in a petition launched by PNP Games CEO Jade Pearce. This petition, which calls for Sony to reconsider its strategy, has rapidly amassed over 170,000 signatures.

The situation intensified when PlayStation broke a six-day social media silence on July 7th. Rather than addressing the growing controversy, the platform posted an advertisement for a new wireless arcade fight stick. According to reports from IGN and Kotaku, this promotional tweet was immediately inundated with critical comments from users demanding clarity on the future of physical media.

At the heart of the community's frustration are core concerns regarding game ownership and long-term preservation. Gaming preservation advocates, including the "Does it Play" account, have publicly criticized Sony, reportedly labeling them a "spineless corpo" and urging them to reverse a decision they believe will "strip everyone of ownership over their games." This sentiment echoes remarks from an ex-PlayStation executive, who characterized Sony's move as "fairly dramatic," recalling a period when digital sales were virtually nonexistent.
