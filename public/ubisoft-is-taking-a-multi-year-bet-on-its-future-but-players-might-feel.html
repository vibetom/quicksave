Ubisoft Is Taking a Multi-Year Bet on Its Future, But Players Might Feel the Squeeze First

A net loss and anticipated 'low point' fiscal year are part of Ubisoft's plan for a 'strong rebound' by 2027, but their transformation comes with a cost: fewer releases and a smaller workforce.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 21, 2026 · 21:38 UTC 4 MIN

Ubisoft's latest financial report has landed, painting a clear picture of current struggles alongside a blueprint for a future rebound. The publisher reported a net loss of 161.3 million euros for the first half of fiscal year 2026, which concludes in March 2026. This period also saw a notable drop in revenue and net bookings, with declines of 21.8% and 17.4% respectively, when compared to the prior year.

The company is not shying away from these figures, acknowledging them as part of a larger, multi-year strategic transformation. Ubisoft has stated its commitment to completing this overhaul over the next financial year. They've also warned that the current fiscal year (FY26-27) is projected to mark the lowest point for their free cash flow, a period that will also bring a "softer release slate and restructuring costs."

For players, this means navigating a potentially quieter period. While the company repositions itself, fewer major titles might hit shelves, a direct consequence of the operational adjustments underway. This quiet spell is framed as a necessary step before a projected upswing.

As part of its restructuring efforts, Ubisoft has significantly reduced its operational footprint. The total number of employees was 16,590 at the close of March 2026, representing a reduction of approximately 1,200 individuals from the previous year. This streamlining extends to their finances, with the fixed cost base shrinking by 180 million euros (8%) compared to last year. These actions contribute to cumulative fixed cost savings that have now reached nearly 325 million euros since fiscal year 2022-23.

Despite the present challenges and an anticipated lean year, Ubisoft is forecasting a brighter horizon. They expect a "strong rebound" with positive free cash flow by fiscal year 2027-28, followed by "robust" free cash flow for fiscal year 2028-29. This forward-looking confidence is supported by an improved financial position on other fronts; non-IFRS net debt has significantly decreased to 187 million euros by the end of March 2026, a substantial improvement from 885 million euros a year earlier. This improvement was partly bolstered by an investment from Tencent.

Some critics, according to reports, point to the historical pattern of large profit and loss swings as an indicator of execution risk, tied heavily to the performance of major releases. However, Ubisoft is betting on this transformation to stabilize its trajectory. The company is actively working to reshape its structure and strategy, aiming for sustained financial health rather than a reliance on individual blockbusters. Players, for their part, will be watching to see if this strategic bet pays off, hoping for a return to both financial stability and a renewed stream of compelling games.
