EWC Hits Paris: What a New City Means For Esports' Biggest Tournament

The Esports World Cup is underway in Paris, signaling a major shift for the global competition. Here's a look at what to expect from the event's first outing beyond Saudi Arabia.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 04, 2026 · 15:39 UTC 3 MIN

The Esports World Cup (EWC) is currently unfolding in Paris, France, marking a significant milestone as the event's inaugural appearance outside of Saudi Arabia. From July 6 to August 23, the city of lights is hosting a global convergence of competitive gaming, shifting the EWC's landscape in a big way.

This year's event is a titan, gathering over 2,000 players from 200 clubs, representing 100 countries around the world. These elite competitors are battling across 25 tournaments, spanning 24 distinct games, turning Paris into a vibrant hub for digital athleticism.

The financial stakes are equally massive, with a combined prize pool of $75,000,000 up for grabs across the various tournaments. An additional $30,000,000 is earmarked for the Club Championship, underscoring the substantial investment poured into the competitive gaming ecosystem.

July is particularly hot with several headline games in full swing. The Dota 2 EWC, featuring a $2,000,000 prize pool, runs from July 6 to July 18. The Valorant EWC also boasts a $2,000,000 prize, with its group stage wrapping up July 7 and playoffs electrifying from July 9 to July 12. Fans recently saw Team Heretics achieve a 2-1 victory over All Gamers in a preliminary Group D match for Valorant on July 3. Not to be outdone, the League of Legends EWC, another $2,000,000 prize pool event, will challenge teams from July 13 to July 19.

For those keen to follow the pulse of the tournament, Esports Charts has launched an EWC Viewership Hub. This new resource provides real-time audience data, daily updates, and news, offering a transparent window into how many eyes are fixed on Paris's esports spectacle.
