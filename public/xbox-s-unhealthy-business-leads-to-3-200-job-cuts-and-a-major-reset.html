Xbox's 'Unhealthy' Business Leads To 3,200 Job Cuts And A Major Reset

CEO Asha Sharma confirms significant layoffs and a strategic pivot towards Game Pass, free-to-play, and mobile gaming as the company aims for a 'bigger future' beyond traditional consoles.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 07, 2026 · 16:45 UTC 3 MIN

Microsoft's Xbox division has announced a significant restructuring, including the elimination of 3,200 jobs over the next year, representing roughly 20% of its current staff. This move, reported by the Los Angeles Times on July 6, 2026, is part of a sweeping reorganization designed to revitalize the company's gaming business.

Xbox Chief Executive Asha Sharma addressed staff in a memo, stating, "Our business today is not healthy." Sharma detailed that Xbox is currently operating with margins "three to 10 times lower than comparable businesses," underscoring the urgency for a fundamental change. She emphasized the need to "reset Xbox" to forge a sustainable path forward.

This "reset" involves a clear strategic pivot away from a sole focus on console games. Instead, Xbox will prioritize its Game Pass subscription service, alongside free-to-play titles and mobile-driven programs, according to the Los Angeles Times and Tech News outlets. This shift acknowledges that prior expansions of Game Pass and general content did not meet the company's growth expectations.

Sharma conveyed to staff that these organizational adjustments are intended to secure a more expansive future for Xbox, rather than diminish its scope. She further clarified that Xbox plans to commit the same level of investment this year as it has in previous periods, but with a sharpened emphasis on precision, self-control, and clarity. This approach, she explained, is about Xbox "going where consumers are," signaling an adaptation to the broader gaming landscape.
