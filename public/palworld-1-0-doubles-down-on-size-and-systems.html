Palworld 1.0 Doubles Down on Size and Systems

The creature-collecting survival hit officially launches on July 10 with a massive new region, a dozen new Pals, and game-changing breeding and server features.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 20, 2026 · 18:54 UTC 3 MIN

Palworld, the survival game that captured the world's attention, officially reaches its 1.0 release on July 10, 2026. This isn't just a version number change; it's a colossal expansion bringing new content across multiple platforms for all players. We'll see the game arrive on personal computers via Steam and the Microsoft digital storefront, on Xbox Series X/S, Xbox One, PlayStation 5, and Mac systems.

Existing Palworld owners won't need to purchase anything, as the update will be free. Xbox Game Pass subscribers will also find the updated game included, making it easy for a broad audience to jump in. Best of all for those already invested, current save data will carry over, meaning no required restart to explore all the fresh additions.

The most striking new feature is the introduction of the World Tree region. This second major island is set to roughly double the playable map size, offering a vast new frontier for players to explore. Imagine the possibilities as an entirely new ecosystem of challenges, resources, and secrets unfolds.

Naturally, a new region means new inhabitants. A dozen fresh creatures are joining the world with the 1.0 update, expanding the roster of Pals to capture, befriend, and put to work. This influx of new companions adds further depth to the game's core loop of collection and progression.

Beyond exploration and collection, Palworld 1.0 is also upgrading its core systems. The Genetic Recombination breeding system is set to offer players intricate new ways to customize and optimize their Pals, allowing for more strategic progression. For those who enjoy playing with friends, dedicated servers are getting a significant boost with Server Clustering, designed to enhance the multiplayer experience for larger communities and more ambitious builds.

With its official launch, Palworld is clearly not resting on its early access laurels. The sheer scale of this update, from a dramatically expanded map to advanced breeding mechanics and improved server infrastructure, signals a strong commitment to evolving the game well beyond its initial viral success. Get ready to dive into a much bigger, more intricate world on July 10.
