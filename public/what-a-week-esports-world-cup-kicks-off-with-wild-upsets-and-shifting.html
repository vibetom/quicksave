What a Week! Esports World Cup Kicks Off With Wild Upsets and Shifting Viewer Tides

The inaugural week of the Esports World Cup 2026 in Paris delivered major shocks and unexpected viewership trends for games like Apex Legends and Valorant.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 13, 2026 · 21:28 UTC 3 MIN

The Esports World Cup (EWC) 2026 has thrown open its doors in Paris, France, and its opening week has been a whirlwind of unexpected outcomes and shifting digital tides. Running from July 8 to August 23, this global spectacle gathers over 2,000 elite players and 200 clubs from more than 100 countries. With a massive $75 million prize pool spread across 25 tournaments and 24 different games, the EWC is a true test of competitive mettle, and week one has proven just how unpredictable that test can be.

The early days of the tournament have already delivered some memorable moments. In a story that unfolded like a classic fighting game narrative, Kenta "mi2ha4" Ichihara made an emotional run through the FATAL FURY: City of the Wolves bracket. After a tense 5-4 semi-final victory, mi2ha4 ultimately secured the runner-up position, a testament to raw skill and determination on the big stage.

Team-based competition brought its own brand of drama, particularly in Apex Legends. Elite Esports EU appeared unstoppable, finishing first in the group stage, only to stumble dramatically in the Match Point Finals. Their 13th-place finish meant they missed out on championship eligibility, a harsh reminder that early leads don't always guarantee a spot at the top.

Beyond the competitive upsets, the EWC's first week has also revealed significant shifts in audience engagement. Apex Legends, for instance, experienced a remarkable surge in popularity, boasting a 74% peak viewership growth during the EWC opening week. This made it the game's most-watched event of the year, signaling a strong connection with the EWC platform.

However, not every title saw an increase. The Valorant EWC tournament, by contrast, saw a notable dip, with a 45.8% drop in peak concurrent viewership compared to its 2025 showing, according to Esports Charts. While still attracting 247,996 Peak Viewers, this decline highlights the dynamic and ever-changing nature of esports fan interest. The EWC 2026 is off to a dynamic start, showcasing both the thrilling unpredictability of competitive gaming through surprising upsets and significant shifts in game-specific viewership, highlighting the evolving landscape of esports popularity.
