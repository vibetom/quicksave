Miyazaki Joins Gaming's Elite: What His BAFTA Fellowship Means For Souls-likes

Hidetaka Miyazaki, the visionary behind *Dark Souls* and *Elden Ring*, is set to receive BAFTA's highest honor, acknowledging not just his genius but the genre he forged.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 21, 2026 · 11:28 UTC 3 MIN

Hidetaka Miyazaki, the creative force behind modern classics like Dark Souls and Elden Ring, will be honored with a BAFTA Fellowship, as announced on June 19, 2026. This prestigious award, the highest accolade BAFTA can bestow upon an individual in games, recognizes Miyazaki for his "groundbreaking work" and "immense influence" on the video game industry. It's a testament not just to his individual genius, but to the indelible mark his unique approach has left on the landscape of play.

Miyazaki's career is a string of titles that have redefined challenge and exploration. As President of FromSoftware, he directed games that became cultural touchstones, from the gothic horrors of Bloodborne to the intricate combat of Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice, and the sprawling, mythic majesty of Elden Ring. Each release has deepened a distinct design philosophy, captivating players with rich, fragmented narratives and demanding, rewarding gameplay.

But the reach of Miyazaki's "immense influence" extends far beyond his own studio's output. The "Souls-like" genre he helped popularize has become a cornerstone of modern gaming, inspiring countless developers to embrace challenging difficulty, intricate world-building, and a sense of hard-won triumph. This BAFTA Fellowship is a powerful validation of the genre's profound impact, signaling its cemented place in the pantheon of influential gaming experiences alongside other legendary styles.

Miyazaki now stands among an elite group of gaming visionaries who have received the BAFTA Fellowship. Previous recipients include industry titans like Shigeru Miyamoto, the creator of Mario and Zelda, Valve co-founder Gabe Newell, and the narrative pioneer Amy Hennig. His inclusion in such esteemed company underscores the lasting legacy of his work and the recognition of the 'Souls-like' approach as a pivotal force in the evolution of video games. This honor is not just for Miyazaki, but for the challenging, exploration-heavy, and often enigmatic design philosophy that he championed, ensuring its continued relevance and inspiration for years to come.
