This Week's Finals Prove Esports World Cup 2026 is Already Huge

The League of Legends and Dota 2 tournaments are wrapping up with multi-million dollar prizes, highlighting the massive scale of this year's record-setting global competition.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 15, 2026 · 16:00 UTC 2 MIN

The Esports World Cup 2026, a colossal event that kicked off in Paris on July 6 and will run through August 23, is already delivering on its promise of unprecedented scale. With a staggering overall prize pool of $75 million spread across 25 tournaments and 24 games, this year's EWC is a true landmark in competitive gaming. This week, two of its most anticipated tournaments, League of Legends and Dota 2, are heading for their grand finales, underscoring the high stakes and immense excitement.

On July 19, the spotlight will be squarely on the grand finals for both the League of Legends and Dota 2 EWC tournaments. While the EWC as a whole represents a new high-water mark for prize money, these two titans of esports are pulling their own weight. Each tournament boasts a substantial $2 million prize pool, a significant slice of the overall EWC fund. This individual game prize money is complemented by an additional $30 million allocated to the Club Championship, further emphasizing the depth of competition and reward.

The League of Legends tournament moved through its Group Stage from July 15-16, with Playoffs following on July 17-18, all culminating in Friday's championship clash. Similarly, the Dota 2 tournament is also expected to conclude with its grand final on July 19. These climactic moments are more than just individual game finales; they are vital pulse points for the entire Esports World Cup, showcasing the event's ambition to mark a "new global chapter" for competitive gaming. At its launch, the EWC announced plans to reach a global audience topping 640 million, signaling the event's broad aspirations for captivating and expanding the reach of esports.
