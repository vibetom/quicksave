What Xbox's Massive Layoffs Mean For Its Future

Thousands of jobs cut, four studios divested, and a direct acknowledgment of an 'unhealthy business' signal a painful but necessary recalibration for the console giant.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 14, 2026 · 15:55 UTC 3 MIN

Xbox has embarked on a significant restructuring, impacting thousands of employees across its gaming division. These widespread cuts represent approximately two percent of Microsoft's global workforce, with an estimated 3,200 gaming jobs slated for reduction over the coming fiscal year. The changes have touched long-tenured staff, including Kevin LaChapelle, who served as Vice President of Xbox Platform for 37 years, leading initiatives such as the backward compatibility program and cloud gaming team.

Alongside the job reductions, four game studios are departing the Xbox ecosystem. Compulsion Games and Double Fine Productions are transitioning to independent operations. Meanwhile, Ninja Theory and Undead Labs have entered into agreements to join new owners, marking a notable shift in Xbox's internal studio portfolio.

Specific development teams have experienced substantial workforce reductions. Id Software saw 136 developers laid off, Bethesda Game Studios in Austin cut 22 positions, and ZeniMax Online Studios reduced its staff by 213. Obsidian confirmed 52 layoffs, though reports suggest the actual number of workers impacted at Obsidian could be between 60 to 70. Further reports indicate particularly severe cuts at ZeniMax Online Studios, potentially affecting up to half the development team and altering the future roadmap for The Elder Scrolls Online.

Xbox CEO Asha Sharma directly addressed the rationale behind these profound changes, attributing them to an "increasingly challenging gaming landscape." Sharma also stated that Xbox's "business today is not healthy," operating at "significantly lower margins," clearly linking the layoffs to the company's financial performance.

Internal organizational adjustments are also part of this restructuring. Mojang Studios will now report directly to CEO Asha Sharma, indicating a more centralized operational approach. Additionally, Helen Chiang has been promoted to Xbox Chief Operating Officer, reflecting a revamped leadership structure.

The strategic shift is further underscored by reported project cancellations. A new online game from ZeniMax Online Studios, previously described as a blend of Destiny 2 and Cyberpunk 2077, was reportedly canceled for 2025, illustrating a move away from certain development initiatives amidst this period of consolidation.
