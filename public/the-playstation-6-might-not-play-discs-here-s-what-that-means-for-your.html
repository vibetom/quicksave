The PlayStation 6 Might Not Play Discs. Here's What That Means For Your Games.

Sony is ending disc-based game production by 2028, signaling an all-digital future for PlayStation that could reshape how you own and play games.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 01, 2026 · 16:43 UTC 3 MIN

Sony is set to stop producing disc-based PlayStation games by 2028, a move confirmed by the company and reported by GamesRadar+. This decision sends a clear signal about the direction of future PlayStation consoles and has sparked considerable conversation among players.

This shift strongly suggests that the next console, widely anticipated as the PlayStation 6, could launch around 2028 without a disc drive. IGN Southeast Asia has reported on this potential timeline, noting that such a change aligns with Sony's previous indications about not selling hardware at a significant loss. Removing a disc drive could be a strategic way to manage production costs, especially as digital sales continue to dominate the market.

Indeed, this decision reflects a broader industry trend where digital demand is increasingly overshadowing physical media, a point highlighted by Wccftech. For many internet users, this news has ignited a fresh "physical media defense arc," bringing long-standing debates about game ownership and preservation to the forefront. Players are once again weighing the benefits of physical discs against the convenience and growing prominence of digital libraries.

This isn't an isolated move for Sony. The company has also indicated plans to shut down the PlayStation Store for PS3 and Vita, a reversal of a previous decision. This broader digital-first strategy raises questions about the long-term accessibility of older titles and the overall landscape of game preservation. When content becomes exclusively digital, its longevity is tied to the storefronts that host it.

For players, the implications are significant. An all-digital future fundamentally alters how games are owned and shared. The iconic "This is How You Share Games on PS4" video, which once championed the ease of lending physical discs, feels like a relic in this evolving landscape. Players will likely become more reliant on digital storefronts, impacting everything from resale value to the ability to borrow games from friends. As PlayStation charts a course towards an exclusively digital horizon, the conversation around what it truly means to own a game continues to intensify.
