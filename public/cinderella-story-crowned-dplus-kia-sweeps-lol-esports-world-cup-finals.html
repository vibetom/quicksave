Cinderella Story Crowned: DPlus KIA Sweeps LoL Esports World Cup Finals

After two incredible underdog runs, DPlus KIA dominated Karmine Corp 3-0 in Paris, securing their first international title since 2020 and a massive prize.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 19, 2026 · 21:12 UTC 3 MIN

The Esports World Cup 2026 League of Legends Grand Final has concluded, and DPlus KIA emerged victorious, sweeping Karmine Corp with a commanding 3-0 performance on July 19, 2026. This triumph in Paris marks a significant return to form for DPlus KIA, crowning them champions of one of the year's most anticipated esports events.

Both finalists journeyed through the Playoffs on what many are calling "Cinderella Runs," defying expectations to reach the final stage. Karmine Corp, for their part, pulled off major upsets, first taking down the LPL powerhouse AG.AL in the quarterfinals, then sending home the formidable T1. DPlus KIA's path was equally impressive, as they eliminated Bilibili Gaming before halting Gen.G's quest for a third consecutive EWC title in a high-stakes semifinal.

The Grand Final itself was a display of DPlus KIA's strategic aggression and skill. The third game stood out as a clear example of their dominance, with the team securing early kills and overwhelming Karmine Corp in the laning phase. This aggressive playstyle resulted in a decisive 23-5 kill lead in a swift 27-minute match, cementing their supremacy. Following their stellar performance, Smash was deservedly named the series MVP.

This victory is particularly sweet for DPlus KIA and their mid-laner Showmaker, as it represents their first international title since 2020. Their hard-fought win earned them a substantial US$600,000 prize from the League of Legends tournament's US$2 million prize pool. Karmine Corp, despite their second-place finish, also walked away with a significant US$340,000.

The Esports World Cup 2026, hosted in Paris from July 6 to August 23, 2026, continues to unfold as a landmark event in competitive gaming. Boasting an unprecedented US$75 million prize pool distributed across 25 tournaments and 24 game titles, it underscores the escalating scale and professionalization of the esports world.
