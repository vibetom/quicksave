Xbox Shuts Down Hi-Fi Rush Studio Tango Gameworks, Realigns Others

Microsoft announced the closure of multiple Xbox-owned studios, including the beloved team behind Hi-Fi Rush, raising questions about the future of creative development under Xbox.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 05, 2026 · 21:32 UTC 3 MIN

Microsoft has confirmed the closure of several Xbox-owned development studios, a significant move that includes the shutdown of Tango Gameworks, Arkane Austin, and Alpha Dog Games. Additionally, Roundhouse Studios will merge into ZeniMax Online Studios, the team behind The Elder Scrolls Online.

Matt Booty, head of Xbox Game Studios, conveyed these decisions internally via email. The closures are positioned as a strategic effort to "prioritize high-impact titles" and to "further invest in Bethesda's portfolio." This refocusing suggests a tightened scope for future projects within the Xbox ecosystem.

The shutdown of Tango Gameworks has particularly caught the industry's eye. Known for its work on The Evil Within series, Tango Gameworks also delivered Hi-Fi Rush in January 2023. Hi-Fi Rush received widespread critical acclaim and became a surprise hit for Xbox Game Pass, demonstrating a unique creative spark that resonated with players.

Arkane Austin, the studio responsible for Prey and Dishonored, will also close. This follows the release of Redfall in May 2023, a title that faced significant criticism and did not meet commercial expectations. Mobile developer Alpha Dog Games, known for Mighty Doom, is also being shut down. Meanwhile, the integration of Roundhouse Studios into ZeniMax Online Studios will see its talent contribute to The Elder Scrolls Online.

These organizational changes affect numerous employees across the studios, who will receive severance packages and support in seeking new opportunities. This restructuring follows Microsoft's substantial acquisition of Activision Blizzard King for $69 billion in October 2023. According to IGN, this move is widely seen as part of a broader trend of layoffs and industry-wide restructuring, including within Microsoft's Xbox division, signaling ongoing shifts in the gaming landscape.
