Xbox Teases 'Something Big,' And It Could Change How You Play Your Old Discs

An Xbox program lead's cryptic message has insiders buzzing about a long-rumored disc-to-digital program, which could offer players more flexibility with their game collections.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 13, 2026 · 16:46 UTC 3 MIN

Xbox players are on the edge of their seats following a mysterious tease from the company's program lead, Brad Rossetti. In a message sent to Xbox Insiders, Rossetti hinted at a "something big" coming "next week," adding cryptically, "The wait is worth the wait, I promise." The gaming community has taken this as a strong indication that a long-rumored disc-to-digital program might finally be on its way.

Industry speculation is particularly focused on this possibility. Jez Corden, an editor at Windows Central, who has previously reported on such a feature, fueled the rumors with a tweet stating, "positron cometh." "Positron" is widely believed to be the codename for this much-anticipated disc-to-digital initiative.

According to various reports, this program would allow users to convert their physical game discs into digital licenses. Some of these reports suggest that this could fall under the existing "Xbox Play Anywhere" framework, giving players the option to seamlessly transition their game libraries from physical to digital ownership.

This potential move from Xbox arrives at a crucial time for the industry. With a growing shift towards digital-only platforms, and Sony's confirmed plans to stop manufacturing PlayStation discs by 2028, players are increasingly looking for ways to preserve their existing physical game collections. A disc-to-digital program would offer a bridge, allowing gamers to future-proof their libraries and maintain access to their titles in an evolving landscape.

The implications for players could be significant, offering greater flexibility and convenience. We at QUICKSAVE will be keeping a close eye on this development and will provide full details as soon as the official announcement drops next week.
