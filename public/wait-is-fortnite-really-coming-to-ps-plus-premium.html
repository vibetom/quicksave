Wait, Is Fortnite Really Coming To PS Plus Premium?

New reports suggest Epic's battle royale behemoth could join Sony's top-tier subscription, but how would a free game fit into a paid catalog?

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 11, 2026 · 12:12 UTC 2 MIN

Epic Games' colossal battle royale, Fortnite, is reportedly making a significant leap onto PlayStation Plus Premium's Game Catalog. This move, widely reported across various gaming news outlets, hints at a potential strategic pivot for Epic, exploring new avenues beyond its established free-to-play model.

The reported inclusion would place Fortnite squarely within the Game Catalog offered to PlayStation Plus Premium subscribers. This is distinct from the regular exclusive in-game content packs that PlayStation Plus members already receive for Fortnite, suggesting a deeper integration into the top-tier subscription offering.

Here's where things get interesting: the precise mechanics of how a free-to-play title like Fortnite would integrate into a paid subscription service's catalog remain a puzzle. Rumors are circulating about the potential benefits for subscribers, which could range from exclusive cosmetic bundles to V-bucks, or even direct access to the Battle Pass. The details are still unconfirmed, leaving us to wonder what kind of value proposition this could unlock for players.

It's worth noting that Fortnite already has a foot in the door with PlayStation's premium services. The game currently supports PS5 cloud streaming, a feature available to Premium subscribers. This pre-existing connection between Fortnite and the highest tier of PlayStation Plus indicates a foundation for this reported expansion. If this report holds true, it could signal an intriguing evolution in how free-to-play giants partner with platform subscription services, potentially reshaping how players access and enjoy these massive titles.
