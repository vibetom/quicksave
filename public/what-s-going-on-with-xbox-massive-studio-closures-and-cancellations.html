What's Going On With Xbox? Massive Studio Closures And Cancellations Reported

Reports indicate Xbox is undergoing a significant restructuring, with multiple studios facing closure and high-profile projects like *Marvel's Blade* on the chopping block, while Xbox says it's focusing on "highest priorities."

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 30, 2026 · 21:53 UTC 3 MIN

A wave of reports suggests Xbox may be navigating a period of significant upheaval, potentially involving widespread studio closures and project cancellations. According to GameSpot, Xbox is reportedly considering the shutdown of "at least five studios," a move that would represent a considerable restructuring of its internal development teams.

Among the projects reportedly under threat is Arkane Lyon's highly anticipated Marvel's Blade. GameSpot's reporting indicates that the game could face cancellation, potentially alongside the closure of Arkane Lyon itself. This would be a profound shift for the studio behind critical darlings like Deathloop and Dishonored.

The reported funding purge extends beyond Arkane Lyon, with Wccftech reporting that IO Interactive's online fantasy RPG has also been impacted. However, not all high-profile projects are reportedly on the chopping block; Hideo Kojima's OD is reportedly safe from these cuts, according to Wccftech.

These reported changes aren't limited to internal development. Wccftech also indicates that Xbox is reportedly freezing new third-party deals for Xbox Game Pass. If accurate, this could signal a strategic shift in how Xbox plans to curate and expand its Game Pass library, potentially focusing more on first-party titles or existing partnerships rather than bringing in new external games.

In response to these developments, Xbox has offered an official statement, confirming its focus on "highest priorities." However, the company also states it is not cutting back overall spending on games. This suggests a strategic reallocation of resources rather than a total reduction in investment in the gaming sector.

As these reports circulate, the industry is watching closely. Such widespread changes inevitably impact the talented developers and teams involved. The human cost of studio closures and project cancellations is always substantial, and our thoughts are with everyone potentially affected by these reported decisions. We will continue to monitor these developments and report on new information as it emerges.
