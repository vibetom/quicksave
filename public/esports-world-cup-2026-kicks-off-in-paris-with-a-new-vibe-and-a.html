Esports World Cup 2026 Kicks Off in Paris With a New Vibe and a Staggering Prize Pool

The first international EWC brings 25 tournaments, new games, and a $75 million prize pool to Paris, setting the stage for a massive global esports spectacle.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 08, 2026 · 21:38 UTC 3 MIN

The Esports World Cup (EWC) has officially begun its 2026 journey, staking its claim in Paris from July 6th through August 23rd at the Paris Expo Porte de Versailles. This iteration marks a significant shift for the tournament, moving beyond its previous home in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, to embrace a global stage for the first time.

This year’s event arrives with an unprecedented $75 million prize pool, a sum that shatters previous records. This colossal bounty will be distributed across 25 tournaments, encompassing 24 distinct games. The sheer scale is staggering, drawing in more than 2,000 players and 200 clubs from over 100 countries, all converging to compete for glory and a piece of that substantial prize.

The tournament’s expanded game lineup is a testament to its ambition, featuring both familiar faces and fresh additions. Enthusiasts can look forward to new competitive avenues in titles like Trackmania, a unique Fortnite in its Reload mode (part of a multi-year partnership with Epic Games), Call of Duty: Black Ops 7, and EA Sports FC 26. Beyond the main competition, players and fans can anticipate content reveals, with the Rainbow Six Siege team slated to unveil details from the upcoming Year 11 Season 3 during the EWC 2026.

In a move reflecting a broader commitment to diversity, Mobile Legends: Bang Bang will host not only a Mid-Season Cup but also a dedicated Women's Invitational. This highlights a growing push for more inclusive competitive spaces within the esports ecosystem.

The early reception from fans has been robust, with organizers announcing that 100,000 tickets had already been sold as of July 8th. This strong initial engagement suggests that the EWC's pivot to an international locale and its expanded offerings are resonating with a global audience, signaling a vibrant future for competitive gaming on one of its biggest stages yet.
