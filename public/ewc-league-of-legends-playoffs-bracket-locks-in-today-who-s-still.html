EWC League of Legends: Playoffs Bracket Locks In Today, Who's Still Standing?

After intense Group Stage battles, eight teams will secure their spot in the Playoffs for the $2 million prize pool, while others face elimination. Here's a look at the teams fighting for survival.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 16, 2026 · 18:38 UTC 3 MIN

The Esports World Cup 2026's League of Legends tournament is shifting into high gear as its Group Stage draws to a close today, July 16, 2026. By the end of the day, the complete eight-team Playoffs bracket will be set, with half of the initial contenders advancing and the other half heading home.

Already, four teams have had their EWC journey cut short. Furia from Group A, Team Secret and Movistar KOI from Group B, and Lyon from Group D have all been eliminated after succumbing to two consecutive losses in the Group Stage. Their aspirations for the $2 million prize pool have been dashed, leaving the remaining teams to battle for supremacy.

Today's schedule is packed with crucial matchups that will determine who earns a coveted spot in the next stage. The Upper Bracket Finals are set to deliver direct qualifiers, pitting G2 Esports against AG.AL in Group A, Sentinels against Gen.G Esports in Group B, Bilibili Gaming against T1 in Group C, and Hanwha Life against JD Gaming in Group D. The victors of these clashes will punch their ticket straight to the Playoffs.

For those who stumble in the Upper Bracket Finals, or for teams still fighting their way through the lower brackets, the Lower Bracket Finals offer a final chance at redemption. These high-stakes matches will see the loser of Group A's Upper Bracket Final face Dplus, the loser of Group B's Upper Bracket Final go up against Karmine Corp, the loser of Group C's Upper Bracket Final challenge GAM Esports, and the loser of Group D's Upper Bracket Final contend with MIBR.LOS. These games are do-or-die, determining the last teams to round out the Playoffs roster.

The League of Legends tournament at the Esports World Cup 2026 is designed for speed, compressing its entire run into a tight five-day window. This rapid format ensures constant action and high-pressure moments as teams vie for a share of the substantial $2 million prize pool. The tournament is a key part of the larger Esports World Cup 2026, which is unfolding in Paris, France, from July 6 to August 23, 2026, boasting an colossal $75 million total prize pool across all its featured titles. The event encompasses 25 tournaments across 24 games, featuring more than 2,000 players, 200 clubs, and 100 countries, all competing under the EWC's "Club Championship" format, which pits entire organizations against each other for global esports dominance.
