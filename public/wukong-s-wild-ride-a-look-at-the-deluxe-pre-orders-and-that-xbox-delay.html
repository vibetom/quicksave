Wukong's Wild Ride: A Look At The Deluxe Pre-Orders And That Xbox Delay

Black Myth: Wukong is open for pre-orders, with a staggering array of editions and a puzzling year-long wait for Xbox players. What's the deal?

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 19, 2026 · 12:12 UTC 3 MIN

Black Myth: Wukong, the action RPG steeped in Chinese mythology and inspired by the classic novel Journey to the West, has captivated the gaming world. Players will step into the shoes of the "Destined One," a staff-wielding monkey on a quest to retrieve six relics linked to Sun Wukong's six senses. The immense anticipation is clear: by May 2024, it became Steam's most-wishlisted title, climbing to the top of Steam's global best-sellers chart by early August.

Anticipation will pay off on August 20, 2024, for PC (Windows) and PlayStation 5 players. However, the excitement is muted for Xbox Series X/S owners, who face a significant wait. The Xbox version is slated for release a full year later, on August 20, 2025. This substantial platform disparity has certainly sparked conversation among the community.

For those ready to dive in, pre-orders are live on platforms like Steam and the PlayStation Store. Early adopters can snag the "Trailblazer's Scarlet Gourd" in-game item as a bonus. The game arrives in four flavors: Standard, Digital Deluxe, a physical PC-exclusive Deluxe Edition, and a physical PC-exclusive Collector's Edition.

The Digital Deluxe Edition offers a suite of in-game cosmetics, including the Bronzecloud Staff, Folk Opera Armor Set, and Wind Chimes, alongside a digital soundtrack. However, the physical editions are where things get truly interesting – and expensive. The Deluxe Edition for PC, priced at $169, includes a steelbook case, a wind chime necklace, a gold sun crow pin, and a silk scroll.

Climbing to the apex, the Collector's Edition commands a $400 price tag, featuring a substantial 40cm "Confront Destiny" figurine, among other premium physical collectibles. It's worth noting that these physical editions, whether Deluxe or Collector's, include a code in a box rather than a traditional physical disc, a detail that might surprise some expecting a disc to go along with their high-value purchase.

While the journey to launch has seen the game become a wishlist darling, it hasn't been without its bumps. Reports in 2023 surfaced regarding accusations of in-studio sexism, adding another layer to the game's public narrative as it approaches its staggered release.
