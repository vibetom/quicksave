Cyberpunk 2077 Hits 40 Million Sales: What It Means for Night City's Future

CD Projekt Red's latest milestone solidifies the game's comeback and sets the stage for Project Orion, Edgerunners Season 2, and more.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 05, 2026 · 15:44 UTC 3 MIN

Night City just keeps getting bigger. CD Projekt Red announced on July 3, 2026, that Cyberpunk 2077 has now sold over 40 million copies worldwide. This impressive figure includes both the base game and the comprehensive Ultimate Edition bundle, painting a clear picture of the title's enduring appeal.

This latest surge of 5 million units since November 2025 demonstrates Cyberpunk 2077's continued momentum, nearly three years after its initial launch. The game's widespread availability across platforms like PC, PlayStation, Xbox, PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X|S, and even the Nintendo Switch 2 has undoubtedly broadened its reach, cementing its place among the top 20 best-selling video games ever made.

For CD Projekt Red, this milestone isn't just about past performance; it's a solid springboard for what's next. Joint-CEO Michał Nowakowski highlighted that these sales provide a "great foundation for our upcoming projects in this universe." This vision includes the highly anticipated sequel, currently code-named Project Orion, which, as of April 2026, already has a dedicated team of 163 developers bringing it to life.

The Cyberpunk universe is also poised for further expansion beyond games. Fans can look forward to the return of the acclaimed anime series, with Cyberpunk: Edgerunners Season 2 expected to drop in Fall 2026. This multi-pronged approach, leveraging both gaming and animated storytelling, suggests a robust, long-term strategy for the franchise.

This continued success isn't just a testament to Cyberpunk 2077's journey, but a strong signal that Night City is far from finished. With a sequel in development and further narrative exploration on the horizon, the future of the Cyberpunk franchise looks brighter than ever.
