Ubisoft Restructures Again: What We Know About Studio Closures and Job Cuts

For the third time this year, Ubisoft has announced significant changes, closing two studios and impacting nearly 400 positions. We break down the confirmed details.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 11, 2026 · 19:20 UTC 2 MIN

Ubisoft is undergoing another substantial internal reorganization, marked by the closure of two development studios and a significant number of job reductions across its global operations. This marks the third time this year that the publisher has announced such changes.

Ubisoft Winnipeg and Ubisoft Belgrade are closing their doors, directly affecting the teams housed within these two distinct studio identities. For Winnipeg, this represents the conclusion of its role in the larger Ubisoft development network, while Belgrade's closure similarly disbands its contributors to various projects.

In total, approximately 380 jobs are being cut across Ubisoft as part of this latest strategic shift, according to reporting by Daily Gaming News. These reductions span various departments and functions within the company, impacting a broad swathe of its workforce. Beyond outright closures, Ubisoft Barcelona is also undergoing restructuring, indicating broader changes to its operational model and team compositions.

This series of organizational adjustments at Ubisoft occurs against a backdrop of similar movements within the wider gaming industry. For example, Xbox is reportedly planning major layoffs of its own. An internal memo from Sharma, cited by Video Games Chronicle, stated a clear need to "reassess our priorities," suggesting that resource allocation and strategic focus are points of consideration across multiple major industry players right now.

For Ubisoft, these repeated organizational shifts underscore a continuous process of recalibration. Each round of layoffs and studio closures means a change in professional landscapes for hundreds of individuals, and a redefinition of the company's development footprint.
