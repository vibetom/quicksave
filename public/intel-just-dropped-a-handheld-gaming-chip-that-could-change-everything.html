Intel Just Dropped a Handheld Gaming Chip That Could Change Everything

Early benchmarks show Intel's Arc G3 Extreme processor significantly outperforming AMD's top handheld chip, signaling a new era for portable PC gaming.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 10, 2026 · 18:56 UTC 3 MIN

For nearly three years, the Windows gaming handheld market has largely been a domain of AMD processors. Devices like the ROG Ally and the Legion Go have relied on AMD Ryzen chips, establishing the company as a dominant force in portable PC gaming. Now, Intel is stepping into the arena with its first-ever chip designed exclusively for handhelds, and it's already making waves.

Intel has officially introduced the Arc G3 Extreme, a new processor crafted for handheld gaming as a component of its Core Ultra Series 3 "Panther Lake" family. This isn't just a laptop chip repurposed for a smaller device; it's a ground-up design tailored for the unique demands of handheld gaming.

The Arc G3 Extreme packs a punch, combining a 14-core CPU capable of speeds up to 4.70GHz with Intel's integrated Arc B390 GPU, which features 12 Xe3 cores. These are serious specifications aimed at delivering robust performance in a compact form factor.

The first device to feature this new silicon will be the Acer Predator Atlas 8, which Acer unveiled on May 28, 2026. This means players won't have to wait long to see the Arc G3 Extreme in action.

What truly sets this launch apart, however, are the early performance indicators. According to reports from independent lab benchmarks conducted at Computex 2026, Intel's new chip significantly outperformed its primary competitor. In matched-hardware testing at identical 35-watt power limits across eight different games, early results indicate the Arc G3 Extreme surpassed AMD's premier Ryzen Z2 Extreme by an average of 41 percent. This kind of lead, if it holds up in broader testing, could reshape the landscape of handheld gaming.

This isn't just a new product; it's a direct challenge to AMD's long-standing dominance in a rapidly growing market. With the Arc G3 Extreme, Intel is signaling its intent to be a major player, offering consumers a powerful new option for their portable gaming experiences.
