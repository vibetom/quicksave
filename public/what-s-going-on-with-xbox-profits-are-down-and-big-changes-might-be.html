What's Going On With Xbox? Profits Are Down, And Big Changes Might Be Coming

Microsoft's CEO has voiced concerns about Xbox's profitability, sparking rumors of a platform spin-off and a push for faster releases of beloved titles like Fallout and Elder Scrolls.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 14, 2026 · 16:02 UTC 2 MIN

A tremor is running through the Xbox ecosystem. Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella recently opened up about profitability, noting a particular concern that "more monetization of Xbox games [is] happening on YouTube." It's a candid observation that puts a spotlight on the platform's financial performance.

Adding to the conversation, reports from Kotaku suggest that some within Xbox acknowledge a growing challenge in defining the platform's distinct appeal. An Xbox leader reportedly indicated that explaining the platform's unique draw has become challenging, implying that a broader release strategy for games might have lessened its singular identity.

The discussions around Xbox's future extend to its very structure. Wccftech reports that Microsoft is weighing significant strategic shifts, including the possibility of spinning off Xbox entirely, exploring a sale, or even establishing a joint venture. This potential corporate restructuring could reshape the landscape for both players and developers.

Yet, even as these major considerations loom, there's a simultaneous drive to double down on core strengths. Alongside the potential for structural change, Microsoft is reportedly pushing to accelerate the release schedule for highly anticipated titles like Fallout and Elder Scrolls. Wccftech further reports that an overhaul led by Asha Sharma is specifically targeting the Fallout series, with Obsidian's team behind The Outer Worlds reportedly viewed as an ideal fit to contribute to this renewed franchise focus. This signals a clear intent to leverage beloved intellectual property, regardless of the platform's eventual form.

The coming months could prove pivotal for Xbox, as Microsoft navigates these profitability concerns and considers fundamental changes to its gaming division.
