Esports World Cup Kicks Off In Paris With Record Prize Pool

The Esports World Cup 2026 has officially begun in the City of Lights, boasting a massive $75M prize pool and a new focus on club organizations.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 03, 2026 · 13:19 UTC 3 MIN

The Esports World Cup (EWC) 2026 officially began on July 8, marked by an opening ceremony at La Seine Musicale in Paris. While the grand opening set the celebratory tone, the tournament action itself commenced earlier, with some competitions already delivering early thrills. This year signifies a major shift for the event, relocating from Saudi Arabia to the vibrant French capital, signaling a new chapter for competitive gaming.

Underscoring the scale of this new era, the EWC 2026 boasts a combined prize pool of $75 million, setting a new record for the tournament. An additional $30 million has been earmarked specifically for the Club Championship, highlighting the event's intentional pivot to emphasize club organizations over national teams.

This year's EWC is a vast undertaking, featuring 25 tournaments across 24 different games. Over 2,000 players from 200 clubs, representing more than 100 countries, are participating. The full event schedule runs from July 6 to August 23, with several marquee games kicking off intense competition throughout July.

Fans are already tracking major titles like Dota 2, which runs from July 6-18, and Valorant, scheduled from July 9-12. League of Legends will take center stage from July 13-19, with Free Fire following from July 15-18. Early Valorant matches, which began on July 2nd, have already seen decisive outcomes: 100 Thieves swept Rex Regum Qeon 2-0, Team Vitality dominated Karmine Corp with a 2-0 sweep, and BBL Esports secured a 2-1 victory over EDward Gaming.

Beyond the intense competition, the EWC 2026 is also cultivating a full festival experience. The opening ceremony on July 8 was a spectacle, featuring performances by acclaimed artists like DJ Snake, Aya Nakamura, Theodora, and Mosimann. The event also fosters community engagement with an exclusive Valorant showmatch pitting French creators Inoxtag and Squeezie against each other. Furthermore, the Esports Foundation Creator Program offers approved co-streamers the chance to earn rewards and expand their audience by sharing the tournament action.
