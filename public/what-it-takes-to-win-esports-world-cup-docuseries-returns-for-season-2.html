What It Takes To Win: Esports World Cup Docuseries Returns for Season 2

Emmy-winning filmmaker R.J. Cutler is back to peel back the curtain on the lives of esports competitors chasing a massive prize pool, dropping June 26 on Prime Video.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 21, 2026 · 16:14 UTC 3 MIN

The curtain is set to rise once again on the human stories behind competitive gaming, as the Esports Foundation announced the return of 'Esports World Cup: Level Up' for its second season. All five episodes of the docuseries will land on Prime Video on June 26, 2026, offering a deep dive into the 2025 competition.

At the helm for this return is Emmy-winning filmmaker R.J. Cutler, a name recognized for his previous work on acclaimed documentaries such as 'Martha' on Netflix and Apple TV's 'Billie Eilish: The World's A Little Blurry.' Cutler brings his signature character-driven narrative style, ensuring that the series goes beyond mere gameplay to explore the lives that fuel the competition.

'Esports World Cup: Level Up' Season 2 transports viewers to Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, during the seven-week run of the 2025 Esports World Cup. The series meticulously follows players, their clubs, and even their families, offering an intimate look at the immense pressure and ambition that define their journey. It's a stark reminder that behind every competitive match lies a tapestry of personal stakes.

Among the featured competitors are Jake "Boaster" Howlett from Fnatic, a prominent figure in the VALORANT scene, and Vivi "Vivian" Indrawaty of Team Vitality, known for her prowess in MLBB. Their stories, alongside others, will showcase the relentless pursuit of victory within a competition that dangled a formidable $70 million prize pool and the coveted EWC Club Championship.

The docuseries is a production of This Machine, a division of Sony Pictures Television, with Cutler himself returning as director alongside showrunner John Dorsey. The executive production team, which includes Jane Cha Cutler, Trevor Smith, Elise Pearlstein, and Mark Blatty, is also back, ensuring continuity in the storytelling that resonated with audiences in the first season.

While Season 2 reflects on the 2025 events, it also sets the stage for the future. The 2026 edition of the Esports World Cup is slated to take place in Paris, France, from July 6 through August 23. This upcoming tournament is poised to be even grander, boasting an eye-watering $75 million prize pool, underscoring the ever-expanding scale and prestige of global esports.
