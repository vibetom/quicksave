Don't Hold Your Breath: The Steam Deck 2 Isn't Coming Anytime Soon

Valve engineers confirm a Steam Deck 2 is planned, but they're waiting for a 'generational leap' in hardware while prioritizing other projects.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 28, 2026 · 21:36 UTC 3 MIN

If you've been refreshing pages for news about the Steam Deck 2, it's time to settle back into your chair. While Valve has confirmed that a successor to their popular handheld PC is indeed in the pipeline, players shouldn't expect it to land on their laps anytime soon. The company is charting a patient course, waiting for the right moment to make its next big move.

Pierre-Loup Griffais, a Valve engineer, recently offered an update on the Steam Deck 2's status, indicating that while progress has been made since previous discussions, the team hasn't yet identified a truly 'generational leap' in handheld chipsets. This critical hardware jump is what Valve is looking for to justify a completely new model, ensuring a significant upgrade over the current Steam Deck rather than an incremental refresh.

Valve's attention is currently divided among other new hardware ventures. The company recently launched its Steam Machine, and the Steam Frame VR headset is slated to arrive later in 2026. These projects are taking center stage for now, suggesting that a concentrated effort on the Steam Deck 2 remains a future endeavor, not an immediate one.

This means the original Steam Deck isn't going anywhere. It will continue to serve as Valve's flagship portable gaming device for the foreseeable future, a testament to its robust design and ongoing support. For those contemplating a purchase, the message is clear: the current model still has plenty of road left.

Interestingly, rumors have circulated since early June 2026 about a potential shift in architecture for the Steam Deck 2, moving away from the current AMD x86 system towards an ARM-based system-on-a-chip. However, these remain just rumors, with no official confirmation from Valve. For now, the focus is on patience and the promise of a truly significant upgrade down the line.
