Intel Unveils Arc G-Series Processors, Ushering in a New Era for Handheld PC Gaming

New Arc G-Series chips are officially designed from the ground up for portable power, arriving in devices next year.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 13, 2026 · 21:44 UTC 3 MIN

Intel has officially pulled back the curtain on its new Arc G-Series processors, chips engineered specifically for handheld gaming PCs. The announcement, a highlight of Computex 2026, signals a significant evolution in portable PC gaming, moving past adapted desktop or laptop components towards hardware purpose-built for the form factor.

These new processors are not just a slight tweak; they represent a dedicated push into the handheld arena. At their core, the Arc G-Series processors feature up to Intel Arc B390 graphics, a robust setup for on-the-go gaming. Processing power comes from a unique configuration combining two P-cores, eight E-cores, and four LPE cores, all built on the advanced Intel 18A process node technology. This combination is designed to deliver a balance of performance and efficiency crucial for a portable device.

Beyond raw processing, Intel is integrating a suite of features aimed at enhancing the overall handheld experience. Connectivity gets a major boost with integrated Intel Wi-Fi 7 R2 and dual Bluetooth 6. For high-speed data transfer and expanded peripheral support, the chips include Intel Thunderbolt

4, offering up to 40 Gbps bandwidth and supporting Thunderbolt Share for seamless device interaction.

Loading games will also see an improvement, thanks to Intel Precompiled Shaders. This technology promises faster game launches by allowing handheld systems to download prebuilt shader files directly from the Intel cloud for supported titles. It's a smart play to cut down on waiting times, letting players jump into the action quicker.

The industry is already lining up behind Intel's new venture. Major OEM partners are preparing to launch devices powered by these Arc G-Series processors. Gamers can look forward to new handhelds like Acer's Predator Atlas 8, MSI's Claw 8 EX AI+, and offerings from OneXPlayer. These systems are slated to begin rolling out from June 2026, with wider availability anticipated throughout the rest of the year. This move by Intel could indeed reshape the competitive landscape of handheld PC gaming, offering a fresh foundation for the next generation of portable powerhouses.
