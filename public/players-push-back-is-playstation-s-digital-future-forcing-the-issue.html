Players Push Back: Is PlayStation's Digital Future Forcing The Issue?

A petition against PlayStation discontinuing physical discs has exploded past 120,000 signatures, showing a clear rift between Sony's 'natural direction' and player demand for choice.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jul 06, 2026 · 19:14 UTC 2 MIN

PlayStation's announcement to cease physical game disc production for new releases beginning in January 2028 has ignited a significant response from the gaming community. Sony Interactive Entertainment framed this shift as a 'natural direction,' citing consumer trends where digital media preference 'significantly outpaces physical discs.' After this date, new titles will only be accessible through the PlayStation Store or as digital codes from retailers. However, games already available or releasing before January 2028 will remain unaffected in their disc format.

In the wake of this declaration, an online petition launched by PNP Games' Jade Pearce on Change.org has rapidly gained momentum. As of July 6, 2026, the petition has amassed 124,149 signatures, with new supporters joining every few seconds, according to Push Square. This surge in signatures underscores a strong undercurrent of player concern regarding the impending change.

Supporters of the petition argue that the elimination of physical media will restrict consumer choice, negatively impact local economies, and grant a select few platform holders complete authority over how players access and own their purchased games. The petition articulates a clear demand for the preservation of physical game options.

While PlayStation has positioned this move as an adaptation to evolving market preferences, the robust response to the petition illustrates a growing tension between corporate strategy and community values. The company's commitment to a digital-first future is also reflected in its operational changes; Sony's largest disc-printing factory is already being repurposed, signaling a definitive move away from physical media. This development further implies that future hardware, such as the presumed PlayStation 6, may not include disc drive support, solidifying the digital trajectory despite ongoing player resistance.
