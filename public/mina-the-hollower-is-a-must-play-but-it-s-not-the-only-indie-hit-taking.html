Mina the Hollower is a Must-Play, But It's Not The Only Indie Hit Taking Over 2026

Yacht Club Games' latest smash is a 'Game of the Year' contender right out of the gate, leading an impressive charge of indie titles making waves this year.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 12, 2026 · 18:14 UTC 4 MIN

Yacht Club Games, the studio that delivered the beloved Shovel Knight, has once again struck gold with their latest release, Mina the Hollower. This new title has immediately captured the gaming world's attention, launching with a stellar 91 Metacritic score and earning the coveted "Metacritic Must-Play" award within its first week. Critics are already hailing it as one of 2026's top indie games, firmly planting it in early "Game of the Year" discussions.

While sharing the retro spirit of its predecessor, Mina the Hollower carves its own path. This project began as a smaller endeavor by Yacht Club Games developer Alec Faulkner before it grew into a major release. The game shifts away from the platforming focus of Shovel Knight, instead diving deep into exploration and action-adventure. Many are likening this evolution to Yacht Club's "Zelda to Shovel Knight's Mario," showcasing a fresh direction for the acclaimed developer.

Mina the Hollower's immediate success isn't an isolated incident; it's a testament to a broader trend. Industry observers are already noting that 2026 is shaping up to be a remarkable period for independent games, with some suggesting it might even eclipse 2025's strong indie lineup. It seems the indie scene is not just thriving, but actively setting new benchmarks for creativity and quality.

Beyond Mina, the indie landscape of 2026 is rich with diverse offerings. Titles like Tombwater, a 2D Western soulslike, and Hermit and Pig, a story-rich RPG with a perfect 100% user rating on Steam, have already made their mark. The visual novel genre also sees a notable entry with Schrödinger's Call, according to Green Man Gaming Blog.

The momentum shows no sign of slowing, with an exciting slate of indie games on the horizon for June 2026. PC Gamer reports upcoming releases such as Solarpunk, a co-op survival game set on floating islands, and Cursemark, a dark action roguelike entering Early Access. Fighting game fans can look forward to Invincible VS, a 3v3 tag fighter based on the animated series, while Dungeon Clawler offers a unique blend of deckbuilding and claw machine mechanics. For those who enjoy a darker narrative, Directive 8020 brings sci-fi survival horror to The Dark Pictures Anthology. Meanwhile, Wax Heads promises a narrative-driven life sim set in a record store.

Even more quietly, Milo: Paws & Buoys, a cozy puzzle game starring an animal protagonist, is slated for a June 18 release on Windows. Its initial visibility has largely been driven by achievement trackers rather than extensive marketing, as reported by TrueAchievements. This wide array of innovative and compelling titles reinforces the idea that indie games are not merely filling gaps but are increasingly leading the charge in gaming innovation and player engagement.
