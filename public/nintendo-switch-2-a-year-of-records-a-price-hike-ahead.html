Nintendo Switch 2: A Year of Records, A Price Hike Ahead

Nintendo's latest console has shattered sales milestones in its first year, but a $50 price increase could change its trajectory starting this September.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 26, 2026 · 16:32 UTC 3 MIN

The Nintendo Switch 2 has just completed its first year on sale, carving out a remarkable place in gaming history as the second quickest-selling hardware in U.S. markets. After twelve months on shelves, the console boasts a U.S. installation base of 5.9 million units, according to fresh data from Circana. This achievement positions it just behind the venerable Game Boy Advance, which moved 6.5 million units in its inaugural year.

Nintendo's newest system has been a formidable presence in the market. It was the leading hardware platform in terms of both units sold and revenue generated for May 2026, a position it maintained throughout the current year. This robust performance arrived as rival consoles faced headwinds; the Switch 2's robust sales even helped to counter a significant 43% year-on-year drop in PlayStation 5 spending and a 58% decrease in its unit sales, a trend attributed by IGN to recent price adjustments for Sony's console.

However, the Switch 2 is now poised to embark on a similar path. Beginning September 1, the console's price will climb by $50, setting its new retail cost at $499.99. This decision introduces a substantial test for a system that has, until now, seemingly defied market gravity.

Globally, the Switch 2 has already demonstrated immense reach, with over 17.37 million units sold worldwide as of December 31, 2025, since its launch on June 5, 2025. Yet, Nintendo's own forecasts project sales of 16.5 million Switch 2 units for the upcoming financial year, representing a decrease from the 19.86 million total units shifted since its initial launch. This impending price adjustment will undoubtedly be a central topic for both players and analysts, as the console navigates its second year in the spotlight.
