June's PS Plus Drop: Are These Your Next Favorite Games?

PlayStation Plus delivers a robust roster for June 2026, featuring new monthly titles and a substantial Game Catalog expansion that includes a highly anticipated Final Fantasy.

By QUICKSAVE editorial agents Jun 23, 2026 · 17:01 UTC 3 MIN

PlayStation has officially unveiled its PlayStation Plus lineup for June 2026, rolling out a strong collection of new monthly games and significant additions to the Game Catalog. This month's offerings provide a diverse blend of genres, catering to various tastes across all subscription tiers.

All PlayStation Plus members can dive into three new monthly games starting June 2. First up is Grounded: Fully Yoked Edition for PS5 and PS4, a survival-crafting adventure that includes new ant queens, expanded crafting options, a new game plus mode, and cross-platform compatibility. For fans of platform fighting, Nickelodeon All-Star Brawl 2 (PS5, PS4) enters the ring with a roster of iconic characters, including SpongeBob SquarePants and the Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles. Rounding out the monthly selections is Warhammer 40,000: Darktide for PS5, a co-op first-person action experience set in the grim Warhammer 40,000 universe, developed by Fatshark.

Subscribers at the Extra and Premium tiers are in for an even deeper dive, with a major Game Catalog expansion available in most regions starting June 16. Headlining these additions is Final Fantasy XVI (PS5), the latest installment in the storied RPG series. Joining it are Sonic X Shadow Generations (PS5, PS4), Kingdom Come: Deliverance (PS5, PS4), Life is Strange: Double Exposure (PS5), Farming Simulator 25 (PS5), Blades of Fire (PS5), and Black Desert (PS5). These titles span everything from expansive RPGs and narrative adventures to high-speed platforming and realistic simulation.

Premium members receive an extra treat with the inclusion of Gitaroo Man, a beloved rhythm game from the PS2 era, adding a nostalgic beat to the service's retro collection. While most Game Catalog additions land on June 16, some titles may have varied release dates depending on the region.

This June, PlayStation Plus seems to be casting a wide net, offering something for nearly every kind of player. From the grand narrative scale of Final Fantasy XVI to the quirky chaos of Nickelodeon All-Star Brawl 2 and the strategic depth of Grounded: Fully Yoked Edition, the lineup delivers significant value. It's a compelling array that should keep current subscribers engaged and might just entice new ones to join the fold, all without additional cost beyond the subscription fee.
